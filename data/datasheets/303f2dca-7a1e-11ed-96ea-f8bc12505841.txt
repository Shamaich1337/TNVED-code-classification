### Component Summary: MF-SM250-2 by Bourns

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Rating (Vmax)**: 15 Volts
- **Current Ratings**:
  - **Maximum Current (Imax)**: 100 Amps
  - **Hold Current (Ihold)**: 2.50 Amps
  - **Trip Current (Itrip)**: 5.00 Amps
- **Resistance**:
  - **Minimum Resistance (Rmin)**: 0.024 Ohms
  - **Maximum Resistance (R1)**: 0.085 Ohms
- **Maximum Time to Trip**: 0.085 seconds at 23 °C
- **Power Dissipation**: 25.0 Watts at 23 °C
- **Operating Temperature Range**: -40 °C to +85 °C
- **Package Type**: Surface Mount Device (SMD)
- **Moisture Sensitive Level**: Not specified in the provided data, but compliant with AEC-Q200 for automotive applications.
- **Special Features**:
  - RoHS compliant and halogen-free
  - AEC-Q200 Rev-C compliant for automotive applications
  - Packaged per EIA 481-2 standard

#### Description:
The MF-SM250 is a Polymer Positive Temperature Coefficient (PTC) resettable fuse designed to protect electronic circuits from overcurrent conditions. When the current exceeds the hold current rating, the device heats up and increases its resistance, effectively limiting the current flow and protecting downstream components. Once the fault condition is removed and the device cools down, it resets automatically, allowing normal operation to resume without the need for replacement.

#### Typical Applications:
The MF-SM250 is suitable for a variety of applications, particularly in environments where low voltage power supplies are used. Typical applications include:
- **Computers and Peripherals**: Protecting sensitive components from overcurrent conditions.
- **General Electronics**: Used in consumer electronics to prevent damage from faults.
- **Automotive Applications**: Ensuring safety and reliability in automotive electronic systems, complying with automotive industry standards.

This component is ideal for use in any circuit where overcurrent protection is necessary, providing a reliable and resettable solution to safeguard electronic devices.
