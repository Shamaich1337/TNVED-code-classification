### Component Summary: SGA-5386Z

**Manufacturer:** Qorvo, Inc.  
**Product Type:** SiGe HBT MMIC Amplifier  
**Product Code:** SGA-5386Z

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Device Operating Voltage (VD): 3.1 V to 4.1 V (Typical: 3.6 V)
  - Maximum Device Voltage: 5 V

- **Current Ratings:**
  - Device Operating Current (ID): 54 mA to 66 mA (Typical: 60 mA)
  - Maximum Device Current: 120 mA

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Power consumption is determined by the operating voltage and current, with typical conditions at 3.6 V and 60 mA.

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - -40 °C to +85 °C

- **Package Type:**
  - SOT-86

- **Special Features:**
  - High gain of 14.9 dB at 1950 MHz
  - Cascadable 50 Ω input/output
  - Low thermal resistance (Junction - Lead: 97 °C/W)
  - Requires only two DC-blocking capacitors, a bias resistor, and an optional RF choke for operation.
  - Cancellation of emitter junction non-linearities for improved intermodulation product suppression.

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - Not specified in the provided text.

#### Description:
The SGA-5386Z is a high-performance SiGe HBT (Silicon-Germanium Heterojunction Bipolar Transistor) MMIC (Monolithic Microwave Integrated Circuit) amplifier designed for RF applications. It utilizes a Darlington configuration with one-micron emitters, which enhances thermal performance and increases breakdown voltage while minimizing leakage current. The device is optimized for high-frequency operation, covering a frequency range from DC to 5000 MHz.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Power Amplifier Driver:** Used in various communication systems to amplify signals before transmission.
- **Cellular Communication:** Suitable for applications in GSM, UMTS, and PCS systems.
- **IF Amplifier:** Used in intermediate frequency stages of receivers.
- **Wireless Data Transmission:** Ideal for applications in wireless data communication and satellite systems.

The SGA-5386Z is particularly useful in environments where high gain and low noise are critical, making it suitable for modern wireless communication systems.
