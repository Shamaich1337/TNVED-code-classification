### Component Summary: SMAJ28CA Transient Voltage Suppressor Diode

**Key Specifications:**
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Working Peak Reverse Voltage (VRWM): 28V
  - Breakdown Voltage (VBR): 31.1V (minimum), 34.4V (maximum)
  
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Maximum Reverse Leakage Current (IR) at VRWM: 1.0 µA
  - Maximum Peak Pulse Current (Ipp) for 10/1000 µs: 59.0A
  - Maximum Clamping Voltage at Ipp (10/1000 µs): 45.4V
  - Maximum Clamping Voltage at Ipp (8/20 µs): 8.8V

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Minimum Peak Pulse Power Dissipation (TP = 1 ms): 400 Watts
  - Steady State Power Dissipation at TL = 75 °C: 1.0 Watts

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Junction Temperature (TJ): -55 °C to +150 °C
  - Storage Temperature (TSTG): -55 °C to +150 °C

- **Package Type:**
  - Surface Mount SMA (DO-214AC)

- **Special Features:**
  - RoHS compliant
  - Fast response time: < 1.0 picoseconds for unidirectional devices and < 5.0 picoseconds for bidirectional devices.
  - UL Recognized (UL File Number: E153537)

- **Moisture Sensitive Level:**
  - JEDEC J-STD-020E: Level 1

**Description:**
The SMAJ28CA is a Transient Voltage Suppressor (TVS) diode designed to protect sensitive electronic components from voltage spikes caused by electrostatic discharge (ESD), electrical fast transients (EFT), and surge events. It is a bidirectional device, meaning it can clamp voltage spikes in both directions, making it suitable for AC applications as well as DC.

**Typical Applications:**
- **Surge Protection:** Used in power management systems to protect against voltage transients.
- **ESD Protection:** Commonly found in portable communications, computing, and video equipment to safeguard sensitive circuits from ESD events.
- **Signal Integrity:** Helps maintain signal integrity in data lines by clamping voltage spikes that could disrupt communication.
- **Consumer Electronics:** Integrated into devices such as smartphones, tablets, and laptops to enhance reliability and longevity.

This component is particularly valuable in environments where electronic devices are exposed to unpredictable voltage spikes, ensuring that they operate safely and effectively.
