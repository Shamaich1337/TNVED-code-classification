### Component Summary: XCR3064XL-7VQG44I

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings**: 
  - Supply Voltage (VCC): 2.7V to 3.6V
- **Current Ratings**: 
  - Typical Standby Current: 17 μA at 25 °C
  - Dynamic Current: 
    - 1 MHz: 0.75 mA
    - 50 MHz: 15 mA
- **Power Consumption**: 
  - Typical ICC at various frequencies (up to 36.27 mA at 180 MHz)
- **Operating Temperature Range**: 
  - Industrial: -40 °C to +85 °C
- **Package Type**: 
  - 44-pin Very Thin Quad Flat Pack (VQFP)
- **Special Features**: 
  - Low power operation
  - 5.5 ns pin-to-pin logic delays
  - System frequencies up to 192 MHz
  - In-system programming (ISP)
  - Full IEEE Standard 1149.1 boundary-scan (JTAG)
  - Programmable slew rate control per macrocell
  - Security bit to prevent unauthorized access
- **Moisture Sensitive Level**: 
  - Level I (according to JEDEC J-STD-020E)

#### Description:
The **XCR3064XL-7VQG44I** is a **CoolRunner™ XPLA3** 64-macrocell Complex Programmable Logic Device (CPLD) designed for low-power applications. It features a 3.3V core supply and is optimized for power-sensitive designs, providing a total of 1,500 usable gates with fast pin-to-pin propagation delays of 5.5 ns. The device supports in-system programming and offers a predictable timing model, making it suitable for various digital logic applications.

#### Typical Applications:
The XCR3064XL-7VQG44I is typically used in:
- **Power Management**: Efficiently managing power in portable and battery-operated devices.
- **Signal Processing**: Implementing custom logic for signal manipulation in communication systems.
- **Data Storage**: Used in applications requiring programmable logic for data handling and storage solutions.
- **Control Systems**: Ideal for embedded control applications where low power consumption is critical.
- **Consumer Electronics**: Found in devices that require programmable logic for various functionalities.

This component is particularly advantageous in environments where space and power efficiency are paramount, making it a popular choice in modern electronic designs.
