### Component Summary: CDSOD323-T12SC

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Minimum Breakdown Voltage @ 1 mA (VBR): 13.3 V
  - Working Peak Voltage (VM): 12.0 V
  - Maximum Clamping Voltage @ IP = 1 A (VF): 19.0 V

- **Current Ratings:**
  - Unidirectional Peak Pulse Power (t p = 8/20 µs): 500 W
  - Bidirectional Peak Pulse Power (t p = 8/20 µs): 400 W
  - Maximum Leakage Current @ VWM (ID): 1 µA

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Not explicitly stated, but power handling is indicated by peak pulse power ratings.

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Operating Temperature (TL): -55 to +150 °C
  - Storage Temperature (TSTG): -55 to +150 °C

- **Package Type:**
  - SOD-323 (Surface Mount)

- **Special Features or Notes:**
  - Lead-free and RoHS compliant.
  - ESD protection up to 30 kV.
  - Surge protection capability greater than 24 A.
  - Protects one line in uni/bidirectional configuration.
  - Meets IEC 61000-4-2 (ESD), IEC 61000-4-4 (EFT), and IEC 61000-4-5 (Surge) requirements.

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (JEDEC J-STD-020E):**
  - Level 1

#### Description:
The **CDSOD323-T12SC** is a **Transient Voltage Suppressor (TVS) diode** designed to protect sensitive electronic components from voltage spikes caused by electrostatic discharge (ESD) and surge events. It is available in a compact SOD-323 package, making it suitable for space-constrained applications. The device can operate in both unidirectional and bidirectional configurations, providing flexibility in circuit design.

#### Typical Applications:
The CDSOD323-T12SC is commonly used in:
- **Portable Electronics:** Such as computer notebooks, cellular phones, and personal digital assistants (PDAs).
- **Consumer Electronics:** Including digital cameras and other devices that require protection from voltage transients.
- **Communication Equipment:** To safeguard against ESD and surge events that can damage sensitive components.

This component is essential for ensuring the reliability and longevity of electronic devices by providing robust protection against transient voltage events.
