### Component Summary: VS-10BQ100-M3/5BT

**Manufacturer:** Vishay Semiconductors  
**Product Code:** VS-10BQ100-M3

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Maximum DC Reverse Voltage (V_R): 100 V
  - Maximum Working Peak Reverse Voltage (V_RWM): 100 V

- **Current Ratings:**
  - Maximum Average Forward Current (I_F(AV)): 1 A
  - Maximum Peak One Cycle Non-Repetitive Surge Current (I_FSM): 
    - 780 A (5 μs sine)
    - 38 A (10 ms sine)

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Forward Voltage Drop (V_F at I_F): 
    - 0.75 V at 1 A (T_J = 25 °C)
    - 0.59 V at 2 A (T_J = 125 °C)

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Junction Temperature (T_J): -55 °C to +175 °C

- **Package Type:**
  - SMB (DO-214AA)

- **Special Features:**
  - Low forward voltage drop
  - Guard ring for enhanced ruggedness and long-term reliability
  - High frequency operation
  - Moisture Sensitive Level: MSL level 1, per JEDEC J-STD-020, LF maximum peak of 260 °C

#### Description:
The VS-10BQ100-M3 is a high-performance Schottky rectifier designed for applications that require low forward voltage drop and a compact footprint on printed circuit boards (PCBs). Schottky rectifiers are semiconductor devices that allow current to flow in one direction while blocking it in the opposite direction, making them essential in power conversion applications.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Power Management:** Used in switching power supplies to improve efficiency by reducing power loss.
- **Disk Drives:** Acts as a rectifier in power supply circuits for hard drives and SSDs.
- **Converters:** Utilized in DC-DC converters for efficient power conversion.
- **Freewheeling Diodes:** Provides a path for inductive kickback in relay and motor applications.
- **Battery Charging:** Employed in battery management systems to prevent reverse current flow.
- **Reverse Battery Protection:** Protects circuits from damage due to incorrect battery connections.

This component is ideal for applications where space is limited and efficiency is critical, making it a popular choice in modern electronic designs.
