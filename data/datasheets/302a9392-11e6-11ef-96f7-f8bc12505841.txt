A printedcircuit board with a good, low-inductance ground planeis mandatory. All decoupling capacitors (the small100nF ceramic type is a good choice) should be mount-ed as close as possible to the power-supply pins.Separate decoupling capacitors for analog V\nCCand for\ndigital V DDare also recommended. Close attention\nshould be paid to the bandwidth of the decoupling andterminating components. Short lead lengths on theinputs and outputs are essential to avoid unwanted par-asitic feedback around the comparators. Solder thedevice directly to the printed circuit board instead ofusing a socket.\nInput Slew-Rate Requirements\nAs with all high-speed comparators, the high gain-band-width product of the MAX900–MAX903 can create oscil-lation problems when the input traverses the linearregion. For clean output switching without oscillation orsteps in the output waveform, the input must meet mini-mum slew-rate requirements. Oscillation is largely afunction of board layout and of coupled source imped-ance and stray input capacitance. Both poor layout andlarge-source impedance will cause the part to oscillateand increase the minimum slew-rate requirement. Insome applications, it may be helpful to apply some posi-tive feedback between the output and + input. Thispushes the output through the transition region cleanly,\nbut applies a hysteresis in threshold seen at the inputterminals.\nTTL Output and Latch Inputs\nThe comparator TTL-output stages are optimized for dri-ving low-power Schottky TTL with a fan-out of four.\nWhen the latch is connected to a logic high level, the\ncomparator is transparent and immediately responds tochanges at the input terminals. When the latch is con-nected to a TTL low level, the comparator output latchesin the same state as at the instant that the latch commandis applied, and will not respond to subsequent changesat the input.
