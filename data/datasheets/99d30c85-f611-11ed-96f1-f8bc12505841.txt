### Component Summary: MC-306 32.7680K-A0:ROHS

**Component Type:** Crystal Unit

**Description:**
The MC-306 is a crystal oscillator designed to provide a stable frequency reference, specifically at a nominal frequency of 32.768 kHz. It is commonly used in timing applications, particularly in clocks and microcontrollers, due to its precise frequency stability and low power consumption.

### Key Specifications:
- **Nominal Frequency:** 32.768 kHz
- **Storage Temperature Range:** -55 °C to +125 °C
- **Operating Temperature Range:** -40 °C to +85 °C
- **Level of Drive (DL):** 1.0 µW Max
- **Frequency Tolerance:** ±20 × 10^-6 (±50 × 10^-6, ±100 × 10^-6 at +25 °C, DL=0.1 µW)
- **Turnover Temperature (Ti):** +25 °C ±5 °C
- **Parabolic Coefficient (B):** -0.04 × 10^-6 / °C² Max
- **Load Capacitance (CL):** 6 pF to ∞ (standard: 12.5 pF)
- **Motional Resistance (ESR):** 50 kΩ Max
- **Motional Capacitance (C1):** 1.8 fF Typ (4.0 fF ~ 0.6 fF)
- **Shunt Capacitance (C0):** 0.9 pF Typ (2.0 pF ~ 0.6 pF)
- **Frequency Aging:** ±3 × 10^-6 / year Max (±5 × 10^-6 / year Max in the first year at +25 °C)
- **Package Type:** Metal case with dimensions of 8.0 × 3.8 × 2.54 mm
- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):** Not specified in the provided text.

### Special Features:
- The metal case may be exposed on the top or bottom, which is purely cosmetic and does not affect performance.
- Designed for automotive applications, including car multimedia, body electronics, and remote keyless entry systems.

### Typical Applications:
- **Clock Generation:** Used in digital clocks and watches for accurate timekeeping.
- **Microcontroller Timing:** Provides a stable clock signal for microcontrollers in various electronic devices.
- **Automotive Electronics:** Utilized in automotive applications for engine control units, airbag systems, and other critical timing functions.

This crystal unit is essential for applications requiring precise timing and frequency stability, making it a vital component in both consumer electronics and automotive systems.
