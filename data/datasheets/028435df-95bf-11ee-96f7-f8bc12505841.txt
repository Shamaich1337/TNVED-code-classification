### Component Summary: ETP01-1621RL

**Manufacturer:** STMicroelectronics  
**Product Code:** ETP01-1621RL  
**Document ID:** Doc ID 14515 Rev 4  
**Date:** May 2011  

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Operating Junction Temperature Range: -40°C to 150°C
  - Storage Temperature Range: -55°C to 150°C
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Peak Pulse Current (Ipp):
    - 2/10 µs: 150 A
    - 5/310 µs: 40 A
    - 8/20 µs: 100 A
  - Non-repetitive Surge Peak On-State Current (ITSM): 8 A (20 ms)
- **Power Consumption:** Not explicitly stated; primarily a surge protection device.
- **Operating Temperature Range:** -40°C to 150°C
- **Package Type:** SO-8 (JEDEC registered)
- **Special Features:**
  - Low capacitance: 13 pF max at 0 V
  - Trisil™ technology for fail-safe mode in short circuit conditions
  - UL94 V0 approved resin
- **Moisture Sensitive Level:** Not specified in the provided text.

#### Description:
The ETP01-1621RL is a low capacitance transient surge arrestor designed to protect high-speed communication networks, particularly in Ethernet applications. It utilizes planar technology to combine high surge capability with low capacitance, ensuring minimal distortion of high-speed signals. This component is particularly effective in meeting stringent telecommunications surge standards.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Telecommunications:** Protection against surge events in telecommunications equipment, specifically for 10/100 Mbps Ethernet and T1/E1 line cards.
- **Networking Equipment:** Used in devices that require protection from electrical surges to maintain signal integrity and reliability.
- **Compliance with Standards:** Helps equipment meet various standards such as UL61950, IEC 950 / CSA C22.2, and UL1459, as well as surge protection standards like Telcordia GR-1089 and ITU-T K20/21.

This component is essential for ensuring the longevity and reliability of electronic devices in environments where electrical surges are a concern, making it a critical component in modern communication systems.
