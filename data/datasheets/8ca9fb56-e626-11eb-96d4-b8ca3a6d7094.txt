### Component Summary: PX1172R by SkyTraq Technology

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings**: 
  - Operating Voltage: 3.3V DC ±10%
  - Backup Voltage: 1.3V to 3.6V
- **Current Ratings**: 
  - Typical Current Consumption: 100mA
- **Power Consumption**: 
  - 100mA during operation (excluding active antenna current)
- **Operating Temperature Range**: 
  - -40°C to +85°C
- **Package Type**: 
  - Dimensions: 17mm x 22mm x 2.9mm
- **Special Features**: 
  - Centimeter-level accuracy using RTK (Real-Time Kinematic) technique
  - Multi-Band, Quad-GNSS support (GPS, GLONASS, Galileo, BeiDou)
  - Fast signal acquisition and high sensitivity
  - Supports NMEA-0183 and RTCM 3.x protocols
  - RoHS compliant
- **Moisture Sensitive Level**: 
  - MSL4 (must be used for SMT reflow mounting within 72 hours after being taken out from the vacuumed ESD-protective moisture barrier bag)

#### Description:
The **PX1172R** is a compact GNSS RTK receiver module designed for high-precision positioning applications. It utilizes a 230-channel Phoenix GNSS engine capable of processing signals from multiple satellite systems, achieving centimeter-level accuracy through carrier phase RTK techniques. The module is optimized for low power consumption and is suitable for integration into various mobile applications due to its small form factor.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Machine Control & Automation**: Used in industrial automation systems for precise positioning and control.
- **Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs)**: Provides accurate navigation and positioning for drones and other UAVs.
- **Precision Agriculture**: Enables farmers to utilize precise positioning for planting, harvesting, and field mapping.
- **GIS Data Collection**: Facilitates accurate geographic data collection for mapping and surveying.
- **Precision Heading & Attitude**: Used in applications requiring precise orientation and heading information.

The PX1172R is particularly beneficial in environments where high accuracy is critical, such as construction, agriculture, and autonomous vehicle navigation. Its ability to quickly acquire signals and maintain accuracy under various conditions makes it a versatile choice for developers and engineers in the GNSS field.
