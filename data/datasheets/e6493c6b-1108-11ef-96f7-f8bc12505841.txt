  - Continuous Output Current: ±25 mA
  - Input Clamp Current: ±20 mA

- **Operating Temperature Range**: 
  - SN74HCT14: -40°C to 85°C
  - SN54HCT14: -55°C to 125°C
  
- **Package Type**: 
  - SOIC (D) package with 14 pins
  
- **Special Features**: 
  - Schmitt-Trigger inputs for noise immunity
  - TTL-compatible inputs
  - Can drive up to 10 LSTTL loads
  
- **Moisture Sensitive Level**: 
  - MSL Level 1 (JEDEC J-STD-020E)

#### Description:
The SN74HCT14 is a hex Schmitt-Trigger inverter from Texas Instruments. It contains six independent inverters that perform the Boolean function Y = A in positive logic. The Schmitt-Trigger inputs provide a minimum separation between positive and negative switching thresholds, which helps to eliminate issues caused by slow or noisy input signals.

#### Typical Applications:
The SN74HCT14 is commonly used in various applications, including:
- **Uninterruptible Power Supplies (UPS)**: For signal conditioning and logic level shifting.
- **Computer Peripherals**: As a buffer or inverter in data lines.
- **Printers**: For controlling logic signals.
- **AC Servo Drives**: In motor control applications.
- **Desktop Computers**: For logic level interfacing and signal processing.

This component is ideal for applications requiring reliable signal processing with low power consumption and noise immunity, making it suitable for both consumer electronics and industrial applications.
