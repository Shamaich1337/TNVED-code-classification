### Component Summary: GVA-123+

**Description:**
The GVA-123+ is a monolithic ultra-wideband amplifier designed using GaAs HBT technology. It is characterized by its excellent gain flatness and broad frequency range, making it suitable for various high-frequency applications. The amplifier is RoHS compliant and is packaged in a SOT-89 style for effective thermal performance.

**Key Specifications:**
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Supply Operating Voltage (Vcc): 4.8 V to 5.2 V (typical 5.0 V)
  - DC Voltage on Pin 3: Maximum 6 V

- **Current Ratings:**
  - Device Operating Current: 52 mA to 57 mA (typical 57 mA at 5 V)
  - Maximum Operating Current: 100 mA

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Power Dissipation: 0.34 W

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Operating Temperature: -40°C to 85°C
  - Storage Temperature: -65°C to 150°C

- **Package Type:**
  - Case Style: DF782 (SOT-89)

- **Special Features:**
  - Ultra-wideband performance (0.01 to 12 GHz)
  - Excellent gain flatness of ±0.7 dB from 0.05 to 8 GHz
  - No external matching components required
  - Low additive phase noise, typically -169 dBc/Hz at 10 KHz offset
  - Good input and output return loss (12-28 dB over 0.05 - 6 GHz)
  - ESD Ratings: HBM Class 1C (1000 to <2000V), MM Class M2 (100 to <200V)

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL1 in accordance with IPC/JEDEC J-STD-020D

**Typical Applications:**
The GVA-123+ is utilized in various applications, including:
- Base station infrastructure for cellular and wireless communications
- Test instruments for RF measurements
- MMDS (Multichannel Multipoint Distribution Service) and Wireless LAN systems
- LTE (Long-Term Evolution) networks
- Satellite communication systems
- Avionics applications
- Low phase noise synthesizer applications

This amplifier is particularly advantageous in scenarios where a broad frequency response and minimal signal distortion are critical, making it a versatile choice for modern communication systems.
