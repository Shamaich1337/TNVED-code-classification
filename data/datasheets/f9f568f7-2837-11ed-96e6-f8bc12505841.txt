### Component Summary: ADM811MARTZ-REEL7

**Description:**
The ADM811MARTZ-REEL7 is a microprocessor supervisory circuit designed by Analog Devices. It is housed in a 4-lead SOT-143 package and is primarily used for monitoring supply voltages in microprocessor systems. The ADM811 series provides a reliable voltage monitoring solution, ensuring that the microprocessor operates correctly by generating reset signals when the supply voltage falls below a specified threshold.

**Key Specifications:**
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Supply Voltage (VCC): 1.0 V to 5.5 V (operating range)
  - Reset Threshold Voltage: 4.25 V to 4.50 V (specific to ADM811M variant)
  
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Supply Current: 8 µA (typical) at VCC < 5.5 V; 5 µA (typical) at VCC < 3.6 V

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Low power consumption of 5 µA typical, making it suitable for battery-operated devices.

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - -40°C to +85°C

- **Package Type:**
  - 4-Lead SOT-143 (RA-4)

- **Special Features:**
  - Precision voltage monitor with options for various reset thresholds (2.5 V, 3 V, 3.3 V, 5 V).
  - Built-in manual reset functionality.
  - Reset assertion down to 1 V.
  - Glitch immunity to prevent false triggering from transient voltage spikes.

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL Level 1 per JEDEC J-STD-020E.
