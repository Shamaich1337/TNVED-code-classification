### Component Summary: NDNC01055

**Description:**
The NDNC01055 is a high-performance power amplifier MMIC (Monolithic Microwave Integrated Circuit) designed for operation in the frequency range of 14 to 18 GHz. It utilizes 0.25 µm GaN (Gallium Nitride) HEMT (High Electron Mobility Transistor) technology, which allows for efficient power amplification in high-frequency applications. The chip is grounded through a via hole on the back side and is 100% tested for reliability.

**Key Specifications:**
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Drain Voltage (Vd): 28V (Absolute Maximum: 32V)
  - Gate Voltage (Vg): -2.7V (Absolute Maximum: -10V)
  
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Drain Current (Id): 4A
  - Gate Current (Ig): 40mA
  
- **Power Consumption:**
  - DC Power Consumption (Pd): 128W
  
- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Channel Temperature (Tch): Up to 175°C
  - Storage Temperature (Tstg): -55°C to 150°C
  - Mounting Temperature (Tm): 310°C (1 min, N2 protection)
  
- **Package Type:**
  - Dimensions: 3.3 mm x 4.3 mm x 0.1 mm
  
- **Special Features:**
  - Typical Power Gain: 23 dB
  - Typical Output Power: 47 dBm at Vd = 28V
  - Typical Power Added Efficiency (PAE): 34%
  - Input Power (Pin): 30 dBm
  - Moisture Sensitive Level: Not specified in the provided text.

**Typical Applications:**
The NDNC01055 is primarily used in applications requiring high-frequency power amplification, such as:
- **Telecommunications:** For signal amplification in communication systems operating in the microwave frequency range.
- **Radar Systems:** Enhancing signal strength for radar applications.
- **Satellite Communications:** Amplifying signals for satellite transmission and reception.
- **Test Equipment:** Used in various RF test setups where high power and frequency are required.

This component is ideal for systems that demand high efficiency and performance in the 14-18 GHz range, making it suitable for advanced communication and radar technologies.
