#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings**: 
  - Output Voltage: 3.3V
  - Input Voltage: Up to 15V
- **Current Ratings**: 
  - Maximum Output Current: 800mA
- **Power Consumption**: 
  - Quiescent Current: 4 to 10 mA (typical)
- **Operating Temperature Range**: 
  - Junction Temperature: -40°C to +125°C
  - Storage Temperature: -65°C to +150°C
- **Package Type**: 
  - SOT-223 (DCY)
- **Special Features**: 
  - Low dropout voltage: 1.2V max at 800mA
  - Internal current limit and thermal overload protection
  - Laser-trimmed output voltage for accuracy
- **Moisture Sensitive Level**: 
  - MSL Level 1 (JEDEC J-STD-020E)

#### Description:
The REG1117-3.3/2K5 is a low dropout (LDO) voltage regulator from Texas Instruments. It is a three-terminal device designed to provide a stable output voltage of 3.3V with a maximum output current of 800mA. The device features a low dropout voltage, allowing it to operate efficiently with minimal input-output voltage differential, making it suitable for battery-powered applications.

#### Typical Applications:
- **SCSI-2 Active Termination**: Used in SCSI applications to provide stable voltage levels.
- **Hand-held Data Collection Devices**: Ideal for portable devices requiring efficient power management.
- **Battery-Powered Instrumentation**: Suitable for devices that rely on battery power, ensuring longevity and efficiency.
- **Battery Management Circuits**: Used in notebook and palmtop PCs for managing battery voltage levels.
- **Core Voltage Supply**: Provides power to FPGAs, PLDs, DSPs, and CPUs, ensuring stable operation.

This component is particularly useful in applications where space is limited and efficiency is critical, making it a popular choice in modern electronic designs.
