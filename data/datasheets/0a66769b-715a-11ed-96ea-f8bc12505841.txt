### Component Summary: ILD8150EXUMA1 (Infineon Technologies)

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings**: 
  - Input Voltage (VIN): 8 V to 80 V
  - Maximum Voltage at SW Pin: 90 V
- **Current Ratings**: 
  - Output Current: Up to 1.5 A DC
- **Power Consumption**: 
  - Operating current in dim-to-off: 2.3 mA
  - Shutdown current: 100 µA (typical)
- **Operating Temperature Range**: 
  - Junction Temperature (TJ): -40 °C to 150 °C
- **Package Type**: 
  - PG-DSO-8 (standard)
  - PG-DSO-8 with exposed pad (ILD8150E variant)
- **Special Features**: 
  - Integrated 80 V high-side MOSFET switch with low RON (290 mΩ typical)
  - Hybrid dimming capability down to 0.5% of target current
  - Cycle-by-cycle current limitation
  - Under-voltage lockout and thermal protection
  - Flicker-free operation with a modulation frequency of 3.4 kHz
  - Digital soft-start feature
  - Pull-down transistor to prevent LED glowing
- **Moisture Sensitive Level**: 
  - Not specified in the provided data, but typically assessed according to JEDEC J-STD-020E.

#### Description:
The **ILD8150** is a high-performance LED driver integrated circuit (IC) designed for driving high-power LEDs in various lighting applications. It operates as a DC/DC buck converter, utilizing hysteretic current regulation to maintain stable output current. The device features an integrated high-side MOSFET switch, which enhances efficiency and reduces the need for external components.

#### Typical Applications:
The ILD8150 is primarily used in:
- **Electronic Control Gear (ECG)** for LED luminaires, providing efficient power management for LED lighting systems.
- General lighting applications where precise control of LED brightness is required, such as in tunable white lighting and flat panel designs.
- Applications requiring hybrid dimming solutions, combining analog and PWM dimming for enhanced control over light output and quality.
- Professional LED lighting solutions that demand high output current accuracy and thermal management.

This IC is particularly suitable for applications operating close to SELV (Safety Extra Low Voltage) limits, providing a high safety voltage margin and ensuring reliable operation under varying conditions.
