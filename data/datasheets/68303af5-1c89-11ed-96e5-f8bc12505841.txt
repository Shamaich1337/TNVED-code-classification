The extr emely small temperature drift  \nof 2 nV/°C ensures a minimum offset voltage error over their \nentire temperature range of −4 0°C to +125°C, making these \namplifiers ideal for a variety of sensitive measurement applica-tions in harsh operating environments.  \nThe AD8628/ AD8629/ AD8630 achieve a high degree of precision \nthrough a patented combination of auto-zeroing and chopping. \nThis unique topology allows the AD8628/ AD8629/ AD8630 to \nmaintain their low offset voltag e over a wide temperature range \nand over their operating lifetime. The AD8628/ AD8629/ AD8630  \nalso optimize the noise and bandwidth over previous generations \nof auto-zero amplifiers, offering the lowest voltage noise of any auto-zero amplifier by more than 50%. \nPrevious designs used either au to-zeroing or chopping to add \nprecision to the specifications of an amplifier. Auto-zeroing \nresults in low noise energy at th e auto-zeroing frequency, at the \nexpense of higher low frequency no ise due to aliasing of wideband \nnoise into the auto-zeroed frequency band. Chopping results in \nlower low frequency noise at the expense of larger noise energy at the chopping frequency. The AD8628/ AD8629/ AD8630 \nfamily uses both auto-zeroing and chopping in a patented ping-pong arrangement to obtain lowe r low frequency noise together \nwith lower energy at the chopping and auto-zeroing frequencies, maximizing the signal-to-nois e ratio for the majority of \napplications without the need for additional filtering. The \nrelatively high clock frequency of 15 kHz simplifies filter requirements for a wide, useful noise-free bandwidth. \nThe AD8628 is among the few auto-zero amplifiers offered in \nthe 5-lead TSOT package. L | Page 17 of 24 INPUT OVERVOLTAGE PROTECTION \nAlthough the AD8628/ AD8629/ AD8630 are rail-to-rail input \namplifiers, care should be take n to ensure that the potential \ndifference between the inputs does not exceed the supply voltage. \nUnder normal negative feedback operating conditions, the \namplifier corrects its output to ensure that the two inputs are at \nthe same voltage. However, if either input exceeds either supply rail by more than 0.3 V, larg e currents begin to flow through \nthe ESD protection diodes in the amplifier.  \nThese diodes are connected betw een the inputs and each supply \nrail to protect the input transistors against an electrostatic \ndischarge event, and they are norm ally reverse-biased. However, if \nthe input voltage exceeds the supply voltage, these ESD diodes can \nbecome forward-biased. Without current limiting, excessive \namounts of current could flow through these diodes, causing permanent damage to the device. If inputs are subject to overvoltage, appropriate series resistors should be inserted to limit the diode current to less than 5 mA maximum. \nOUTPUT PHASE REVERSAL \nOutput phase reversal occurs in some amplifiers when the input common-mode voltage ra nge is exceeded. As common-\nmode voltage is moved outside the common-mode range, the outputs of these amplifiers can suddenly jump in the opposite \ndirection to the supply rail. This is the result of the differential input pair shutting down, causing a radical shifting of internal voltages that results in the erratic output behavior.  \nThe AD8628 /AD8629/ AD8630 amplifiers have been carefully \ndesigned to prevent any output phase reversal, provided that \nboth inputs are maintained within the supply voltages. If one or \nboth inputs could exceed either su pply voltage, a resistor should be \nplaced in series with the input to limit the current to less than 5 mA. This ensures that the output  does not reverse its phase. \nOVERLOAD RECOVERY TIME \nMany auto-zero amplifiers are plag ued by a long overload recovery \ntime, often in ms, due to the complicated settling behavior of the internal nulling loops after sa turation of the outputs. The \nAD8628/ AD8629 /AD8630 have been designed so that internal \nsettling occurs within two clock cycles after output saturation occurs. This results in a much shorter recovery time, less  than 10 μs, when compared to other auto-zero amplifiers. The wide bandwidth of the AD8628/ AD8629 /AD8630 enhances \nperformance when the parts are used to drive loads that inject transients into the outputs. ### Component Summary: AD8628ARTZ-REEL7

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings**: 
  - Supply Voltage: 2.7 V to 5.5 V
  - Absolute Maximum Supply Voltage: 6 V
- **Current Ratings**: 
  - Output Current: ±30 mA (typical), ±15 mA (−40°C to +125°C)
  - Short-Circuit Current: ±25 mA (typical), ±40 mA (−40°C to +125°C)
- **Power Consumption**: 
  - Supply Current per Amplifier: 0.85 mA to 1.1 mA (typical), 1.0 mA to 1.2 mA (−40°C to +125°C)
- **Operating Temperature Range**: 
  - −40°C to +125°C
- **Package Type**: 
  - 5-Lead SOT-23 (RJ-5)
- **Special Features**: 
  - Zero-drift architecture with low offset voltage (1 μV max)
  - Rail-to-rail input and output swing
  - Low input bias current (100 pA max)
  - Qualified for automotive applications
- **Moisture Sensitive Level**: 
  - MSL Level 1 (JEDEC J-STD-020E)

#### Description:
The AD8628ARTZ-REEL7 is a zero-drift, single-supply, rail-to-rail input/output operational amplifier designed for high precision applications. It features ultra-low offset voltage and drift, making it suitable for sensitive measurement applications. The amplifier operates from a single supply voltage ranging from 2.7 V to 5.5 V and is capable of delivering high performance with minimal noise.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Automotive Sensors**: Used in pressure and position sensors, where precision is critical.
- **Medical Instrumentation**: Ideal for applications requiring high accuracy and low noise, such as thermocouple amplifiers and strain gauge amplifiers.
- **Precision Current Sensing**: Suitable for applications in battery management systems and power monitoring.
- **Photodiode Amplifiers**: Effective in applications requiring low noise and high gain.
- **Signal Conditioning**: Used in various signal processing applications where low drift and high precision are essential.

The AD8628ARTZ-REEL7 is particularly advantageous in environments where temperature variations can affect performance, ensuring reliable operation across a wide temperature range.
