With V CC = 3.3 V and driving an RS-232 load, the output \nvoltage swing is typically ±6 V . \nReceiver Section \nThe receivers are inverting level-shifters that accept RS-232 \ninput levels and translate them into 3 V logic output levels. RS-232 voltage levels are maintained at data rates up to 460 kbps even under worst-case loading conditions. ### Component Summary: ADM1385ARSZ-REEL7

**Key Characteristics and Specifications:**

- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Operating Voltage Range: 3.0 V to 5.5 V (typically 3.3 V)
  - Internally Generated Positive Supply (V+): +6 V nominal
  - Internally Generated Negative Supply (V-): -6 V nominal

- **Current Ratings:**
  - Power Supply Current: 1.3 mA (no load), 8 mA (with load)
  - Shutdown Supply Current: 0.01 μA to 0.5 μA

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Typical power consumption is low due to CMOS technology, allowing for extended battery life in portable applications.

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Industrial Version: -40°C to +85°C

- **Package Type:**
  - 20-lead Shrink Small Outline Package (SSOP)

- **Special Features or Notes:**
  - Low power shutdown mode
  - Driver disable mode
  - ESD protection to IEC 1000-4-2 standards (±8 kV contact discharge, ±15 kV air gap discharge)
  - Compatible with MAX3222/MAX3232 and LTC1385

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (JEDEC J-STD-020E):**
  - Not specified in the provided text, but typically SSOP packages are moisture sensitive.

**Description of the Component:**
The ADM1385 is a low-power, high-speed RS-232 line driver/receiver designed to operate from a single 3.3 V power supply. It is part of a family of transceivers that includes the ADM3202 and ADM3222, which are designed for high-speed data transmission up to 460 kbps while conforming to EIA-232E and CCITT V.28 specifications. The device utilizes a charge pump to generate the necessary RS-232 voltage levels from a lower voltage supply, making it suitable for battery-powered applications.

**Typical Applications:**
The ADM1385 is commonly used in:
- General-purpose RS-232 data links
- Portable instruments
- Printers, palmtop computers, and PDAs
- Any application requiring reliable serial communication with low power consumption

This component is particularly advantageous in scenarios where space and power efficiency are critical, such as in mobile devices and embedded systems.
