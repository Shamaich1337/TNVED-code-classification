### Component Summary: TPS767D325PWPR

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Input Voltage Range: -0.3V to +13.5V
  - Output Voltage: 2.5V (fixed) and 3.3V (fixed)
  
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Output Current Range: 0mA to 1A per regulator
  - Output Current Limit: 2A (internally limited)

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Quiescent Current: 85 µA (typical) at full load
  - Shutdown Quiescent Current: 1 µA

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Junction Temperature: -40°C to +125°C
  - Storage Temperature: -65°C to +150°C

- **Package Type:**
  - 28-Pin PowerPAD™ TSSOP (PWP)

- **Special Features:**
  - Low Dropout Voltage: Typically 350mV at 1A
  - Dual Output: 2.5V and 3.3V
  - Fast Transient Response
  - 2% Output Voltage Tolerance over load and temperature
  - Thermal Shutdown Protection
  - Dual Open-Drain Power-On Reset with 200ms delay
  - Enable (EN) pin for power-saving shutdown mode

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - Level 2, according to JEDEC J-STD-020E

#### Description:
The **TPS767D325PWPR** is a dual-output low-dropout (LDO) voltage regulator designed primarily for applications requiring multiple voltage levels, such as digital signal processors (DSPs). It features a PMOS pass element that allows for low dropout voltage and low quiescent current, making it suitable for battery-powered devices. The device provides two regulated output voltages: 2.5V and 3.3V, with the ability to handle output currents up to 1A per channel.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Power Management:** Ideal for powering DSPs and microcontrollers in portable and battery-operated devices.
- **Signal Processing:** Used in applications where stable voltage levels are critical for signal integrity.
- **Mixed-Voltage Systems:** Suitable for systems that require multiple voltage levels for different components.
- **Reset Circuits:** The integrated reset functionality can be utilized in microcontroller applications to ensure proper startup sequences.

This component is particularly advantageous in designs where space is limited, and efficiency is paramount, such as in mobile devices and embedded systems.
