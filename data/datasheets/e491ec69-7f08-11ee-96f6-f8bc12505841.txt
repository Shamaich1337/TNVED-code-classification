### Component Summary: NL27WZU04DFT2G

**Description:**
The NL27WZU04 is a high-performance dual unbuffered inverter designed for operation within a voltage range of 1.65V to 5.5V. It is part of the ON Semiconductor family of logic devices and is characterized by its low power consumption and high-speed performance. The device is particularly suitable for applications requiring logic level shifting and signal inversion.

**Key Specifications:**
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - **Operating Voltage (VCC):** 1.65V to 5.5V
  - **Maximum DC Supply Voltage:** 
    - SC−88 (NLV): -0.5V to +7.0V
    - SC−74, UDFN6: -0.5V to +6.5V
- **Current Ratings:**
  - **Output Source/Sink Current (IOUT):** ±50 mA
  - **Input Diode Current (IIK):** -50 mA
  - **Output Diode Current (IOK):** ±50 mA
  - **Supply Current (ICC or IGND):** ±100 mA
- **Power Consumption:**
  - **Power Dissipation (PD):** 
    - SC−88: 300 mW
    - SC−74: 300 mW
    - UDFN6: 812 mW
- **Operating Temperature Range:** -55°C to +125°C
- **Package Type:** Available in SC−88, SC−74, and UDFN6 packages.
- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):** Level 1 (JEDEC J-STD-020E)
- **Special Features:**
  - Input overvoltage tolerant up to 5.5V.
  - Supports partial power down protection (IOFF).
  - Pb-Free, Halogen Free/BFR Free, and RoHS compliant.
  - AEC−Q100 qualified for automotive applications.

**Typical Applications:**
The NL27WZU04 is commonly used in:
- **Logic Level Shifting:** Converting signals between different voltage levels.
- **Signal Inversion:** Inverting digital signals in various electronic circuits.
- **Automotive Applications:** Due to its AEC−Q100 qualification, it is suitable for automotive electronics.
- **Consumer Electronics:** Used in devices requiring low-voltage logic operations.
- **Communication Systems:** Employed in signal processing and data transmission applications.

This component is ideal for designers looking for a reliable and efficient dual inverter for low-voltage applications, particularly in environments where space and power efficiency are critical.
