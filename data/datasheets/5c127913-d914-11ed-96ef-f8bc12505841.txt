### Component Summary: HMC532LP4ETR

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Supply Voltage (Vcc): +3V (max +3.5V)
  - Tuning Voltage (Vtune): 1V to 13V (max +15V)
  
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Supply Current (Icc): 60 mA (min), 85 mA (typ), 100 mA (max) at Vcc = +3V
  
- **Power Consumption:**
  - Power Output: +14 dBm (typical)
  - Continuous Power Dissipation: 303 mW (at T = 85°C)
  
- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Storage Temperature: -65°C to +150°C
  - Operating Temperature: -40°C to +85°C
  
- **Package Type:**
  - 24 Lead QFN (4 x 4 mm) surface mount package
  
- **Special Features or Notes:**
  - Integrated resonators, negative resistance devices, varactor diodes, and buffer amplifiers
  - No external resonator needed
  - Excellent phase noise performance: -103 dBc/Hz @ 100 kHz offset
  - ESD Sensitivity: Class 1A (HBM)
  
- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL) JEDEC J-STD-020E:**
  - MSL1 for both HMC532LP4 and HMC532LP4E

#### Description:
The HMC532LP4ETR is a GaAs InGaP Heterojunction Bipolar Transistor (HBT) Monolithic Microwave Integrated Circuit (MMIC) Voltage Controlled Oscillator (VCO). It operates within a frequency range of 7.1 to 7.9 GHz and is designed to provide low noise performance with a typical output power of +14 dBm. The device integrates various components such as resonators, varactor diodes, and buffer amplifiers into a compact surface mount package, making it suitable for high-frequency applications.

#### Typical Applications:
The HMC532LP4ETR is primarily used in:
- **VSAT Radio:** For satellite communications, where stable frequency generation is crucial.
- **Point-to-Point/Multipoint Radio:** In wireless communication systems that require reliable signal transmission.
- **Test Equipment & Industrial Controls:** For precise frequency generation in testing and control applications.
- **Military End-Use:** In defense applications where performance and reliability are critical.

This component is ideal for applications requiring low phase noise and high-frequency stability, making it a valuable choice for modern communication systems.
