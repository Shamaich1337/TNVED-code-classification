### Component Summary: DG408DY-T1-E3 (Vishay)

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Maximum Supply Voltage (V+ to V-): ± 22 V
  - Absolute Maximum Voltage Rating: 44 V
  - Digital Inputs: -2 V to (V+) + 2 V
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Continuous Current (any terminal): 30 mA
  - Peak Current (pulsed at 1 ms, 10% duty cycle): 100 mA
- **Power Consumption:**
  - Supply Current (I_SUPPLY): 10 µA (typical)
- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - -40 °C to 85 °C
- **Package Type:**
  - 16-pin TSSOP (Thin Shrink Small Outline Package)
- **Special Features:**
  - Low on-resistance: R_DS(on) typically 100 Ω
  - Low charge injection: Q = 20 pC
  - Fast transition time: t_TRANS = 160 ns
  - TTL compatible logic
  - Enable function for resetting the multiplexer
- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL Level 1 per JEDEC J-STD-020E

#### Description:
The **DG408** is a high-performance CMOS analog multiplexer designed to connect one of eight single-ended inputs to a common output based on a 3-bit binary address (A0, A1, A2). It features break-before-make switching to prevent crosstalk between adjacent channels. The device is capable of conducting current in both directions when on and can block voltages up to the power supply rails when off. The enable (EN) function allows for easy control of the multiplexer state.

#### Typical Applications:
The DG408 is suitable for a variety of applications, including:
- **Data Acquisition Systems:** Used for selecting different input signals for processing.
- **Audio Signal Switching and Routing:** Ideal for audio applications where multiple sources need to be switched to a single output.
- **Automated Test Equipment (ATE) Systems:** Facilitates the testing of multiple channels in electronic devices.
- **Battery-Powered Systems:** Its low power consumption makes it suitable for portable applications.
- **Medical Instrumentation:** Can be used in devices requiring precise signal routing and low power operation.

This component is particularly advantageous in applications where high speed and low power dissipation are critical, making it ideal for battery-operated and remote instrumentation systems.
