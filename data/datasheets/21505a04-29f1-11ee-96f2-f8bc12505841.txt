  - Nominal Supply Voltage: 3.3V
  - Threshold Voltage (VIT): 2.93V (typical)
  
- **Current Ratings**: 
  - Supply Current: 220nA (typical), up to 450nA (maximum)
  - Maximum Low Output Current (IOL): 5mA
  - Maximum High Output Current (IOH): -5mA

- **Power Consumption**: 
  - Supply current is very low, typically 220nA, making it suitable for battery-powered applications.

- **Operating Temperature Range**: 
  - -40°C to 85°C

- **Package Type**: 
  - SOT-23 (5-pin) and WSON (6-pin)

- **Special Features**: 
  - Selectable reset delay (10ms or 200ms)
  - Active-low open-drain RESET output
  - Manual reset capability
  - Precision voltage supervision for low-power DSPs and microcontrollers

- **Moisture Sensitive Level**: 
  - MSL Level 1 (JEDEC J-STD-020E)

#### Description:
The **TPS3838K33DBVR** is a **NanoPower Voltage Supervisor** designed to monitor supply voltages in low-power applications. It provides a reset signal to microcontrollers and digital signal processors (DSPs) when the supply voltage falls below a specified threshold. The device features a selectable reset delay, allowing for flexibility in system initialization timing.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Battery-Powered Equipment**: Ideal for portable devices where low power consumption is critical.
- **Microcontroller and DSP Systems**: Used in applications requiring precise voltage monitoring and reset functionality.
- **Wireless Communication Systems**: Ensures reliable operation by monitoring supply voltage levels.
- **Intelligent Instruments**: Provides reset signals to maintain system integrity during power fluctuations.
- **Automotive Systems**: Can be used in automotive applications requiring low power and high reliability.

This component is particularly useful in applications where maintaining system stability during power-up and power-down sequences is essential, making it a valuable addition to designs involving low-power microcontrollers and DSPs.
