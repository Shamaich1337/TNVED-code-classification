### Component Summary: TPD12S521DBTR

**Manufacturer:** Texas Instruments  
**Product Code:** TPD12S521DBTR  
**Package Type:** TSSOP (38 pins)  
**Moisture Sensitive Level:** Level 2 (JEDEC J-STD-020E)

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Supply Voltage (5V_SUPPLY): 5V to 5.5V
  - Bias Supply Voltage (LV_SUPPLY): 1.7V to 5.5V
- **Current Ratings:**
  - On-chip Current Regulator Output: 55 mA
  - Operating Supply Current: 110 µA to 130 µA
  - Bias Supply Current: 1 µA to 5 µA
- **Power Consumption:**
  - Operating Supply Current: 110 µA to 130 µA
  - Short-Circuit Current Limit: 90 mA to 175 mA
- **Operating Temperature Range:** -40°C to 85°C
- **Special Features:**
  - IEC61000-4-2 Level 4 ESD Protection (±8-kV Contact Discharge)
  - Integrated voltage-level shifting for control lines
  - Backdrive protection on multiple pins
  - Lead-free and RoHS compliant
- **Capacitance:**
  - High-Speed TMDS Lines: 0.8 pF
  - Control Lines: 3.5 pF

#### Description:
The TPD12S521 is a single-chip electro-static discharge (ESD) protection device designed specifically for HDMI transmitter ports. It provides robust ESD protection while maintaining minimal additional capacitance on high-speed differential signals. The device integrates a current regulator, allowing for HDMI receiver detection even when powered off, and features voltage-level shifting to eliminate the need for external components.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Consumer Electronics:** Used in devices such as TVs, monitors, and projectors to protect HDMI interfaces.
- **PCs and Laptops:** Provides ESD protection for HDMI ports in computing devices.
- **Set-Top Boxes and DVD Players:** Ensures reliable operation of HDMI connections in home entertainment systems.
- **General HDMI Transmitter Protection:** Suitable for any application requiring HDMI connectivity with ESD protection.

The TPD12S521 is ideal for applications where high-speed data integrity is critical, and it simplifies design by integrating multiple functions into a single package, reducing the need for additional components.
