### Component Summary: PTVS30VS1UR (Nexperia)

#### Key Characteristics and Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Reverse Standoff Voltage (VRWM): 30 V
  - Breakdown Voltage (VBR): 33.3 V (min), 35.1 V (typ), 36.8 V (max)
  
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Rated Peak Pulse Power (PPPM): 400 W
  - Rated Peak Pulse Current (IPPM): 8.3 A (max)
  - Reverse Leakage Current (IRM): 0.001 μA (max)

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Power consumption is primarily determined by the clamping voltage and the current during transient events, with a maximum clamping voltage of 48.4 V.

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Junction Temperature (Tj): -150 °C to +150 °C
  - Ambient Temperature (Tamb): -55 °C to +150 °C
  - Storage Temperature (Tstg): -65 °C to +150 °C

- **Package Type:**
  - SOD123W (small and flat lead low-profile Surface-Mounted Device)

- **Special Features or Notes:**
  - AEC-Q101 qualified, suitable for automotive applications.
  - Designed for transient overvoltage protection.
  - Very low package height of 1 mm.
  - Compliant with IEC 61643-321 standards for surge protection.

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL according to JEDEC J-STD-020E is not specified in the provided data.

#### Description:
The PTVS30VS1UR is a unidirectional Transient Voltage Suppressor (TVS) designed to protect sensitive electronic components from voltage spikes and transients. It is capable of handling a peak pulse power of 400 W, making it suitable for applications where high-energy transients may occur. The device is housed in a compact SOD123W package, which allows for easy surface mounting in various electronic circuits.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Power Supply Protection:** Safeguarding power supply circuits from voltage transients that could damage components.
- **Automotive Applications:** Protecting automotive electronics from voltage spikes due to load dump or other transient events.
- **Industrial Applications:** Used in industrial equipment to protect against electrical noise and transients.
- **Power Management:** Ensuring stable operation of power management circuits by clamping voltage spikes.

This component is essential in applications where reliability and protection against transient voltages are critical, ensuring the longevity and functionality of electronic devices.
