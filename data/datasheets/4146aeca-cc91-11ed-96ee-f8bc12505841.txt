B | Page 12 of 16 APPLICATIONS INFORMATION  \nPOWER/GROUND LAYOUT AND BYPASSING  \nThe ADCLK905/ADCLK907/ADCLK925 buffers are  designed \nfor very high speed applications. Consequently, high speed design  \ntechniques must be used to achieve the specified  performance. \nIt is critically important to use low imped ance supply planes  for \nboth the negative supply (V EE) and the positive supply (V CC) planes \nas part of a multilayer board. Providing the lowest inducta nce \nreturn path for  switching  currents ensures the best possible  \nperformance in the target application.  \nIt is also important to adequately bypass the input and output \nsupplies. A 1  µF electrolytic bypass capacitor should be placed \nwithin several inches of  each power supply pin to ground. In \naddition, multiple high quality 0.001 µ F bypass capacitors \nshould  be placed as close as possible to each of the V EE and  VCC \nsupply pins and should be connected to the GND plane with \nredundant  vias. High frequency bypass  capacitors should be \ncarefully selected for minimum inductance and ESR. Parasitic \nlayout inductance should be strictly avoided to maximize the effectiveness of the bypass at high frequencies.  \nOUTPUT STAGE S \nThe s pecified performance can be achieved only b y using proper \ntrans mission line terminati ons. The outputs of the ADCLK905/ \nADCLK907/ ADCLK925 buffer s are designed to directly drive \n800 mV into 50 Ω  cable or microstrip/stripline transmission \nlines terminated with 50  Ω referenced to V CC − 2 V. The PECL \noutput stage is shown in Figure 25. The outputs are designed  for \nbest transmission  line matching. If high speed  signals must be \nrouted more than a centimeter,  either the microstrip  or the \nstripline technique  is required to ensure proper transition times \nand to prevent excessive output ringing and pulse width -\ndependent propagation delay dispersion.  \nVEEVCC\nQ\nQ\n06318-025 \nFigure 25. Discontinuities along input and output \ntransmission lines can also severely limit the specified jitter  \nperformance  by reducing the effective input s lew rate . \nIn a 50  Ω environment,  input and output matching ha ve a \nsignificant impact on performance.   - Operating Voltage: 2.5 V to 3.3 V

  - -40°C to +125°C
- **Package Type**: 
  - 16-Lead Lead Frame Chip Scale Package (LFCSP)
- **Special Features**:
  - 95 ps propagation delay
  - 7.5 GHz toggle rate
  - 60 fs random jitter (RJ)
  - On-chip terminations at both input pins
- **Moisture Sensitive Level**: 
  - Level 1 (JEDEC J-STD-020E)

#### Description:
The **ADCLK925BCPZ-R7** is an ultrafast clock/data buffer designed for high-speed applications. It is part of the ADCLK900 series from Analog Devices, which utilizes a proprietary silicon germanium (SiGe) bipolar process. The ADCLK925 variant features one input and two outputs, making it suitable for applications requiring signal distribution with minimal delay and jitter.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Clock and Data Signal Restoration**: Enhances the integrity of clock and data signals in high-speed digital systems.
- **Automated Test Equipment (ATE)**: Used in testing environments where precise timing and signal integrity are critical.
- **High-Speed Instrumentation**: Ideal for applications that require fast and accurate signal processing.
- **High-Speed Line Receivers**: Functions as a buffer to drive high-speed signals over long distances.
- **Threshold Detection**: Can be used in systems that require precise voltage level detection.
- **Converter Clocking**: Provides clock signals for data converters in high-speed applications.

This component is particularly valuable in environments where high-speed data transmission and minimal signal degradation are essential, such as telecommunications, data centers, and advanced computing systems.
