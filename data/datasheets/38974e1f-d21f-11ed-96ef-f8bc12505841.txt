### Component Summary: PHA-23LN+

**Manufacturer:** Mini-Circuits  
**Product Code:** PHA-23LN+  
**Type:** Monolithic Amplifier  
**Description:** The PHA-23LN+ is an advanced wideband amplifier utilizing E-PHEMT technology, designed for ultra-high dynamic range applications. It operates over a broad frequency range from 30 MHz to 2 GHz and features low noise figures and high linearity, making it suitable for various communication systems.

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Operating Voltage: 3V to 5V
  - DC Voltage on Pin 3: 10V

- **Current Ratings:**
  - Operating Current: 72.4 mA (typical at 3V), 141.7 mA (typical at 5V)

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Power Dissipation: 53.3W (maximum)
  - Input Power (CW): +4 dBm (continuous at 3V), +8 dBm (continuous at 5V)

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Operating Temperature: -40°C to 105°C
  - Storage Temperature: -65°C to 150°C

- **Package Type:**
  - Case Style: DF782 (SOT-89)

- **Special Features:**
  - Extremely high IP3: 40.9 dBm typical at 30 MHz, 37.4 dBm typical at 1 GHz
  - Low Noise Figure: 1.2 dB at 1 GHz
  - Good input and output return loss across the frequency range
  - RoHS compliant

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL1 in accordance with IPC/JEDEC J-STD-020D

#### Description:
The PHA-23LN+ is a monolithic amplifier designed for high-performance applications in wireless communications. Its E-PHEMT technology allows for excellent linearity and low noise, making it ideal for amplifying weak signals while maintaining signal integrity.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Base Station Infrastructure:** Used in cellular and CATV systems to amplify signals for transmission and reception.
- **Driver Amplifiers:** Suitable for complex waveform upconverter paths and linearized transmit systems.
- **Secondary Amplifiers:** Employed in ultra-high dynamic range receivers to enhance signal quality and range.

This amplifier is particularly beneficial in environments where high linearity and low noise are critical, such as in modern communication systems and broadcasting applications.
