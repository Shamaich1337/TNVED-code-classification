Decoupling has two main advantages for this application: it helps to reduce electromagnetic\ninterference and rejects the ripple that may appear on the output.\nA bypass capacitor combination, composed of 100 nF in addition to 10 nF and 1 nF in parallel is recommended\nbecause it eliminates spikes on the supply line better than a single 100 nF capacitor. Each millimeter of the PCB\ntrack plays an important role. Bypass capacitors must be placed as close as possible to the comparator supply\npin.\nThe smallest value capacitor should be preferably placed closer to the supply pin.\nIn addition, important values of input impedance in series with parasitic PCB capacity and input comparator\ncapacity create an additional RC filter. It generates an additional propagation delay.\nFor high speed signal applications, PCB must be designed with great care taking into consideration low resistive\ngrounding, short tracks and quality SMD capacitors featuring low ESR. Bypass capacitor stores energy and\nprovides a complementary energy tank when spikes occur on the power supply line. If the input signal frequency\nis far from the resonant frequency, impedance strongly increases and the capacitor loses bypassing capability.\nPlacing different capacitors with different resonant frequencies allows a wide frequency bandwidth to be covered.\nIt is also recommended to implement an unbroken ground plane with low inductance.\nFigure 20.
