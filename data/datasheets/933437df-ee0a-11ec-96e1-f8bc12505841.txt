### Component Summary: SN74LVC2G157DCUT

#### Key Characteristics and Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Supply Voltage (VCC): 1.65V to 5.5V
  - Absolute Maximum Supply Voltage: 6.5V
  - Input Voltage: 0V to 5.5V

- **Current Ratings:**
  - Continuous Output Current: ±50 mA
  - Maximum Output Drive: ±24 mA at 3.3V

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Maximum Supply Current (ICC): 10 µA
  - Power Dissipation Capacitance (Cpd): 37 pF at VCC = 3.3V

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - -40°C to 85°C

- **Package Type:**
  - VSSOP (8 pins), DSBGA (8 pins), and SSOP (8 pins)

- **Special Features:**
  - Supports live insertion and partial-power-down mode
  - Down translation capability for inputs up to 5.5V
  - Latch-up performance exceeds 100mA per JESD 78, Class II
  - ESD protection: 2000V (Human Body Model), 1000V (Charged Device Model)

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - Level 1, according to JEDEC J-STD-020E

#### Description:
The SN74LVC2G157 is a single 2-line to 1-line data selector multiplexer designed for operation within a voltage range of 1.65V to 5.5V. It features a common strobe input (G) that controls the selection of data from two sources, providing both true and complementary outputs. The device is notable for its low power consumption and high-speed operation, with a maximum propagation delay (tpd) of 6 ns at 3.3V.

#### Typical Applications:
The SN74LVC2G157 is widely used in various applications, including:
- **Data Routing:** It allows a single controller to select data from two different digital signal sources, such as sensors.
- **Embedded Systems:** Commonly found in embedded PCs, HVAC systems, and network-attached storage (NAS).
- **Consumer Electronics:** Utilized in devices like barcode scanners, e-books, and high-definition TVs.
- **Communication Systems:** Employed in video communication systems and software-defined radios (SDR).
- **Biometric Systems:** Used in fingerprint biometrics and other sensor applications.

This component is ideal for applications requiring efficient data selection and routing with minimal power consumption, making it suitable for battery-operated devices and systems where space is a constraint.
