### Component Summary: MASWSS0178TR

**Manufacturer:** M/A-COM Technology Solutions Inc.  
**Product Code:** MASWSS0178TR  
**Type:** SPDT High Isolation Terminated Switch  
**Frequency Range:** 0.01 - 3.0 GHz  

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Control Voltage: 0 / +5 V
  - Operating Voltage: +8.5 V
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Control Current (VC = 5 V): 2 µA (typical), up to 13 µA (maximum)
- **Power Consumption:**
  - Input Power (0.5 - 3.0 GHz): +30 dBm
  - Control Voltage: +33 dBm
- **Operating Temperature Range:** -40°C to +85°C
- **Storage Temperature Range:** -65°C to +150°C
- **Package Type:** Lead-Free MSOP-8-EP
- **Special Features:**
  - High Isolation: 55 dB @ 0.9 GHz, 50 dB @ 1.9 GHz
  - Low Insertion Loss: 0.6 dB @ 0.9 GHz, 0.7 dB @ 1.9 GHz
  - 50 Ω Internal Terminations
  - RoHS Compliant
  - Halogen-Free “Green” Mold Compound
  - 260°C Reflow Compatible
- **Moisture Sensitive Level:** Meets JEDEC moisture sensitivity level 1 requirements.

#### Description:
The MASWSS0178 is a GaAs (Gallium Arsenide) monolithic switch designed for high isolation and low insertion loss in a compact, lead-free surface mount package. It operates effectively across a wide frequency range, making it suitable for various RF applications. The device utilizes a 1.0-micron gate length MESFET process, which enhances its performance and reliability through full chip passivation.

#### Typical Applications:
The MASWSS0178 is ideal for:
- **Synthesizer Switching:** Used in frequency synthesizers to switch between different frequency paths.
- **Transmit/Receive Switching:** Commonly found in radio and cellular equipment to manage the signal path between transmitters and receivers.
- **Switch Matrices:** Employed in complex routing systems where multiple signal paths need to be managed.
- **Filter Banks:** Utilized in systems requiring selective frequency filtering, such as GPS and fiber optic modules.

This component is particularly valuable in telecommunications, radio frequency applications, and any system requiring efficient signal routing with minimal loss and high isolation.
