### Component Summary: SN74AC74D

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Supply Voltage (V_CC): 2.0 V to 6.0 V
  - Absolute Maximum Supply Voltage: -0.5 V to 7.0 V
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Continuous Output Current (I_O): ±50 mA
  - Continuous Current through V_CC or GND: ±200 mA
- **Power Consumption:**
  - Typical ICC (Input Current): 2 µA at V_CC = 5.5 V
- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - -40°C to 85°C
- **Package Type:**
  - SOIC (Small Outline Integrated Circuit) - D package
- **Special Features:**
  - Dual positive-edge-triggered D-type flip-flops
  - Inputs accept voltages up to 6 V
  - Maximum propagation delay (t_pd): 10 ns at 5 V
- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - Level 1 (according to JEDEC J-STD-020E)

#### Description:
The SN74AC74D is a dual positive-edge-triggered D-type flip-flop from Texas Instruments. It features two flip-flops that can be used to store binary data. The device has preset (PRE) and clear (CLR) inputs that allow for setting or resetting the outputs regardless of the clock input. When the PRE and CLR inputs are inactive (high), the data present at the D input is transferred to the outputs on the rising edge of the clock pulse.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Data Storage:** Used in digital circuits to store binary information temporarily.
- **State Machines:** Commonly employed in sequential logic circuits for state storage.
- **Timing Applications:** Useful in applications requiring synchronization of data transfer.
- **Frequency Division:** Can be used in frequency division circuits where clock signals need to be manipulated.

This component is ideal for applications in digital electronics where reliable data storage and transfer are required, such as in microcontrollers, digital signal processing, and various logic circuits.
