### Component Summary: SN74LVC257APWR

#### Key Characteristics and Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Operating Voltage (VCC): 1.65 V to 3.6 V
  - Input Voltage: Up to 5.5 V
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Continuous Output Current: ±50 mA
  - Continuous Current through VCC or GND: ±100 mA
- **Power Consumption:**
  - Typical ICC (Supply Current): 10 µA at VCC = 3.6 V
- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - -40 °C to 85 °C (commercial grade)
  - -55 °C to 125 °C (military grade)
- **Package Type:**
  - TSSOP (Thin Shrink Small Outline Package), 16 pins
- **Special Features:**
  - 3-state outputs that do not load the data lines when the output-enable (OE) input is high.
  - Maximum propagation delay (tpd) of 4.6 ns at VCC = 3.3 V.
  - ESD protection exceeds JESD 22 standards (2000 V HBM).
  - Latch-up performance exceeds 250 mA per JESD 17.
- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL Level 1, per JEDEC J-STD-020E.

#### Description:
The SN74LVC257APWR is a quadruple 2-line to 1-line data selector and multiplexer designed for low-voltage applications. It operates within a voltage range of 1.65 V to 3.6 V and can accept input voltages up to 5.5 V, making it suitable for interfacing between different voltage levels in mixed-signal environments. The device features 3-state outputs, allowing for flexible data routing without loading the data lines when not in use.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Data Routing:** The SN74LVC257A is commonly used in digital signal routing applications where multiple data sources need to be multiplexed to a single output line.
- **Level Translation:** It can serve as a level translator between 3.3 V and 5 V systems, facilitating communication between devices operating at different voltage levels.
- **Bus-Organized Systems:** Ideal for use in bus-organized systems where multiple data lines are managed efficiently.
- **Telecom Infrastructure:** Utilized in telecom systems for signal multiplexing and data management.
- **I/O Expanders:** Can be used in applications requiring additional input/output lines.

This component is particularly valuable in high-speed applications due to its low propagation delay and ability to handle mixed voltage levels, making it a versatile choice for modern electronic designs.
