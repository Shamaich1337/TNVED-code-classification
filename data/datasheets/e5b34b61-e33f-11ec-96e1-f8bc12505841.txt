### Component Summary: LM28-A3S-TI-N-DR

**Manufacturer:** APAC OPTO ELECTRONICS INC  
**Product Code:** LM28-A3S-TI-N-DR  
**Type:** 850 nm Multi-mode Transceiver (Small Form Pluggable - SFP)  
**Function:** This component is designed for high-speed data communication, specifically for Fast Ethernet applications, providing both transmission and reception capabilities with diagnostic monitoring.

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Supply Voltage (Vcc): 3.1 V to 3.5 V
  - Absolute Maximum Supply Voltage: -0.5 V to 4.0 V
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Supply Current (ITX + IRX): Up to 200 mA
- **Power Consumption:**
  - Single power supply of 3.3 V
- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - -40°C to 85°C (case temperature)
- **Package Type:**
  - Small Form Pluggable (SFP)
- **Special Features:**
  - RoHS compliant
  - Hot pluggable
  - Class 1 laser product compliant with EN 60825-1
  - Compliant with SFF8472 diagnostic monitoring interface
  - Duplex LC connector
- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - Not specified in the provided text.

#### Description:
The LM28-A3S-TI-N-DR is an 850 nm multi-mode transceiver that operates at a bit rate of 125 Mbps, making it suitable for Fast Ethernet applications. It features diagnostic monitoring capabilities, allowing for real-time assessment of the transceiver's performance and health. The device is designed to be hot pluggable, which facilitates easy replacement without powering down the system.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Data Communication:** Primarily used in networking equipment for Fast Ethernet connections.
- **Telecommunications:** Suitable for applications requiring reliable data transmission over short distances.
- **Diagnostic Monitoring:** Ideal for systems that require real-time monitoring of optical performance and health.
- **Industrial Automation:** Can be utilized in environments where robust communication links are necessary.

This transceiver is particularly valuable in environments where space is limited and high-speed data transfer is essential, such as in data centers, enterprise networks, and telecommunications infrastructure.
