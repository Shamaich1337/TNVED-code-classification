I For more information www.analog.comAPPLICATIONS INFORMATION\nThe L T6700-1/L T6700-2/L T6700-3/L T6700HV-1/\nL T6700HV-2/L T6700HV-3 devices are a family of dual micropower comparators with a built-in 400mV refer -\nence. Features include wide supply voltage range (1.4V to 18V), Over\n-The-Top input and output range, 2% accurate \nrising input threshold voltage and 6.5mV typical built-in hysteresis. The comparator’s open-collector outputs can sink up to 40mA typical.\nInternal Reference\nEach of the comparator sections has one input available \nexternally, with the three versions of the part differing by the polarity of those available inputs (i.e., inverting or noninverting). The other comparator inputs are connected internally to the 400mV reference. The rising input threshold voltage of the comparators is designed to be equal to that of the reference (i.e., ≈400mV). The reference voltage is established with respect to the device GND connection.\nHysteresis\nEach comparator has built-in 6.5mV (typical) hysteresis to \nsimplify designs, ensure stable operation in the presence of noise at the inputs, and to reject supply rail noise that might be induced by state change load transients. The hysteresis is designed such that the falling input threshold voltage is nominally 393.5mV . External positive feedback circuitry can be employed with noninverting comparator inputs to increase effective hysteresis if desired, but such circuitry will provide an apparent effect on both the rising and falling input thresholds (the actual internal thresholds remain unaffected).\nComparator Inputs\nA comparator input can swing from ground to 18V (36V \nfor L T6700HV), regardless of the supply voltage used. The typical input current for inputs well above threshold (i.e., >800mV) is a few pA leaking into an input. With decreasing input voltage, a small bias current begins to be drawn out of the input, reaching a few nA when at ground potential. The input may be forced 100mV below ground without causing an improper output, though some additional bias current will begin to flow from the parasitic ESD input pro -\ntection diode. Inputs driven further negative than 100mV below ground will not cause comparator malfunction or damage \n(provided the current is limited to 10mA), but \nthe accuracy of the reference cannot be guaranteed, in which case the output state of the alternate comparator may be affected.\nComparator Outputs\nThe comparator outputs are open collector and capable \nof sinking 40mA typical. Load currents are directed out the GND pin of the part. The output off-state voltage may range between –0.3V and 18V (36V for L T6700HV) with respect to ground, regardless of the supply voltage used. When the output high state bias voltage is above 18V , a 100k minimum pull-up resistor is required and total load capacitor must be less than 100nF . If the output high state is above 18V , caution must be taken to prevent a short from the output directly to the bias voltage, even if the output is in the off state. As with any open-collector device, the outputs may be tied together to implement wire-AND logic functions.\nPower Supplies\nThe comparator family core circuitry operates from a single \n1.4V to 18V supply. A minimum 0.1µF bypass capacitor is required between the V\nS pin and GND. When an output \nload is connected to the supply rail near the part and the output is sinking more than 5mA, a 1µF bypass capaci -\ntor is recommended. The voltage reference built into the L\nT6700 can be susceptible to high noise on the supply \nline, particularly noise that is less than 50kHz and larger than 20mV\nP-P. In order to reduce the probability of a false \ncomparator output in the presence of high supply noise, an RC filter should be used to reduce the noise. ### Component Summary: LT6700IS6-1#TRMPBF

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Supply Voltage (V_S): 1.4V to 18V
  - Input Voltage: -0.3V to 18V (36V for LT6700HV)
  - Output Voltage: -0.3V to 18V (36V for LT6700HV)
  
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Supply Current (I_S): 6.5 µA (typical at 5V)
  - Output Sinking Capability: > 5 mA (guaranteed over temperature)
  
- **Power Consumption:**
  - Low quiescent current of 6.5 µA, making it suitable for battery-powered applications.
  
- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - -40°C to 85°C (commercial grade)
  
- **Package Type:**
  - 6-lead Plastic TSOT-23 (S6 package)
  
- **Special Features:**
  - Built-in 400mV reference voltage.
  - Internal hysteresis of 6.5mV (typical).
  - Open-collector outputs for level translation.
  - AEC-Q100 qualified for automotive applications.
  
- **Moisture Sensitive Level:**
  - MSL Level 1 (JEDEC J-STD-020E).

#### Description:
The LT6700IS6-1#TRMPBF is a dual micropower comparator designed for low voltage applications. It features a built-in 400mV reference voltage, making it ideal for battery monitoring and other low-power applications. The device operates with supply voltages ranging from 1.4V to 18V and consumes only 6.5 µA of quiescent current, which is particularly beneficial for battery-operated devices. The comparators have open-collector outputs, allowing them to interface with various voltage levels.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Battery-Powered System Monitoring:** The low power consumption and built-in reference make it suitable for monitoring battery levels.
- **Threshold Detectors:** Can be used to detect specific voltage levels in various circuits.
- **Window Comparators:** The device can be configured to monitor voltage levels within a specified range.
- **Relay Driving:** The open-collector outputs can drive relays or other loads.
- **Industrial Control Systems:** Useful in applications requiring precise voltage monitoring and control.
- **Automotive Monitoring and Controls:** AEC-Q100 qualification makes it suitable for automotive applications.

This component is particularly useful in applications where low power consumption and small size are critical, such as in portable devices and battery management systems.
