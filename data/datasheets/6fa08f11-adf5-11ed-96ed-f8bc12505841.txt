Optional configurations \ninclude devices with active-high and active-low output signals (TPS3824/5), devices with a watchdog timer \n(TPS3820/3/4/8), and devices with manual reset ( MR) pins (TPS3820/3/5/8). RESET  asserts when the supply \nvoltage, V DD, rises above 1.1 V. For devices with active-low output logic, the device monitors V DD and keeps \nRESET  low as long as V DD remains below the negative threshold voltage, V IT−. For devices with active-high \noutput logic, RESET remains high as long as V DD remains below V IT−. An internal timer delays the return of the \noutput to the inactive state (high) to ensure proper system reset. The delay time, t d, starts after V DD rises above \nthe positive threshold voltage (V IT− + V HYS). When the supply voltage drops below V IT−, the output becomes \nactive (low) again. All the devices of this family have a fixed-sense threshold voltage, V IT–, set by an internal \nvoltage divider, so no external components are required.\nThe TPS382x family is designed to monitor supply voltages of 2.5 V, 3 V, 3.3 V, and 5 V. The devices are \navailable in a 5-pin SOT-23 package and are characterized for operation over a temperature range of −40°C to \n85°C. Only TPS3823A-33 is characterized for operation over a temperature range −40°C to 125°C.\n8.2 Functional Block Diagram\n_+\nRESET(1)\nRESET\nMR(2)\nWDI(3)Auto-Reset\nOscillator\nTransition\nDetectorWatchdog\nTimer LogicRESET\nLogic\nVREFVDD\n52 k /c87\n40 k /c87\nA. TPS3828 devices have an open-drain output.TPS3820, TPS3823, TPS3824, TPS3825, TPS3828\nSLVS165N – APRIL 1998 – REVISED JULY 2022 www.ti.com\n14 Submit Document Feedback Copyright © 2022 Texas Instruments Incorporated\nProduct Folder Links: TPS3820  TPS3823  TPS3824  TPS3825  TPS3828\n8.3.4 Watchdog Timer (WDI)\nThe TPS3820, TPS3823, TPS3824, and TPS3828 devices have a watchdog timer that must be periodically \ntriggered by either a positive or negative transition at WDI to avoid a reset signal being issued. The A version does not latch \nthe reset signal to the asserted state if a WDI pulse is received while RESET is asserted. An external FET \ndecouples the WDI signal by disconnecting the WDI input when RESET  is asserted. For more details on this, \nsee Decoupling WDI During Reset Event . - **Special Features**: 
  - Watchdog timer functionality
  - Manual reset input
  - Active-low reset output
  - Open-drain output configuration
- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL)**: 
  - Level 1 (according to JEDEC J-STD-020E)

#### Description:
The TPS3828-33DBVR is a voltage monitor with a watchdog timer designed primarily for DSP and processor-based systems. It provides circuit initialization and timing supervision, ensuring that the system operates correctly during power-up and under varying supply conditions. The device asserts a reset signal when the supply voltage (VDD) rises above a specified threshold and maintains the reset state as long as the voltage remains below a defined level. The TPS3828 features an open-drain output configuration, allowing for flexible integration into various circuit designs.

- **Microcontrollers and DSPs**: Ensures proper initialization and operation by monitoring supply voltage.
- **Industrial Equipment**: Provides reliability in power management for industrial control systems.
- **Automotive Systems**: Used in automotive electronics for voltage monitoring and reset functionality.
- **Portable and Battery-Powered Equipment**: Helps manage power states and resets in battery-operated devices.
- **Wireless Communication Systems**: Ensures stable operation of communication devices by monitoring supply voltage.
- **Notebook and Desktop Computers**: Provides reset functionality to ensure system stability during power fluctuations.

This component is ideal for applications requiring reliable voltage monitoring and reset capabilities, particularly in environments where power stability is critical.
