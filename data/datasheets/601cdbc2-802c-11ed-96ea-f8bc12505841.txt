### Component Summary: MC74AC574DWG

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - DC Supply Voltage (VCC): -0.5 to +7.0 V
  - Recommended Operating Voltage for AC: 2.0 to 6.0 V
  - Recommended Operating Voltage for ACT: 4.5 to 5.5 V

- **Current Ratings:**
  - DC Input Current (per pin): ±20 mA
  - DC Output Sink/Source Current (per pin): ±50 mA
  - Output Current (High): -24 mA
  - Output Current (Low): 24 mA

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Maximum Quiescent Supply Current: 8.0 mA at 5.5 V

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Storage Temperature: -65 to +150 °C
  - Ambient Operating Temperature: -40 to +85 °C

- **Package Type:**
  - SOIC-20 (DW suffix)

- **Special Features:**
  - Octal D Flip-Flop with 3-State Outputs
  - Buffered common Clock (CP) and Output Enable (OE)
  - Outputs can source/sink 24 mA
  - TTL compatible inputs for the ACT version
  - Pb-Free packages available

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL Level: 1 (JEDEC J-STD-020E)

#### Description:
The **MC74AC574** is a high-speed, low-power octal D flip-flop designed for use in digital circuits. It features eight edge-triggered flip-flops with individual D-type inputs and 3-state true outputs. The device captures the input data on the LOW-to-HIGH transition of the clock signal (CP) and allows for easy interfacing with microprocessors due to its pin configuration.

#### Typical Applications:
The MC74AC574 is commonly used in:
- **Data Storage:** As a temporary storage element in digital circuits.
- **Microprocessor Interfaces:** It can serve as an input or output port for microprocessors, facilitating data transfer.
- **Bus-Oriented Applications:** The 3-state outputs make it suitable for bus systems where multiple devices share the same data lines.
- **Signal Processing:** It can be used in various signal processing applications where data needs to be synchronized and stored.

This component is ideal for applications requiring reliable data storage and transfer in digital systems, particularly in environments where low power consumption and high-speed operation are critical.
