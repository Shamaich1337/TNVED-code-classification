The HMC797A is a GaAs PHEMT (Pseudomorphic High Electron Mobility Transistor) MMIC (Monolithic Microwave Integrated Circuit) power amplifier designed for operation in the frequency range of DC to 22 GHz. It is capable of delivering high output power and gain, making it suitable for various high-frequency applications.

**Key Specifications:**
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Supply Voltage (Vdd): +8 V to +11 V (typical +10 V)
  - Gate Bias Voltage (Vgg1): -3 V to 0 V
  - Gate Bias Voltage (Vgg2): +2.5 V to (Vdd - 6.5 V)

- **Current Ratings:**
  - Supply Current (Idd): 400 mA

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Power Dissipation: 5.73 W (at T = 85 °C)

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Storage Temperature: -65 °C to +150 °C
  - Operating Temperature: -55 °C to +85 °C

- **Package Type:**
  - Die Size: 2.89 mm x 1.55 mm x 0.1 mm

- **Special Features:**
  - High output power: +29 dBm at 1 dB gain compression, +31 dBm saturated output power
  - High gain: 15 dB
  - High output IP3: +41 dBm
  - 50 Ohm matched input/output
  - Positive gain slope from 2 to 20 GHz
  - Internally matched I/Os for easy integration into Multi-Chip Modules (MCMs)

- **Moisture Sensitive Level:**
  - JEDEC J-STD-020E: Not specified in the provided text.

**Typical Applications:**
The HMC797A is ideal for:
- Test Instrumentation
- Military and Space applications
- Fiber Optics
- Electronic Warfare (EW) and Electronic Countermeasures (ECM)
- Radar systems

### Summary:
The HMC797A is a high-performance power amplifier suitable for a wide range of applications requiring high linearity and output power in the microwave frequency range. Its robust design and specifications make it particularly well-suited for military, aerospace, and advanced test equipment applications.
