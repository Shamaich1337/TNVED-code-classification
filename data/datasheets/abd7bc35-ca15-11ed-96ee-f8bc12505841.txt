Links: TPS763Submit Documentation Feedback Copyright ©1998 –2019, Texas Instruments Incorporated12Detailed Description\n12.1 Overview\nThe TPS763xx devices uses aPMOS pass element todramatically reduce both dropout voltage and supply\ncurrent over more conventional PNP pass element LDO designs. The PMOS pass element isavoltage-\ncontrolled device that, unlike aPNP transistor, does notrequire increased drive current asoutput current\nincreases.   - Continuous Output Current (IO): 0 to 150 mA

  - Quiescent Current (I(Q)): 85 to 100 µA (typical), 140 µA (maximum)
  - Standby Current: 0.5 to 1 µA (when disabled)
- **Operating Temperature Range**: 
  - Junction Temperature (TJ): -40°C to 125°C
- **Package Type**: 
  - 5-pin SOT-23 (DBV)
- **Special Features**: 
  - Low dropout voltage (typically 300 mV at 150 mA)
  - Thermal protection and overcurrent limitation
  - Logic-enabled shutdown mode
- **Moisture Sensitive Level**: 
  - MSL Level 1 (JEDEC J-STD-020E)

#### Description:
The TPS76350DBVR is a low-dropout (LDO) linear voltage regulator designed to provide a stable output voltage of 5V with a maximum output current of 150 mA. It features a PMOS pass element that allows for low dropout voltage and low quiescent current, making it suitable for battery-operated devices. The device is housed in a compact SOT-23 package, which is ideal for space-constrained applications.

#### Typical Applications:
The TPS76350 is commonly used in various applications, including:
- **Electricity Meters**: Providing stable voltage for measurement circuits.
- **Solar Inverters**: Regulating voltage in renewable energy systems.
- **HVAC Systems**: Powering control circuits in heating, ventilation, and air conditioning systems.
- **Servo Drives and Motion Control**: Supplying power to control systems in robotics and automation.
- **Sensor Transmitters**: Ensuring reliable voltage for sensor operation in various environments.

This component is particularly advantageous in portable systems where low power consumption and minimal heat generation are critical for extending battery life.
