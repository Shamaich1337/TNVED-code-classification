### Component Summary: HMC441LH5

**Description:**
The HMC441LH5 is a GaAs PHEMT (Pseudomorphic High Electron Mobility Transistor) MMIC (Monolithic Microwave Integrated Circuit) medium power amplifier designed for broadband applications. It operates within a frequency range of 7 to 15.5 GHz and is housed in a hermetic SMT (Surface Mount Technology) leadless package. This amplifier is optimized for high reliability and is suitable for military, industrial, and space applications.

**Key Specifications:**
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Supply Voltage (Vdd): +5V (max +6V)
  
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Supply Current (Idd): 90 mA (typical at +5V)
  
- **Power Consumption:**
  - Maximum Power Dissipation: 0.76 W (at T = 85 °C)
  
- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Storage Temperature: -65 °C to +150 °C
  - Operating Temperature: -40 °C to +85 °C
  
- **Package Type:**
  - 12-Terminal Ceramic Leadless Chip Carrier (LCC), dimensions: 5.08 mm x 5.21 mm
  
- **Special Features:**
  - 15 dB of gain
  - 21.5 dBm of saturated output power at 25% Power Added Efficiency (PAE)
  - 50 Ohm matched input/output
  - DC blocking on RF I/Os
  - Screening to MIL-PRF-38535 (Class B or S) available
  
- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL1 (maximum peak reflow temperature of 250 °C)

**Typical Applications:**
The HMC441LH5 is commonly used in:
- Telecom Infrastructure
- Military Radio, Radar, and Electronic Countermeasures (ECM)
- Space Systems
- Test Instrumentation

This component serves as a linear gain block or driver amplifier, making it ideal for applications requiring reliable amplification in high-frequency environments. Its design allows for easy integration into RF systems without the need for external components, enhancing manufacturing efficiency and reliability.
