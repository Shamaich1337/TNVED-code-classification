### Component Summary: BCM856BS by Nexperia

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Collector-Emitter Voltage (V_CE): Max -65 V
  - Collector-Base Voltage (V_CBO): Max -80 V
  - Emitter-Base Voltage (V_EBO): Max -5 V

- **Current Ratings:**
  - Collector Current (I_C): Max -100 mA
  - Peak Collector Current (I_CM): Max -200 mA (single pulse, tp ≤ 1ms)

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Total Power Dissipation (P_tot):
    - BCM856BS (SOT363): Max 200 mW (per transistor), 300 mW (per device)
    - BCM856DS (SOT457): Max 250 mW (per transistor), 380 mW (per device)

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Junction Temperature (T_j): Max 150 °C
  - Ambient Temperature (T_amb): -55 °C to +150 °C
  - Storage Temperature (T_stg): -65 °C to +150 °C

- **Package Type:**
  - SOT363 (SC-88) for BCM856BS
  - SOT457 (SC-74) for BCM856DS

- **Special Features:**
  - PNP/PNP matched double transistors
  - Current gain and base-emitter voltage matching
  - Fully isolated internally
  - AEC-Q101 qualified for automotive applications

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - Not specified in the provided data.

#### Description:
The BCM856BS is a matched pair of PNP transistors housed in a small surface-mounted device (SMD) package. These transistors are designed for applications requiring high precision in current gain and base-emitter voltage, making them suitable for various analog applications. The internal isolation of the transistors allows for improved performance in differential configurations.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Current Mirror:** The BCM856BS can be used in current mirror circuits, which are essential in analog signal processing and biasing applications.
- **Differential Amplifier:** The matched characteristics of the transistors make them ideal for use in differential amplifiers, which are widely used in instrumentation and audio applications.
- **Automotive Electronics:** Given its AEC-Q101 qualification, the BCM856BS is suitable for automotive applications, ensuring reliability under harsh conditions.

This component is particularly useful in scenarios where precision and reliability are critical, such as in automotive and high-performance analog circuits.
