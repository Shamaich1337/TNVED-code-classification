**Manufacturer:** Vishay Siliconix  
**Product Code:** SiZ340BDT-T1-GE3  
**Type:** Dual N-Channel MOSFET

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Maximum Drain-Source Voltage (V_DS): 30 V
  - Maximum Gate-Source Voltage (V_GS): +20 V, -16 V

- **Current Ratings:**
  - Continuous Drain Current (I_D) at 25 °C: 
    - Channel 1: 36 A
    - Channel 2: 69.3 A
  - Pulsed Drain Current (I_DM): 
    - Channel 1: 100 A
    - Channel 2: 150 A

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Maximum Power Dissipation (P_D) at 25 °C: 
    - Channel 1: 16.7 W
    - Channel 2: 31 W

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Junction and Storage Temperature (T_J, T_stg): -55 °C to +150 °C

- **Package Type:**
  - PowerPAIR® 3 x 3 (Leadless package)

- **Special Features:**
  - TrenchFET® Gen IV technology for improved efficiency.
  - Optimized Q_gd/Q_gs ratio for enhanced switching characteristics.
  - 100% R_g and UIS tested for reliability.

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL according to JEDEC J-STD-020E: Not specified in the provided data.

#### Description:
The SiZ340BDT-T1-GE3 is a dual N-channel MOSFET designed for high-performance applications. It features a compact PowerPAIR® package, which allows for efficient thermal management and space-saving designs. The device is optimized for low on-resistance and fast switching capabilities, making it suitable for various power management applications.

#### Typical Applications:
- **CPU Core Power:** Used in power supply circuits for CPUs to ensure efficient power delivery.
- **Computer/Server Peripherals:** Ideal for managing power in various computer components and peripherals.
- **Point of Load (POL) Converters:** Suitable for DC-DC converters that require high efficiency and compact size.
- **Synchronous Buck Converters:** Commonly used in power conversion applications where voltage regulation is critical.
- **Telecom DC/DC Converters:** Effective in telecommunications equipment for power regulation and management.

This component is particularly valuable in applications where space is limited, and efficiency is paramount, such as in modern computing and telecommunications systems.
