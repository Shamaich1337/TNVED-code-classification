### Component Summary: MPXAZ4115A6U

**Key Specifications:**
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Supply Voltage (VS): 4.85 V to 5.35 V (typical 5.1 V)
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Supply Current (Io): 7.0 mA (max), 10 mA (typical)
- **Power Consumption:**
  - Power consumption is primarily determined by the supply current; at 5.1 V, it consumes approximately 35 mW (5.1 V * 7 mA).
- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Storage Temperature (Tstg): -40°C to +125°C
  - Operating Temperature (TA): -40°C to +125°C
- **Package Type:**
  - Small Outline Package (SOP), Case 482
- **Special Features:**
  - Temperature compensated from -40°C to +125°C
  - High humidity and automotive media resistant
  - 1.5% maximum error over 0°C to 85°C
  - Integrated on-chip bipolar op-amp circuitry and thin film resistor networks
- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL Level: Not specified in the provided text, but typically SOP packages are MSL 1 or 2.

**Description:**
The MPXAZ4115A6U is a piezoresistive silicon pressure sensor designed to provide a high-level analog output signal that is proportional to the applied pressure. It integrates advanced micromachining techniques and bipolar semiconductor processing to achieve accurate pressure measurements. The sensor is particularly noted for its small form factor and high reliability, making it suitable for various applications.

**Typical Applications:**
- **Aviation Altimeters:** Used to measure altitude by detecting changes in atmospheric pressure.
- **Industrial Controls:** Employed in various industrial automation systems for pressure monitoring and control.
- **Engine Control:** Utilized in automotive applications to monitor engine parameters and optimize performance.
- **Weather Stations:** Measures atmospheric pressure for weather forecasting and reporting.
- **Microprocessor or Microcontroller-Based Systems:** Ideal for integration into systems requiring precise pressure measurements.

This sensor is particularly advantageous in environments where high humidity and exposure to automotive media are concerns, ensuring reliable operation across a wide range of conditions.
