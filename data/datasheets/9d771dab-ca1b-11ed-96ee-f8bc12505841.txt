I | Page 8 of 11 INTERFACING TO OTHER DEVICES \nThe ADM803 /ADM809 /ADM810  series is designed to integrate \nwith as many devices as possible and, therefore, has a standard \noutput dependent on V CC. This enables the parts to be used in \nboth 3 V and 5 V , or any nominal voltage within the minimum \nand maximum specifications for V CC. This design simplifies \ninterfacing the ADM803 /ADM809 /ADM810  to other devices.  \nENSURING A VALID RESET OUTPUT DOWN TO \nVCC = 0 V  \nWhen V CC falls below 0.8 V , the ADM803 /ADM809  RESET  no \nlonger sinks current. A high impedance CMOS logic input \nconnected to RESET  may, therefore, drift to undetermined logic \nlevels. To eliminate this problem, a 100 kΩ resistor should be connected from RESET\n to ground.  \n \nFigure 12. Ensuring a Valid Reset Output Down to V CC = 0 V  \nBENEFITS OF AN ACCURATE RESET THRESHOLD  \nIn other microprocessors, tolerances in supply voltages lead  \nto an overall increase in reset tolerance levels due to the deterioration of the reset circuit’s power supply. In the ADM803 /ADM809 /ADM810 , the possibility of a malfunction \nduring a power failure is greatly reduced because the devices \ncan operate effectively even when there are large degradations of the supply voltages. Another advantage is the very accurate \ninternal voltage reference circuit of the ADM803 /ADM809 / \nADM810 . These benefits combine to produce an exceptionally \nreliable voltage monitor circuit.  \nINTERFACING TO MICROPROCESSORS WITH \nMULTIPLE INTERRUPTS  \nIn a number of cases, it is necessary to interface many interrupts \nfrom different devices (for example, thermal, altitude, and \nvelocity sensors).
