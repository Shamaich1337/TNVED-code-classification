### Component Summary: MR256A08BCYS35 (Everspin Technologies)

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Supply Voltage (VDD): 3.0V to 3.6V
  - Absolute Maximum Supply Voltage: -0.5V to 4.0V
  - Write Inhibit Voltage (WI): 2.5V to 3.0V

- **Current Ratings:**
  - Active Supply Current (Read): 25mA (typical)
  - Active Supply Current (Write): 55mA (typical)
  - Standby Current: 16mA (typical)

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Power Dissipation: 0.600W (maximum)

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Commercial: 0°C to +70°C
  - Industrial: -40°C to +85°C

- **Package Type:**
  - Available in 44-lead TSOP2 and 48-ball BGA packages.

- **Special Features:**
  - SRAM compatible timing with 35ns read/write cycle.
  - Native non-volatility with data retention for over 20 years.
  - Unlimited read/write endurance.
  - Low-voltage inhibit circuitry to protect data during power loss.
  - RoHS compliant.

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL-3 per JEDEC J-STD-020E.

#### Component Description:
The **MR256A08B** is a 32K x 8 magnetoresistive random access memory (MRAM) device, providing a non-volatile memory solution that combines the speed of SRAM with the data retention capabilities of Flash and EEPROM. It is organized as 262,144 bits, allowing for fast read and write operations with a cycle time of 35ns. The device is designed to retain data without power for over 20 years, making it suitable for applications requiring reliable data storage.

#### Typical Applications:
The MR256A08B is ideal for applications that require fast, non-volatile memory solutions, such as:
- **Data Logging:** Storing critical data in industrial applications where power loss may occur.
- **Embedded Systems:** Used in microcontrollers and other embedded devices that need to retain configuration settings and data.
- **Consumer Electronics:** Applications in devices that require quick access to stored data without the need for battery backup.
- **Automotive Systems:** Storing critical information in automotive electronics that must remain intact during power interruptions.

This component is particularly beneficial in scenarios where data integrity and speed are paramount, providing a robust alternative to traditional memory technologies.
