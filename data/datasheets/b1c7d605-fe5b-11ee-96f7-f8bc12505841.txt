### Component Summary: SMCJ28A Transient Voltage Suppressor Diode

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Working Peak Reverse Voltage (VRWM): 28V
  - Breakdown Voltage (VBR): 31.1V (min), 34.4V (max)
  
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Maximum Reverse Leakage Current (IR) at VRWM: 1 µA
  - Maximum Clamping Voltage at Ipp (10/1000 µs): 45.4V
  - Maximum Peak Pulse Current (Ipp) (10/1000 µs): 59.0A
  - Maximum Clamping Voltage at Ipp (8/20 µs): 33.1V
  - Maximum Peak Pulse Current (Ipp) (8/20 µs): 165.5A

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Minimum Peak Pulse Power Dissipation (TP = 1 ms): 1500 Watts
  - Steady State Power Dissipation at TL = 75 °C: 5.0 Watts

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Junction Temperature (TJ): -55 °C to +150 °C
  - Storage Temperature (TSTG): -55 °C to +150 °C

- **Package Type:**
  - SMC (DO-214AB) surface mount package

- **Special Features:**
  - Fast response time: < 1.0 picosecond for unidirectional devices and < 5.0 picoseconds for bidirectional devices.
  - RoHS compliant.
  - Conforms to JEDEC standards, easy to handle with standard pick and place equipment.

- **Moisture Sensitive Level:**
  - Level 1 (according to JEDEC J-STD-020E)

#### Description:
The SMCJ28A is a Transient Voltage Suppressor (TVS) diode designed to protect sensitive electronic components from voltage spikes caused by electrostatic discharge (ESD), electrical fast transients (EFT), and surge events. It is a unidirectional device that clamps excessive voltage to a safe level, thereby preventing damage to downstream components.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Surge Protection:** Used in power management circuits to protect against voltage spikes.
- **ESD Protection:** Commonly employed in portable communications, computing, and video equipment to safeguard sensitive components from ESD events.
- **Transient Voltage Suppression:** Ideal for applications requiring fast response to transient events, ensuring the reliability and longevity of electronic devices.

This component is particularly useful in environments where electronic devices are exposed to high levels of electrical noise or where transient events are common, making it essential for maintaining the integrity of electronic systems.
