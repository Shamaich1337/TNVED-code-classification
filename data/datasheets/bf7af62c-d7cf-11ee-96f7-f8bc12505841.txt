MAX220–MAX249 contain four sections: dual charge-\npump DC-DC voltage converters, RS-232 drivers, RS-232 \nreceivers, and receiver and transmitter enable control \ninputs.\nDual Charge-Pump Voltage Converter\nThe MAX220–MAX249 have two internal charge-pumps that convert +5V to ±10V (unloaded) for RS-232 driver \noperation. The first converter uses capacitor C1 to double \nthe +5V input to +10V on C3 at the V+ output. The second converter uses capacitor C2 to invert +10V to -10V on C4 \nat the V- output.\nA small amount of power may be drawn from the +10V \n(V+) and -10V (V-) outputs to power external circuitry (see \nthe Typical Operating Characteristics section), except on \nthe MAX225 and MAX245–MAX247, where these pins are not available. V+ and V- are not regulated, so the \noutput voltage drops with increasing load current. Do not load V+ and V- to a point that violates the minimum ±5V \nEIA/TIA-232E driver output voltage when sourcing current \nfrom V+ and V- to external circuitry.\nWhen using the shutdown feature in the MAX222, \nMAX225, MAX230, MAX235, MAX236, MAX240, \nMAX241, and MAX245–MAX249, avoid using V+ and Vto \npower external circuitry. When these parts are shut down, V- falls to 0V, and V+ falls to +5V. For applications where \na +10V external supply is applied to the V+ pin (instead of \nusing the internal charge pump to generate +10V), the C1 \ncapacitor must not be installed and the SHDN pin must be \nconnected to V\nCC. This is because V+ is internally con-\nnected to V CC in shutdown mode.\nRS-232 Drivers\nThe typical driver output voltage swing is ±8V when load -\ned with a nominal 5kΩ RS-232 receiver and V CC = +5V. \nOutput swing is guaranteed to meet the EIA/TIA-232E \nand V.28 specification, which calls for ±5V minimum driver \noutput levels under worst-case conditions. These include a minimum 3kΩ load, V\nCC = +4.5V, and maximum oper-\nating temperature. Unloaded driver output voltage ranges \nfrom (V+ -1.3V) to (V- +0.5V).\nInput thresholds are both TTL and CMOS compatible. The \ninputs of unused drivers can be left unconnected since \n400kΩ input pullup resistors to V CC are built in (except \nfor the MAX220). The pullup resistors force the outputs of \nunused drivers low because all drivers invert. The internal \ninput pullup resistors typically source 12μA, except in shutdown mode where the pullups are disabled. Driver \noutputs turn off and enter a high-impedance state—where leakage current is typically microamperes (maximum \n25μA)—when in shutdown mode, in three-state mode, or \nwhen device power is removed. Outputs can be driven \nto ±15V. The powersupply current typically drops to 8μA \nin shutdown mode. The MAX220 does not have pullup \nresistors to force the outputs of the unused drivers low. Connect unused inputs to GND or V\nCC.\nThe MAX239 has a receiver three-state control line, and the MAX223, MAX225, MAX235, MAX236, MAX240, and \nMAX241 have both a receiver three-state control line and \na low-power shutdown control. Table 2 shows the effects of the shutdown control and receiver three-state  control on the receiver outputs.\nThe receiver TTL/CMOS outputs are in a high-imped -\nance, three-state mode whenever the three-state enable \nline is high (for the MAX225/MAX235/MAX236/MAX239–\nMAX241), and are also high-impedance whenever the shutdown control line is high.\nWhen in low-power shutdown mode, the driver outputs \nare turned off and their leakage current is less than 1μA \nwith the driver output pulled to ground. The driver output leakage remains less than 1μA, even if the transmitter \noutput is backdriven between 0V and (V\nCC + 6V). Below \n-0.5V, the transmitter is diode clamped to ground with  1kΩ series impedance. The transmitter is also zener \nclamped to approximately V\nCC + 6V, with a series imped -\nance of 1kΩ.\nThe driver output slew rate is limited to less than 30V/\nμs as required by the EIA/TIA-232E and V.28 specifica -\ntions. ### Component Summary: MAX232AESE+T

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Supply Voltage (VCC): -0.3V to +6V
  - V+ (Positive Voltage): (VCC - 0.3V) to +14V
  - V- (Negative Voltage): +0.3V to -14V
  - Output Voltage Swing: ±5V to ±15V (depending on the specific variant)
  
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Supply Current (VCC = +5V): Typically 10 mA (no load)
  - Shutdown Supply Current: 0.1 µA (typical)

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Power Dissipation: Up to 842 mW (for 16-Pin Plastic DIP at 70°C)

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Commercial: 0°C to +70°C
  - Industrial: -40°C to +85°C
  - Military: -55°C to +125°C

- **Package Type:**
  - 16-Pin Narrow SO (Surface Mount)

- **Special Features:**
  - Integrated charge pump circuitry for generating ±10V from a single +5V supply.
  - Low-power shutdown mode (5 µW).
  - No external components required for operation.
  - Built-in capacitors for certain variants (e.g., MAX233).

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL Level: 1 (per JEDEC J-STD-020E)

#### Description:
The **MAX232AESE+T** is a dual RS-232 driver/receiver designed for use in EIA/TIA-232E and V.28/V.24 communications interfaces. It is particularly useful in applications where a ±12V supply is not available, as it can operate from a single +5V supply. The device includes integrated charge pump circuitry that generates the necessary voltage levels for RS-232 communication, eliminating the need for external components and saving board space.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Interface Translation:** Converting TTL/CMOS logic levels to RS-232 levels for communication with serial devices.
- **Multidrop RS-232 Networks:** Connecting multiple devices in a network configuration using RS-232 signaling.
- **Portable Diagnostics Equipment:** Used in handheld devices that require serial communication without the need for bulky power supplies.
- **Data Acquisition Systems:** Facilitating communication between sensors and microcontrollers in data logging applications.

The MAX232AESE+T is ideal for applications requiring reliable serial communication with minimal external components, making it a popular choice in embedded systems and portable devices.
