### Component Summary: TXB0106RGYR

**Key Characteristics and Specifications:**

- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - VCCA (A-port supply voltage): 1.2V to 3.6V
  - VCCB (B-port supply voltage): 1.65V to 5.5V

- **Current Ratings:**
  - Continuous output current: ±50 mA
  - Continuous current through VCCA, VCCB, or GND: ±100 mA

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Maximum supply current (ICC): 4 µA (typical)

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - -40°C to 85°C

- **Package Type:**
  - VQFN (16 pins) with dimensions 4.00 mm x 3.50 mm

- **Special Features:**
  - Bidirectional level-shifting and voltage translation
  - Auto-direction sensing
  - ±15 kV ESD protection (Human Body Model)
  - Low power consumption with Ioff support for partial power-down mode
  - OE (Output Enable) input for high-impedance state

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - Level 2, according to JEDEC J-STD-020E

**Description:**
The TXB0106 is a 6-bit bidirectional level-shifting and voltage translator designed to facilitate communication between devices operating at different voltage levels. It features two separate power supply rails, allowing for universal low-voltage bidirectional translation between various voltage nodes. The A-port can accept input voltages from 1.2V to 3.6V, while the B-port can handle voltages from 1.65V to 5.5V. The device is particularly useful in applications where logic levels need to be translated without the need for a direction-control signal.

**Typical Applications:**
The TXB0106 is commonly used in:
- Interfacing between microcontrollers and sensors operating at different voltage levels.
- Level translation in mobile devices such as smartphones, tablets, and headsets.
- Communication between components in desktop PCs and other electronic systems where different voltage levels are present.
- Any application requiring bidirectional data transfer between devices with incompatible voltage levels, such as I2C or SPI communication.

This component is ideal for applications that require reliable and efficient voltage translation while minimizing power consumption and ensuring signal integrity.
