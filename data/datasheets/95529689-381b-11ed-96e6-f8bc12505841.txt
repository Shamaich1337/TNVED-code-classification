#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - **AC RMS Voltage (VRMS):** 275 V
  - **DC Voltage (VVDC):** 350 V
  - **Maximum Voltage (Vimax, 8/20 µs):** 1750 V
- **Current Ratings:**
  - **Nominal Discharge Current (In, according to UL 1449):** 500 A (15 times)
  - **Maximum Surge Current (JPmax, 2 ms):** 27.5 A
- **Power Consumption:**
  - **Maximum Average Power Dissipation (Wmax):** 0.25 W
- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - **Operating Temperature:** -40 °C to +105 °C
  - **Storage Temperature:** -40 °C to +125 °C
- **Package Type:**
  - **Type:** Leaded Varistor
  - **Dimensions:** Diameter varies based on voltage rating (typically 5 mm to 20 mm)
- **Special Features:**
  - High-energy AdvanceD series
  - High surge current ratings up to 10 kA
  - High energy ratings up to 440 J
  - Flame-retardant epoxy resin coating (UL 94 V-0)
- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):** Not specified in the provided data.

#### Description:
The B72207S2271K101 is a leaded metal oxide varistor (MOV) from TDK's EPCOS AdvanceD series. Varistors are voltage-dependent resistors that protect circuits from voltage spikes by clamping excess voltage and diverting surge currents. This specific model is designed to handle high energy and surge currents, making it suitable for applications requiring robust transient voltage suppression.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Surge Protection:** Used in power supply circuits to protect sensitive electronic components from voltage spikes caused by lightning strikes, switching surges, or other transient events.
- **Power Management:** Commonly found in power strips, surge protectors, and power supply units to enhance the reliability and longevity of electronic devices.
- **Signal Processing:** Employed in communication lines to prevent overvoltage conditions that could damage signal processing equipment.
- **Consumer Electronics:** Integrated into various consumer electronics to safeguard against electrical disturbances.

This varistor is particularly suitable for applications where high surge current handling and energy absorption are critical, ensuring the protection of downstream components in various electronic systems.
