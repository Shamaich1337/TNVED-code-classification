The capacitor type\ncan be ceramic, tantalum or aluminum electrolytic. The\nesr range on the output capacitor can range from 0 \uf057 to\n2.0\uf057.\nThe output capacitor range for ceramic capacitors is\n1 µF to 22 µF. ### Component Summary: MCP1702T-3002E/CB

**Key Characteristics and Specifications:**

- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Input Operating Voltage Range: 2.7V to 13.2V
  - Standard Output Voltage: 3.0V (specific to MCP1702T-3002E/CB)
  - Output Voltage Range: 1.2V to 5.5V in 0.1V increments

- **Current Ratings:**
  - Maximum Output Current: 250 mA for output voltages ≥ 2.5V
  - Maximum Output Current: 200 mA for output voltages < 2.5V

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Quiescent Current: 2.0 µA (typical)

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Junction Temperature Range: -40°C to +125°C
  - Storage Temperature Range: -65°C to +150°C

- **Package Type:**
  - SOT-23A (3-lead)

- **Special Features:**
  - Low Dropout Voltage: 625 mV typical at 250 mA (for VOUT = 2.8V)
  - Output Voltage Tolerance: ±0.4% typical
  - Short-Circuit Protection
  - Overtemperature Protection
  - Stable with 1.0 µF to 22 µF output capacitor

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL Level: 1 (JEDEC J-STD-020E)

**Description:**
The MCP1702 is a low dropout (LDO) voltage regulator designed to provide a stable output voltage while consuming minimal quiescent current. It is capable of delivering up to 250 mA of output current, making it suitable for battery-powered applications. The device operates over a wide input voltage range, making it versatile for various power supply configurations.

**Typical Applications:**
The MCP1702 is commonly used in:
- Battery-powered devices (e.g., smoke detectors, CO2 detectors, PDAs, digital cameras)
- Smart battery packs
- Microcontroller power supplies
- Solar-powered instruments
- Low quiescent current voltage references
- Consumer products and battery-powered data loggers

This component is particularly advantageous in applications where low power consumption is critical, such as in portable electronics and remote sensors.
