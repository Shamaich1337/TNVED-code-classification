The first converter uses capacitor C1 to dou-ble the +5V input to +10V on C3 at the V+ output. Thesecond converter uses capacitor C2 to invert +10V to -10V on C4 at the V- output. \nA small amount of power may be drawn from the +10V\n(V+) and -10V (V-) outputs to power external circuitry(see the Typical Operating Characteristics section),\nexcept on the MAX225 and MAX245–MAX247, wherethese pins are not available. V+ and V- are not regulated,so the output voltage drops with increasing load current.Do not load V+ and V- to a point that violates the mini-mum ±5V EIA/TIA-232E driver output voltage whensourcing current from V+ and V- to external circuitry. \nWhen using the shutdown feature in the MAX222,\nMAX225, MAX230, MAX235, MAX236, MAX240,MAX241, and MAX245–MAX249, avoid using V+ and V-to power external circuitry. When these parts are shutdown, V- falls to 0V, and V+ falls to +5V. For applica-tions where a +10V external supply is applied to the V+pin (instead of using the internal charge pump to gen-erate +10V), the C1 capacitor must not be installed andthe SHDN pin must be tied to V\nCC. This is because V+\nis internally connected to V CCin shutdown mode. \nRS-232 Drivers\nThe typical driver output voltage swing is ±8V whenloaded with a nominal 5k ΩRS-232 receiver and V\nCC=\n+5V. Output swing is guaranteed to meet the EIA/TIA-232E and V.28 specification, which calls for ±5V mini-mum driver output levels under worst-case conditions.These include a minimum 3k Ωload, V\nCC= +4.5V, and\nmaximum operating temperature. Unloaded driver out-put voltage ranges from (V+ -1.3V) to (V- +0.5V). \nInput thresholds are both TTL and CMOS compatible.\nThe inputs of unused drivers can be left unconnectedsince 400k Ωinput pullup resistors to V\nCCare built in\n(except for the MAX220). The pullup resistors force theoutputs of unused drivers low because all drivers invert.The internal input pullup resistors typically source 12µA,except in shutdown mode where the pullups are dis-abled. Driver outputs turn off and enter a high-imped-ance state—where leakage current is typicallymicroamperes (maximum 25µA)—when in shutdownmode, in three-state mode, or when device power is\nremoved. Outputs can be driven to ±15V. The power-supply current typically drops to 8µA in shutdown mode.The MAX220 does not have pullup resistors to force theoutputs of the unused drivers low. Connect unusedinputs to GND or V\nCC.\nThe MAX239 has a receiver three-state control line, and\nthe MAX223, MAX225, MAX235, MAX236, MAX240,and MAX241 have both a receiver three-state controlline and a low-power shutdown control. ### Component Summary: MAX232AEPE+

#### Key Characteristics and Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Supply Voltage (VCC): -0.3V to +6V
  - V+ (Output): (VCC - 0.3V) to +14V
  - V- (Output): +0.3V to -14V
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Supply Current (Normal Operation): Typically 15 µA
  - Shutdown Supply Current: Typically 1 µA
- **Power Consumption:**
  - Power Dissipation: 842 mW (at 70°C for 16-Pin Plastic DIP)
- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - -40°C to +85°C
- **Package Type:**
  - 16-Pin Plastic DIP
- **Special Features:**
  - Integrated charge pump for generating ±10V from a +5V supply.
  - Low-power shutdown mode reduces power consumption to less than 5 µW.
  - ESD protection up to ±15kV.
- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL Level: 1 (JEDEC J-STD-020E)

#### Description:
The MAX232AEPE+ is a dual RS-232 line driver/receiver designed for communication interfaces that comply with EIA/TIA-232E standards. It is particularly useful in applications where ±12V is not available, making it ideal for battery-powered systems. The device features a built-in charge pump that generates the necessary voltage levels for RS-232 communication, allowing it to operate from a single +5V supply.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Portable Computers:** Used for serial communication in laptops and other mobile devices.
- **Low-Power Modems:** Facilitates communication in low-power modem applications.
- **Interface Translation:** Converts signals between different voltage levels for compatibility.
- **Battery-Powered RS-232 Systems:** Ideal for systems that require low power consumption.
- **Multidrop RS-232 Networks:** Supports multiple devices on a single communication line.

The MAX232AEPE+ is a versatile and efficient solution for integrating RS-232 communication in modern electronic designs, particularly where space and power efficiency are critical.
