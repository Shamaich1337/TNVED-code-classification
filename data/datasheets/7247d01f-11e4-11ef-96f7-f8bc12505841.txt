TheMAX548A/MAX549A are dual DACs, and the MAX550Ais a single DAC. Each DAC consists of an R-2R laddernetwork that converts 8-bit digital inputs into equivalentanalog output voltages in proportion to the applied ref -\nerence voltage (Figure 1).\nThe DACs feature double-buffered inputs and\nunbuffered outputs. The MAX549A/MAX550A requirean external reference. The MAX548A’s reference inputsare internally connected to V\nDD. The power-supply\nrange is from +2.5V to +5.5V.\nReference Input\nThe voltage applied at REF (V DDfor the MAX548A) sets\nthe full-scale output for all the DACs and may rangefrom +2.5V to V\nDD. The REF input resistance is code\ndependent, with the lowest value occurring with code01010101 (55 hex). To minimize INL errors, the refer -\nence voltage source should have less than 3 Ωoutput\nimpedance.\nDAC Output\nThe MAX548A/MAX549A/MAX550A contain DACs withunbuffered outputs; each output connects directly to anR-2R ladder. Typical output impedance is 33.3k Ω. This\nconfiguration minimizes power consumption andreduces offset errors. For highest accuracy, apply highresistive loads (1M Ωand up). Lower resistive loads can\nbe driven, but output loading increases full-scale error.The magnitude of the expected error is the ratio of the\nDAC output resistance to the DC load resistance at theoutput.\nTypically, an energy pulse is coupled into the DAC out -\nput on CS’s rising edge. Since each DAC output is\nunbuffered, connecting a small capacitor (200pF to1000pF) from the output to ground creates a lowpassfilter that effectively suppresses the pulse for sensitiveapplications (see \nTypical Operating Characteristics ).\nShutdown Mode\nWhen the MAX548A/MAX549A/MAX550A are in shut -\ndown mode, the R-2R ladder disconnects from the refer -\nence source. The MAX549A/MAX550A supply currentdoes not change, but the REF input current decreases toless than 1 µA. This allows the externally applied system\nreference to remain active with minimal power consumption. The MAX548A supply current alsodecreases to less than 1 µA in shutdown mode. When the\nMAX548A/MAX549A/MAX550A exit shutdown mode,recovery time is equivalent to the DAC’s settling time.\nSerial Interface\nThe serial interface is SPI/QSPI and Microwire compati -\nble. An active-low chip select ( CS) enables the input\nshift register to receive data from the serial input (DIN).Data is clocked into the shift register on the rising edgeof the serial-clock signal (SCLK). The clock frequencycan be as high as 10MHz.\nTransmit data MSB first in one 16-bit word or two 8-bit\nbytes. The write cycle can be segmented to allow two8-bit-wide transfers when CS remains low. ### Component Summary: MAX549ACUA

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings**: Operates from a single supply voltage of +2.5V to +5.5V.
- **Current Ratings**: 
  - Typical operating current: 75 µA per DAC at VDD = 2.5V.
  - Shutdown current: Less than 1 µA.
- **Power Consumption**: 
  - 150 µA (MAX548A/MAX549A, VREF = +2.5V).
  - 75 µA (MAX550A, VREF = +2.5V).
- **Operating Temperature Range**: 0°C to +70°C.
- **Package Type**: Available in 8-pin µMAX and DIP packages.
- **Special Features**: 
  - ±1 LSB Total Unadjusted Error (TUE).
  - Power-On Reset clears all registers to zero.
  - Independent shutdown of DACs (for MAX548A/MAX549A).
  - 10 MHz, 3-wire serial interface compatible with SPI/QSPI and Microwire.
- **Moisture Sensitive Level**: Not specified in the provided text.

#### Description:
The MAX549ACUA is a dual 8-bit voltage-output digital-to-analog converter (DAC) from Maxim Integrated. It is designed to convert digital signals into corresponding analog voltages, making it suitable for various applications that require precise voltage outputs. The device features a low power consumption profile, making it ideal for battery-powered and portable applications.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Battery-Powered Systems**: The low power consumption allows for extended battery life.
- **VCXO Control**: Used in voltage-controlled crystal oscillators for frequency control.
- **Comparator-Level Settings**: Provides adjustable reference voltages for comparators.
- **GaAs Amplifier Bias Control**: Used to set bias levels in Gallium Arsenide amplifiers.
- **Digital Gain and Offset Control**: Adjusts gain and offset in digital signal processing applications.

This component is particularly useful in systems where space and power efficiency are critical, such as in portable electronics, instrumentation, and communication devices.
