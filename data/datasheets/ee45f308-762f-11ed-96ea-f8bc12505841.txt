Incorporated7Detailed Description\n7.1 Overview\nTheLM3481 device uses afixed frequency, Pulse Width Modulated (PWM), current mode control architecture. In\natypical application circuit, thepeak current through theexternal MOSFET issensed through anexternal sense\nresistor. The voltage across thisresistor isfedintotheISENpin.This voltage isthen level shifted andfedintothe\npositive input ofthePWM comparator. The output voltage isalso sensed through anexternal feedback resistor\ndivider network and fedintotheerror amplifier (EA) negative input (feedback pin,FB). The output oftheerror\namplifier (COMP pin) isadded totheslope compensation ramp and fedinto thenegative input ofthePWM\ncomparator.\nAtthestart ofany switching cycle, theoscillator sets theRSlatch using theSET/Blank-out and switch logic\nblocks. This forces ahigh signal ontheDRpin(gate oftheexternal MOSFET) andtheexternal MOSFET turns\non.When thevoltage onthepositive input ofthePWM comparator exceeds thenegative input, theRSlatch is\nreset andtheexternal MOSFET turns off.\nThe voltage sensed across thesense resistor generally contains spurious noise spikes, asshown inFigure 18.\nThese spikes canforce thePWM comparator toreset theRSlatch prematurely. Toprevent these spikes from\nresetting thelatch, ablank-out circuit inside theICprevents thePWM comparator from resetting thelatch fora\nshort duration after thelatch isset.This duration, called theblank-out time, istypically 250nsandisspecified as\ntmin(on) intheElectrical Characteristics section.\nUnder extremely light load orno-load conditions, theenergy delivered totheoutput capacitor when theexternal\nMOSFET isonduring theblank-out time ismore than what isdelivered totheload. Anovervoltage comparator\ninside theLM3481 prevents theoutput voltage from rising under these conditions bysensing thefeedback (FB\npin)voltage andresetting theRSlatch. ### Component Summary: LM3481MMX/NOPB

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Input Voltage (VIN): 2.97 V to 48 V
  - Maximum VIN Pin Voltage: 50 V
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Peak Driver Output Current: 1 A
  - Output Current (IOUT): Up to 1.8 A
- **Power Consumption:**
  - Shutdown Current: 10 µA (typical)
  - Supply Current (switching): 3.7 mA (typical)
- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Junction Temperature: -40°C to +125°C
- **Package Type:**
  - 10-Pin VSSOP (DGS)
- **Special Features:**
  - Internal soft-start
  - Current mode operation with cycle-by-cycle current limiting
  - Adjustable undervoltage lockout (UVLO) with hysteresis
  - Thermal shutdown and short-circuit protection
  - Frequency adjustable from 100 kHz to 1 MHz
  - Pulse skipping at light loads
- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL1, 260°C (per JEDEC J-STD-020E)

#### Description:
The **LM3481** is a high-efficiency controller designed for boost, SEPIC, and flyback DC-DC converters. It operates as a low-side N-channel MOSFET controller, allowing for high-frequency operation which reduces the overall solution size. The device features a current mode control architecture that enhances bandwidth and transient response while providing cycle-by-cycle current limiting. 

#### Typical Applications:
- **Automotive Applications:**
  - Start-stop systems
  - Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS)
- **Portable Devices:**
  - Battery-powered Bluetooth audio systems
  - Notebooks, PDAs, and digital cameras
- **Power Supplies:**
  - Offline power supplies
  - Set-top boxes
- **Audio Amplifiers:**
  - Boost converters for audio applications

The LM3481 is particularly suited for applications requiring efficient power management and compact design, making it ideal for both consumer electronics and automotive systems.
