Data Sheet 7 Revision 3.1, 2014-03-25\n   \n1 Features\n• 24 GHz transceiver MMIC\n• Fully integrated low phase noise VCO\n• Switchable prescaler with 1.5 GHz and 23 kHz output• On chip power and temperature sensors\n• Gilbert based homodyne quadrature receiver\n• Single ended RF and LO terminals• Low noise figure NF\nSSB: 12 dB\n• High conversion gain: 26 dB\n• High 1 dB input compression point: -12 dBm• Single supply voltage 3.3 V• Power consumption 500 mW in continuous operating mode\n• 200 GHz bipolar SiGe:C technology b7hf200\n• Fully ESD protected device• VQFN-32-9 leadless plastic package incl. LTI feature\n• Pb-free (RoHS compliant) package\nDescription\nThe BGT24MTR11 is a Silicon Germaniu m MMIC for signal  generation and reception, operating from 24.0 up to\n26.0 GHz. It is based on a 24 GHz fundamental voltage controlled oscillator.  Switchable frequen cy prescalers are\nincluded with output frequencies of 1.5 GHz and 23 kHz. The main RF output delivers typ. 11dBm signal power to\nfeed an antenna and an aux iliary LO output is availabl e to provide LO signal to separate receiver  components.\nA LNA provides low noise figure and a RC polyphase filter  (PPF) is used for LO quadrature phase generation of\nthe homodyne quadrature downconversion mixer. Output  power sensors as well as a temperature sensor are\nimplemented for monitoring purposes. The device is cont rolled via SPI and is manufactured in a 0.18µm SiGe:C\ntechnology offering a cutoff frequency of 200 GHz. The MMIC is packaged in a 32 pin leadless RoHs compliant\nVQFN package.\n\n  BGT24MTR11\nSilicon Germanium 24 GHz Transceiver MMIC\nFeatures\n Data Sheet 8 Revision 3.1, 2014-03-25\n   \nFigure 1 BGT24MTR11 Block DiagramBGT24MTR11_Chip_BID.vsdSPI\nBuffer/16TXPA/655363\nTX Power\nSensor\nTemp.\nSensor\nPPF* LNA\nIFIIFQ\n90°\n0°RFINLO\nBufferMPA\nIFQX\nIFIX\n* Poly Phase FilterFINE\nCOARSEQ1  Q1N Q2 SI  CS  CLK\nLO POWER \nSENSORAMUX2\n2\nTEMPANA\nVCCTEMP\nTXOFFTXX\nLO\nBGT24MTR11\nSilicon Germanium 24 GHz Transceiver MMIC\nElectrical Characteristics   \n Data Sheet 9 Revision 3.1, 2014-03-25\n  2 Electrical Characteristics\n2.1 Absolute Maximum Ratings\nTA= -40 °C to 105 °C; all voltages with respect to grou nd, positive current flowing into pin (unless otherwise\nspecified)1)\n1) Not subject to production test, specified by designTable 1 Absolute Maximum Ratings\nParameter Symbol Values Unit Note / Test Condition\nMin. ### Component Summary: BGT24MTR11E6327

**Manufacturer:** Infineon Technologies  
**Product Code:** BGT24MTR11  
**Type:** Silicon Germanium 24 GHz Transceiver MMIC

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Supply Voltage (VCC): 3.135 V to 3.465 V
  - Maximum Supply Voltage: 3.6 V
  - DC Voltage at RF Pins: 0 V
  - DC Voltage at Pins (Various): -0.3 V to 3.6 V

- **Current Ratings:**
  - Supply Current (ICC): 110 mA (typical), 150 mA (max)
  - DC Current into Pins (Various): Up to 3.5 mA
  - DC Current into TEMP Pin: -1 mA to 1.5 mA

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Continuous Operating Mode: 500 mW
  - Total Power Dissipation: 750 mW (with BIST deactivated)

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Ambient Temperature (TA): -40 °C to 105 °C
  - Junction Temperature (TJ): -40 °C to 150 °C

- **Package Type:**
  - VQFN32-9 (32-pin leadless plastic package)

- **Special Features:**
  - Fully integrated low phase noise VCO
  - On-chip power and temperature sensors
  - Gilbert based homodyne quadrature receiver
  - ESD protection (HBM: 1 kV, CDM: 500 V)
  - RoHS compliant (Pb-free)

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL-3 according to JEDEC J-STD-020E

#### Description:
The BGT24MTR11 is a Silicon Germanium (SiGe) MMIC designed for signal generation and reception in the 24 GHz frequency range. It features a fully integrated voltage-controlled oscillator (VCO) and includes switchable prescalers for output frequencies of 1.5 GHz and 23 kHz. The device is capable of delivering a typical output power of 11 dBm to an antenna and provides auxiliary local oscillator (LO) signals for external components. The architecture includes a low-noise amplifier (LNA) and a polyphase filter for quadrature phase generation, making it suitable for homodyne downconversion.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Wireless Communication:** Used in various wireless communication systems operating in the 24 GHz ISM band, including radar and automotive applications.
- **Sensor Systems:** Ideal for applications requiring precise signal processing and monitoring, such as temperature and power detection.
- **RF Signal Processing:** Suitable for RF signal generation and reception in short-range communication systems, including automotive and industrial sensors.

This component is particularly valuable in applications where low noise and high conversion gain are critical, making it a robust choice for modern RF transceiver designs.
