### Component Summary: SGM2036-ADJYN5G/TR

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Input Voltage Range: 1.6V to 5.5V
  - Adjustable Output Voltage: 0.8V to 5.0V

- **Current Ratings:**
  - Maximum Output Current: 300mA
  - Current Limit: 360mA to 560mA

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Supply Current (No Load): 20µA to 26µA
  - Shutdown Supply Current: 0.01µA to 1µA

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - -40°C to +85°C

- **Package Type:**
  - Available in SOT-23-5, SC70-5, and UTDFN-1×1-4L packages

- **Special Features:**
  - Low dropout voltage
  - Thermal overload protection
  - Logic-controlled shutdown mode (10nA)
  - Auto-discharge function
  - High PSRR (Power Supply Rejection Ratio)
  - Low output noise

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL Level: 1 (per JEDEC J-STD-020E)

#### Description:
The SGM2036-ADJYN5G/TR is a low-power, low-dropout (LDO) linear voltage regulator designed to provide a stable output voltage for various electronic applications. It operates efficiently within a wide input voltage range and is capable of delivering up to 300mA of output current. The adjustable output feature allows for flexibility in voltage settings, making it suitable for a variety of low-voltage applications.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Portable/Battery-Powered Equipment:** Ideal for devices that require efficient power management to prolong battery life.
- **Cellular and Cordless Telephones:** Provides a stable voltage supply for communication devices.
- **PCMCIA Cards and Modems:** Ensures reliable operation in data communication devices.
- **MP3 Players and Handheld Instruments:** Supports low-power audio and measurement devices.
- **Palmtop Computers and Electronic Planners:** Suitable for compact computing devices that require low power consumption.

The SGM2036 series is particularly beneficial in applications where low noise and high power supply rejection are critical, such as RF (Radio Frequency) applications. Its features make it an excellent choice for designers looking to enhance the performance and efficiency of their electronic systems.
