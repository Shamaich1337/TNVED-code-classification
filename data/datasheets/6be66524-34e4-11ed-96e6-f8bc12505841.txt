  - Repetitive Peak Reverse Voltage (VRRM): 1600 V
  - Non-Repetitive Peak Off-State Voltage (VDSM): 1700 V (VDRM + 100 V)
  - Non-Repetitive Peak Reverse Voltage (VRSM): 1700 V (VRRM + 100 V)

  - Non-Repetitive Surge Peak On-State Current (ITSM): 400 A
  - I2t Value for Fusing (tp=10ms): 880 A²s

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Average Gate Power Dissipation (PGM): 1 W
  - Peak Gate Power (PG(AV)): 10 W

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Storage Junction Temperature Range (TJ): -40 to +125 °C
  - Operating Junction Temperature Range (Tstg): -40 to +150 °C

- **Package Type:**
  - TO-3P (Insulated)

- **Special Features:**
  - High dv/dt capability with strong resistance to electromagnetic interference.
  - Rated insulation voltage of 2500 VRMS from all three terminals to external heatsink.
  - Designed for high commutation performance, especially recommended for inductive loads.

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (JEDEC J-STD-020E):**
  - Not specified in the provided data.

#### Component Description:
The SST41Z-1600BW is a TRIAC (Triode for Alternating Current) designed for controlling AC power. It is part of the SST41 series of TRIACs, which are known for their high current handling capabilities and robust performance in various applications. The component features a high voltage rating of up to 1600 V, making it suitable for demanding environments.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Power Management:** Used in circuits that require control of AC loads, such as motor control, lighting control, and heating applications.
- **Signal Processing:** Employed in phase control applications where precise control of AC signals is necessary.
- **Inductive Loads:** Particularly effective in applications involving inductive loads due to its high commutation performance.
- **Industrial Automation:** Commonly used in industrial equipment for controlling power to various devices.

This component is ideal for applications requiring reliable switching and control of high voltage and current AC loads, making it a versatile choice in power electronics.
