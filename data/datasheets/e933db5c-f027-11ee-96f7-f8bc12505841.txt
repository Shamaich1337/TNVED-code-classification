The RS-232\nstandard requires transmitters that will deliver ±5 V minimum\non the transmission channel and receivers that can accept signal\nlevels down to ±3 V. The parts achieve this by integrating step-\nup voltage converters and level-shifting t ransmitters and rec eivers\nonto the same chip. CMOS technology is used to keep thepower dissipation to an absolute minimum. All devices con-\ntain an internal charge pump voltage doubler and a voltage\ninverter that generates ±10 V from the 5 V input. Four exter-\nnal 0.1 µF capacitors are required for the internal charge pump\nvoltage converter.\nThe ADM222/ADM232A/ADM242 is a modification, en hance-\nment and improvement to the AD230-AD241 family and\nderivatives thereof. It is essentially plug-in-compatible and does\nnot have materially different applications.\nCIRCUIT DESCRIPTION\nThe internal circuitry consists of four main sections. These are:\nCharge Pump Voltage Converter\nTTL/CMOS to RS-232 TransmittersRS-232 to TTL/CMOS Receivers\nEnable and Shutdown Functions.\nCharge Pump DC-DC Voltage Converter\nThe Charge Pump Voltage converter consists of an oscillator\nand a switching matrix. The converter generates a ±10 V supply\nfrom the input 5 V level. This is done in two stages using aswitched capacitor technique. The 5 V input supply is doubledto 10 V using capacitor C1 as the charge storage element. The\n–10 V level is also generated from the input 5 V supply using C1\nand C2 as the storage elements.\nCapacitors C3 and C4 are used to reduce the output ripple.\nTheir values are not critical and can be reduced if higher levels\nof ripple are acceptable. The charge pump capacitors C1 and\nC2 may also be reduced at the expense of higher output imped-ance on the V+ and V– supplies.\nThe V+ and V– su pplies may also be used to power external\ncircuitry if the current req uirements are small. Please refer to\nthe typical performance characteristics which shows the V+, V–\noutput voltage vs. current.\nIn the shutdown mode the charge pump is disabled and V+\ndecays to V\nCC while V– decays to 0 V.\nTransmitter (Driver) Section\nThe Drivers convert TTL/CMOS input levels into RS-232\noutput levels. With V CC = 5 V and driving a typical RS-232\nload, the output voltage swing is ±9 V. Even under worst-case\nconditions the drivers are guaranteed to meet the ±5 V RS-232\nminimum requirement.The input threshold levels are both TTL- and CMOS-compat-ible with the switching threshold set at V\nCC/4. With a nominal\nVCC = 5 V, the switching threshold is 1.25 V typical. Unused\ninputs may be left unconnected, as an internal 400 k Ω pull-up\nresistor pulls them high forcing the outputs into a low state.\nAs required by the RS-232 standard, the slew rate is limited to\nless than 30 V/ µs without the need for an external slew limiting\ncapacitor, and the output impedance in the power-off state isgreater than 300 Ω.\nReceiver Section\nThe receivers are inverting level-shifters that accept RS-232\ninput levels ( ±3 V to ±15 V) and translate them into 5 V TTL/\nCMOS levels. The inputs have internal 5 k Ω pull-down resistors\nto ground and are also protected against overvoltages of up to±30 V. The guaranteed switching thresholds are 0.8 V mini-\nmum and 2.4 V maximum, which are well within the ±3 V\nRS-232 requirement. The low level threshold is deliberatelypositive as it ensures that an unconnected input will be inter-\npreted as a low level.\nThe receivers have Schmitt trigger input with a hysteresis level\nof 0.5 V. This ensures error-free reception for both noisy inputs\nand for inputs with slow transition times\nEnable and Shutdown Functions\nOn the ADM222, both receivers are fully disabled during\nshutdown.\nOn the ADM242, both receivers continue to operate normally.\nThis function is useful for monitoring activity so that when it\noccurs, the device can be taken out of the shutdown mode.\nThe ADM242 also contains a receiver enable function ( EN)\nwhich can be used to fully disable the receivers, independent\nof SHDN .\nAPPLICATIONS INFORMATION\nA selection of typical operating circuits is shown in TPCs 1–6\nand Figure 13.\n1090100\n0%\n5VA1\n5/H9262s2.0V\n5V\nFigure 13. ### Component Summary: ADM232AARNZ-REEL

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Supply Voltage (VCC): 5 V ± 10%
  - Output Voltage Swing: ±5 V to ±9 V
  - RS-232 Input Voltage Range: -30 V to +30 V

- **Current Ratings:**
  - Power Supply Current: 4 mA (typical), 8 mA (maximum) under no load
  - Shutdown Power Supply Current: 0.1 µA (typical)

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Quiescent Power Supply Current: < 5 mA
  - Power Dissipation: 400 mW (derate 7 mW/°C above 70 °C)

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Industrial: -40 °C to +85 °C
  - Storage: -65 °C to +150 °C

- **Package Type:**
  - 16-lead Standard Small Outline Package (SOIC_N)

- **Special Features:**
  - Integrated charge pump for generating ±10 V from a single 5 V supply
  - Two RS-232 drivers and two receivers
  - Pin-compatible with MAX222/MAX232A/MAX242
  - Internal pull-up and pull-down resistors for TTL/CMOS inputs and RS-232 inputs, respectively

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL Level: 1 (JEDEC J-STD-020E)

#### Description:
The **ADM232AARNZ-REEL** is a high-speed RS-232 line driver/receiver designed for communication applications. It operates from a single 5 V power supply and utilizes an integrated charge pump to generate the necessary bipolar voltage levels for RS-232 communication. The device supports transmission rates of up to 200 kB/s and is fabricated using BiCMOS technology, which combines low power CMOS with high-speed bipolar circuitry.

#### Typical Applications:
The ADM232A is commonly used in various applications, including:
- **Computers and Peripherals:** For serial communication between devices.
- **Modems:** Facilitating data transmission over telephone lines.
- **Printers:** Enabling communication between computers and printers.
- **Instruments:** Used in test and measurement equipment for data transfer.

This component is ideal for applications requiring reliable RS-232 communication with minimal power consumption, making it suitable for battery-operated devices and systems where power efficiency is critical.
