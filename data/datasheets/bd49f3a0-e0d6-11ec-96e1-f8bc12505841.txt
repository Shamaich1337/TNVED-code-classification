### Component Summary: TLP281 (GB-TP,F) by Toshiba

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Collector-Emitter Voltage (V_CE): 80 V (max)
  - Emitter-Collector Voltage (V_EC): 7 V (max)
  - Isolation Voltage: 2500 Vrms (min)

- **Current Ratings:**
  - LED Forward Current (I_F): 50 mA (max)
  - Collector Current (I_C): 50 mA (max)

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Diode Power Dissipation (P_D): 100 mW (TLP281), 70 mW (TLP281-4)
  - Collector Power Dissipation (P_C): 150 mW (TLP281), 100 mW (TLP281-4)
  - Total Package Power Dissipation (P_T): 200 mW (TLP281), 170 mW (TLP281-4)

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Operating Temperature (T_op): -55 to 100 °C
  - Storage Temperature (T_stg): -55 to 125 °C

- **Package Type:**
  - Surface mount package suitable for PCMCIA applications.

- **Special Features:**
  - High isolation voltage (2500 Vrms).
  - UL Recognized (UL1577).
  - Available in multiple ranks for current transfer ratio (CTR).

- **Moisture Sensitive Level:**
  - Not specified in the provided data, but typically assessed under JEDEC J-STD-020E.

#### Description:
The TLP281 is a phototransistor optocoupler that integrates a gallium arsenide (GaAs) infrared emitting diode and a phototransistor in a single package. It is designed for surface mount assembly, making it suitable for compact electronic applications. The device provides electrical isolation between its input and output, which is essential for protecting sensitive components in various electronic circuits.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Programmable Controllers:** Used in industrial automation systems for signal isolation and control.
- **AC/DC Input Modules:** Provides isolation in power supply circuits.
- **PC Card Modems (PCMCIA):** Utilized in communication devices to ensure signal integrity and isolation.
- **Signal Processing:** Acts as a signal isolator in various electronic devices to prevent noise and interference.

The TLP281 is particularly valued for its compact size, high isolation voltage, and versatility in various electronic applications, making it a reliable choice for engineers designing systems that require robust signal isolation.
