    - 495 µA (Maximum)

    - 8 µA (Shutdown, Maximum)

  - Typical: 320 µA
  - Shutdown Mode: 1.9 µA (Typical)
- **Operating Temperature Range**: 
  - -40°C to 150°C
- **Package Type**: 
  - SOT-23 (6 pins)
- **Special Features**: 
  - SMBus and I2C-compatible two-wire interface
  - Programmable digital temperature resolution (11 to 14 bits)
  - Fast conversion rate (up to 14 ms for 11-bit resolution)
  - Open-drain ALERT output pin
  - Shutdown mode with one-shot feature
- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL)**: 
  - Level 1 (JEDEC J-STD-020E)

#### Description:
The LM73 is a digital-output temperature sensor that utilizes a Delta-Sigma ADC to provide accurate temperature readings. It features a two-wire interface compatible with SMBus and I2C protocols, allowing for easy integration into various systems. The device can operate in different resolutions, enabling users to balance between conversion time and sensitivity. The LM73 is designed to be compact, occupying minimal board space while delivering reliable temperature measurements.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Portable Electronics**: Used for monitoring and managing temperature in handheld devices.
- **Notebook Computers**: Helps in thermal management to prevent overheating.
- **Automotive**: Monitors temperature in various automotive applications for safety and performance.
- **System Thermal Management**: Ensures optimal operating conditions by providing real-time temperature data.
- **Office Electronics**: Used in devices like printers and copiers to monitor internal temperatures.

The LM73 is ideal for applications requiring precise temperature measurements with low power consumption, making it suitable for battery-operated devices and systems where thermal management is critical.
