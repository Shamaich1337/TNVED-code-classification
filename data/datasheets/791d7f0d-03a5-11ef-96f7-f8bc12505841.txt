It operates over a –55 °C to 130 °C (grade 7) or \n–40 °C to 85 °C (grade 9) temperature range. The power supply operating range is 2.4 V to 5.5 V. The maximum temperature accuracy of the STLM20 is ± 1.5 °C (±0.5 °C typ) at an ambient temperature of 25 °C and V\nCC of 2.7 V. The temperature error increases linearly \nand reaches a maximum of ±2.5 °C at the temperature range extremes. The temperature range is affected by the power supply voltage. For the temperature grade 7 device, a power supply voltage of 2.7 V to 5.5 V, the temperature range extremes are +130 °C and –55 °C (decreasing the power supply voltage from 2.7 V to 2.4 V changes the low end of the operating temperature range from –55 °C to –30 °C, while the positive remains at +130 °C).\nThe STLM20 has a maximum quiescent supply current of 8 µA. Therefore, self-heating is \nnegligible.\nFigure 1. Logic diagram\n1. Pin 2 GND may be grounded or left floating (SOT323-5L on ly). ### Component Summary: STLM20W87F

**Key Characteristics and Specifications:**

- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Operating Voltage Range: 2.4 V to 5.5 V
  - Absolute Maximum Supply Voltage: -0.2 V to 6.5 V

- **Current Ratings:**
  - Quiescent Supply Current: 4.8 µA (typical), 8.0 µA (maximum)
  - Output Current: 10 mA (maximum)

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Ultra-low power consumption with a quiescent current of 4.8 µA, making it suitable for battery-operated devices.

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Grade 7: -55 °C to 130 °C
  - Grade 9: -40 °C to 85 °C

- **Package Type:**
  - Available in SOT323-5L (5-lead) and UDFN-4L (4-lead) packages.

- **Special Features or Notes:**
  - Precision analog voltage output with a maximum temperature accuracy of ±1.5 °C at 25 °C (±0.5 °C typical).
  - Negligible self-heating due to low quiescent current.
  - Temperature error increases linearly to ±2.5 °C at temperature extremes.

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (JEDEC J-STD-020E):**
  - Not specified in the provided text.

**Description of the Component:**
The STLM20W87F is an ultra-low current precision analog temperature sensor designed for low-power applications. It provides an analog voltage output that corresponds to the ambient temperature, making it suitable for various temperature monitoring applications. The device is optimized for battery-operated devices, ensuring minimal power consumption while maintaining high accuracy.

**Typical Applications:**
- Smartphones
- Multimedia PDA devices
- GPS devices
- Portable medical instruments
- Voltage-controlled crystal oscillator temperature monitors
- RF power transistor monitoring

This component is particularly useful in applications where accurate temperature measurement is critical, and power efficiency is paramount, such as in portable and battery-powered devices.
