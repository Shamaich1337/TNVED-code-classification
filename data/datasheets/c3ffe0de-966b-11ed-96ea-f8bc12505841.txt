### Component Summary: SiT9365AC-2B2-25E50.000000G

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings**: 2.5 V ±10%
- **Current Ratings**: 
  - Maximum Current Consumption: 89 mA (excluding load termination current)
  - Output Disable Supply Current: 58 mA (OE = Low)
- **Power Consumption**: 89 mA (typical at 3.3 V or 2.5 V)
- **Operating Temperature Range**: -40°C to 105°C (Extended Industrial)
- **Package Type**: 3.2 x 2.5 mm²
- **Special Features**:
  - Ultra-low jitter: 0.1 ps RMS phase jitter (random)
  - Frequency stability as low as ±50 ppm
  - Integrated on-chip regulators to filter power supply noise
  - Programmable for specific combinations of frequency, stability, voltage, and output signaling
- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL)**: MSL1 @ 260 °C

#### Description:
The SiT9365 is a differential MEMS (Micro-Electro-Mechanical Systems) crystal oscillator designed for ultra-low jitter applications. It supports standard frequencies ranging from 25 MHz to 325 MHz and utilizes SiTime's proprietary DualMEMS® temperature sensing and TurboCompensation® technology. This oscillator is engineered to provide exceptional performance in dynamic environments, resisting airflow, thermal gradients, shock, and vibration. The device is factory programmable, allowing for customization without the long lead times typically associated with quartz devices.

#### Typical Applications:
The SiT9365 is ideal for a variety of applications, particularly in environments that require precise timing and low jitter. Typical applications include:
- **Telecommunications**: Used in 10/40/100 Gbps Ethernet, SONET, and Fibre Channel systems.
- **Networking**: Suitable for routers, switches, and other networking equipment.
- **Industrial Applications**: Employed in instrumentation and server applications where reliable clock signals are critical.
- **Data Storage**: Utilized in SATA and SAS interfaces for data integrity and performance.

This oscillator's programmability and robust performance make it a versatile choice for engineers looking to optimize clock configurations in demanding applications.
