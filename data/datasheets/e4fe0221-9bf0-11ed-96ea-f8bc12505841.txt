### Component Summary: Cr4+:YAG (Cr4+:Y3Al5O12)

**Description:**
Cr4+:YAG is a crystal used primarily for passive Q-switching in laser applications. It serves as a working medium for lasers operating in the wavelength range of 1350 to 1600 nm and for passive Q-switching in lasers such as Nd:YAG, Nd:YLF, Nd:YVO4, and Yb:YAG. The crystal is known for its excellent optical properties and is produced using the Czochralski method.

**Key Specifications:**
- **Chemical Formula:** Cr4+:Y3Al5O12
- **Crystal Structure:** Cubic (la3d)
- **Lattice Parameters:** 12.01 Å
- **Mass Density:** 4.56 g/cm³
- **Mohs Hardness:** 8.5
- **Young's Modulus:** 335 GPa
- **Tensile Strength:** 2 GPa
- **Melting Point:** 1970°C
- **Thermal Conductivity:** 0.1213 W/(m·K)

- **Thermal Shock Resistance:** 800 W/m

- **Poisson Ratio:** 0.25
- **Refractive Index @ 1064 nm:** 1.83

### Typical Applications:
Cr4+:YAG is primarily used in:
- **Q-switching and model-locking** for various laser types including Nd:YAG, Nd:YAP, Nd:KGW, and Nd:YVO4 lasers.
- **Pumping** erbium-doped lasers and amplifiers.
- It is increasingly replacing traditional materials like LiF and dye due to its superior properties, making it suitable for high-performance laser systems in industrial, medical, and scientific applications.
