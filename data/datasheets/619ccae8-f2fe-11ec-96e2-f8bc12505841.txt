The input is triggered by \nthe signal edge and allows the use of a small- sized, low cost pulse transformer.  Input is \nactive low (output is high when input is low) to ease the use of the optocoupler. When driven \nby a pulse transformer, the input pulse (positive and negative) width must be larger than the minimum pulse width t\nonmin .\n5.2 Voltage reference\nA voltage reference is used to create accurate timing for the two-level turn-off with external \nresistor and capacitor.\n5.3 Desaturation protection\nDesaturation protection ensures the protection of the IGBT in the event of overcurrent. When the DESAT voltage goes higher than 7 V, th e output is driven low (with 2-level turn-off, \nif applicable). The FAULT output is activated. The FAULT state is exited at the next falling \nedge of IN input.\nA programmable blanking time is used to allow enough time for IGBT saturation. Blanking \ntime is provided by an internal current source and external capacitor.\nDESAT input can also be used with an external comparator for overcurrent or \novertemperature detection.\n5.4 Active Miller clamp\nA Miller clamp allows th e control of the Miller current during a high dV/dt situation and can \neliminate the need for a negative supply voltage.\nDuring turn-off, the gate voltage is monitored and the clamp output is activated when gate \nvoltage goes below 2 V (relativ e to GND). The clamp voltage is VL+3 V max. for a Miller \ncurrent up to 500 mA. The clamp is disabled when the IN input is triggered again.\n5.5 Two-level turn-off\nThe two-level turn-off is used to incr ease the reliability of the application.\nDuring turn-off, gate voltage can be reduced to a programmable level in order to reduce the \nIGBT current (in the event of overcurrent). This action prevents both dangerous overvoltage \nacross the IGBT and RBSOA problems, especially at short-circuit turn-off.\nThe two-level turn-off (Ta) delay is programmable through an external resistor and capacitor \nfor accurate timing (refer to Equation 1 ).\nTurn-off delay (Ta) is also used to delay the input signal  to prevent distortion of input pulse \nwidth.\nDocID018539  Rev 3 9/18TD350E Functional description\n18Equation 1\nTa [\uf06ds] \uf040 0.7 • Roff [k\uf057] • Coff [nF]\n5.6 Minimum ON time\nIn order to ensure the proper operation of the 2-level turn-off function, the input ON time \n(Twin) must be greater than the Twinmin  value:\nEquation 2\nTwinmin = Ta + 2 • Rdel • Coff\nRdel is the internal discharge resistor and Coff is the external timing capacitor.\nInput signals smaller than Ta are ignored. ### Component Summary: TD350ETR (STMicroelectronics)

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Maximum supply voltage (VH - VL): 28 V
  - Positive supply voltage (VH): 0 to 26 V
  - Negative supply voltage (VL): 0 to -10 V
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Source current: 1.5 A (typical), 1.2 A (minimum)
  - Sink current: 2.3 A (typical), 0.75 A (minimum)
- **Power Consumption:**
  - Quiescent current: 5 mA (no load)
  - Power dissipation: 500 mW
- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - -40 °C to +125 °C
- **Package Type:**
  - SO-14 (Tape and reel for TD350ETR)
- **Special Features:**
  - Active Miller clamp
  - Two-level turn-off with adjustable level and delay
  - Desaturation detection
  - Fault status output
  - Negative gate drive capability
  - UVLO (Under Voltage Lock Out) protection
  - 2 kV ESD protection (HBM)
- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL Level 1 (JEDEC J-STD-020E)

#### Description:
The **TD350E** is an advanced gate driver designed for IGBTs (Insulated Gate Bipolar Transistors) and power MOSFETs (Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor Field-Effect Transistors). It integrates control and protection functions, enabling the design of high-reliability systems. The device features an innovative active Miller clamp that simplifies the gate driving process by eliminating the need for a negative gate drive in most applications. It also includes a two-level turn-off feature that helps protect against excessive overvoltage during turn-off conditions.

#### Typical Applications:
The TD350E is primarily used in:
- **1200 V, 3-phase inverters:** Essential for converting DC to AC power in various applications.
- **Motor control:** Used in electric motor drives for precise control of speed and torque.
- **UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply) systems:** Provides backup power and ensures continuous operation during power outages.

This component is ideal for applications requiring robust gate driving capabilities, fault detection, and enhanced reliability in power management systems.
