### Component Summary: MBRM140T1G

**Key Specifications:**
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Peak Repetitive Reverse Voltage (VRRM): 40 V
  - Working Peak Reverse Voltage (VRWM): 40 V
  - DC Blocking Voltage (VR): 40 V

- **Current Ratings:**
  - Average Rectified Forward Current (IO): 1.0 A
  - Peak Repetitive Forward Current (IFRM): 2.0 A
  - Non-Repetitive Peak Surge Current (IFSM): 50 A

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Maximum Instantaneous Forward Voltage (VF) at 1.0 A: 0.55 V (at 25°C)

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Storage Temperature (Tstg): -55 to 150 °C
  - Operating Junction Temperature (TJ): -55 to 125 °C

- **Package Type:**
  - Surface Mount, POWERMITE (D0-216AA)

- **Special Features:**
  - Low profile with a maximum height of 1.1 mm
  - Small footprint area of 8.45 mm²
  - Low forward voltage drop for higher efficiency
  - ESD ratings: Human Body Model = 3B (> 16000 V), Machine Model = C (> 400 V)
  - AEC-Q101 qualified and PPAP capable
  - Pb-Free package

- **Moisture Sensitive Level:**
  - Moisture Sensitivity Level (MSL): Not specified in the provided text.

**Description:**
The MBRM140T1G is a Schottky Power Rectifier designed for surface mount applications. It utilizes the Schottky barrier principle, which allows for a low forward voltage drop and minimal reverse current, making it highly efficient. The component is packaged in a compact POWERMITE form factor, which is significantly smaller than traditional packages while maintaining excellent thermal performance.

**Typical Applications:**
The MBRM140T1G is ideal for use in various applications where space and efficiency are critical. Typical applications include:
- AC-DC and DC-DC converters
- Reverse battery protection circuits
- "ORing" of multiple supply voltages
- Power management in portable and battery-powered devices such as cellular phones, chargers, notebook computers, printers, PDAs, and PCMCIA cards.

This component is particularly suited for automotive and other applications that require stringent site and control change requirements, as indicated by its NRVB prefix.
