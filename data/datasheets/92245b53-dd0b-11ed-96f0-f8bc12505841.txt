### Component Summary: SM8S30AHE3_A/I

**Manufacturer:** Vishay General Semiconductor  
**Product Code:** SM8S30AHE3_A/I  
**Component Type:** Surface Mount Transient Voltage Suppressor (TVS)

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Breakdown Voltage (VBR): 33.3 V (min) to 36.8 V (max)
  - Working Voltage (VWM): 30 V (max)
  
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Peak Pulse Current (IPP): 150 A (10/1000 μs waveform)
  - Peak Forward Surge Current (IFSM): 700 A (8.3 ms single half sine-wave)
  
- **Power Consumption:**
  - Peak Pulse Power Dissipation (PPPM): 6600 W (10/1000 μs waveform), 5200 W (10/10,000 μs waveform)
  - Power Dissipation (PD): 8 W (on an infinite heatsink at TC = 25 °C)

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Junction Temperature (TJ): -55 °C to +175 °C

- **Package Type:**
  - DO-218AB

- **Special Features:**
  - Unidirectional polarity
  - Low leakage current
  - Low forward voltage drop
  - High surge capability
  - AEC-Q101 qualified for automotive applications
  - Meets ISO7637-2 surge specification
  - Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL): Level 1, per JEDEC J-STD-020E

#### Description:
The SM8S30AHE3_A/I is a surface mount transient voltage suppressor designed to protect sensitive electronic components from voltage transients caused by inductive load switching and lightning strikes. It utilizes a passivated anisotropic rectifier technology that ensures high reliability and stability at elevated temperatures, making it suitable for automotive and high-reliability applications.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Automotive Electronics:** Protection against load dump and other voltage transients in automotive circuits.
- **Consumer Electronics:** Safeguarding sensitive components in devices against voltage spikes.
- **Industrial Equipment:** Used in systems where inductive loads may cause voltage transients.
- **Telecommunications:** Protecting communication lines from voltage surges.

This component is particularly valuable in applications where reliability and performance under high-temperature conditions are critical, such as in automotive electronics and industrial systems.
