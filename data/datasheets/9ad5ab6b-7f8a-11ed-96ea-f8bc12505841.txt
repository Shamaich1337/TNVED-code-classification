### Component Summary: LM239ADR (Texas Instruments)

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Single Supply: 2V to 36V (Tested to 30V for Non-V Devices and 32V for V-Suffix Devices)
  - Dual Supplies: ±1V to ±18V (Tested to ±15V for Non-V Devices and ±16V for V-Suffix Devices)
  
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Supply Current: 0.8 mA (Typical) for four comparators
  - Output Current: 20 mA (Maximum)

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Supply current is independent of supply voltage, typically 0.8 mA.

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - -25°C to +85°C for LM239 and LM239A
  - -55°C to +125°C for LM139 and LM139A
  - -40°C to +125°C for LM2901, LM2901AV, and LM2901V
  - 0°C to 70°C for LM339 and LM339A

- **Package Type:**
  - Available in various packages including SOIC (14 pins), PDIP (14 pins), TSSOP (14 pins), and others.

- **Special Features:**
  - Low input bias current: 25 nA (Typical)
  - Low input offset voltage: 2 mV (Typical)
  - Open-collector output allows for wired-AND configurations.
  - Common-mode input voltage range includes ground.
  - Differential input voltage range equal to maximum-rated supply voltage: ±36V.
  
- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL Level 1 per JEDEC J-STD-020E.

#### Description:
The LM239ADR is a quad differential comparator designed to operate from a single power supply over a wide range of voltages. It can also function with dual supplies, making it versatile for various applications. The device features low power consumption and is characterized by low input bias and offset currents, which enhance its performance in precision applications. The open-collector output configuration allows for flexibility in interfacing with other logic families.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Industrial Applications:** Used in various industrial control systems for monitoring and control.
- **Automotive Applications:** Suitable for infotainment systems, body control modules, and other automotive electronics.
- **Power Supervision:** Ideal for voltage monitoring and supervisory circuits.
- **Signal Processing:** Can be used in oscillators, peak detectors, and logic voltage translation.
- **Data Acquisition Systems:** Useful in applications requiring precise voltage comparisons.

This component is widely used in both consumer and industrial electronics due to its reliability and performance across a range of operating conditions.
