### Component Summary: HMC787LC3B

**Description:**
The HMC787LC3B is a GaAs MMIC (Monolithic Microwave Integrated Circuit) fundamental mixer designed for operation in the frequency range of 3 to 10 GHz. It features a passive double-balanced topology, making it suitable for both upconversion and downconversion applications. The device is housed in a compact 12-lead 3x3 mm surface-mount technology (SMT) package, which is RoHS compliant and eliminates the need for external matching components.

**Key Specifications:**
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - LO Drive: +26 dBm (maximum)
  - RF/IF Input: +26 dBm (maximum)
  
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Maximum current at IF pin: 8 mA (to avoid non-functionality or failure)

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Continuous Power Dissipation (at T = 85 °C): 0.74 mW (derated by 11.4 mW/°C above 85 °C)

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Storage Temperature: -65 to +150 °C
  - Operating Temperature: -40 to +85 °C

- **Package Type:**
  - 12-lead 3x3 mm SMT package (Alumina body)

- **Special Features:**
  - High LO/RF Isolation: 55 dB
  - Low Conversion Loss: 9 dB (typical)
  - Wide IF Bandwidth: DC - 4 GHz
  - No external matching required
  - Compatible with high-volume surface mount manufacturing techniques

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL3 (according to JEDEC J-STD-020E)

**Typical Applications:**
The HMC787LC3B is ideal for various applications, including:
- WiMAX Infrastructure
- Microwave Radio Systems
- ISM (Industrial, Scientific, and Medical) & UWB (Ultra-Wideband) Radios
- Test Equipment and Sensors
- Military Communications

This mixer is particularly useful in systems requiring efficient signal processing at microwave frequencies, providing excellent isolation and low conversion loss, which are critical for maintaining signal integrity in high-frequency applications.
