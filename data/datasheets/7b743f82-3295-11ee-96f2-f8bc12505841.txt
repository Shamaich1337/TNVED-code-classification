Bypass capacitors are used to reduce the coupled noise by providing low-impedance \npower sources local to the analog circuitry.\n–Connect low-ESR, 0.1-µF ceramic bypass capacitors between each supply pin and ground, placed as \nclose to the device as possible. A single bypass capacitor from V+ to ground is applicable for single-\nsupply applications.\n•Separate grounding for analog and digital portions of circuitry is one of the simplest and most-effective \nmethods of noise suppression. One or more layers on multilayer PCBs are usually devoted to ground planes. \nA ground plane helps distribute heat and reduces EMI noise pickup. Make sure to physically separate digital \nand analog grounds, paying attention to the flow of the ground current.\n•To reduce parasitic coupling, run the input traces as far away from the supply or output traces as possible. If it \nis not possible to keep them separate, it is much better to cross the sensitive trace perpendicular as opposed \nto in parallel with the noisy trace.\n•Place the external components as close to the device as possible. Keeping R F and R G close to the inverting \ninput minimizes parasitic capacitance, as shown in Section 12.2.\n•Keep the length of input traces as short as possible. Always remember that the input traces are the most \nsensitive part of the circuit.\n•Consider a driven, low-impedance guard ring around the critical traces. A guard ring can significantly reduce \nleakage currents from nearby traces that are at different potentials.\n12.2 Layout Examples\nOUT1\nOUT2 IN1í\nIN1+\nVíV+\nIN2í\nIN2+RG\nRINRF\nGND\nVIN\nVSíGNDVS+\nGNDRun the input traces as far \naway from the supply lines \nas possible\nOnly needed for \ndual-supply \noperationPlace components close to \ndevice and to each other to \nreduce parasitic errors\nUse low-ESR, ceramic \nbypass capacitor\n(or GND for single supply) Ground (GND) plane on another layer\nFigure 12-1. ### Component Summary: LM2904QDRQ1

#### Key Characteristics and Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings**: 
  - Supply Voltage (V+ - V-): 
    - LM2904B-Q1 and LM2904BA-Q1: 3 V to 36 V
    - LM2904-Q1: 3 V to 26 V
- **Current Ratings**: 
  - Supply Current: 300 µA per channel (typical for LM2904B-Q1 and LM2904BA-Q1)
- **Power Consumption**: 
  - Quiescent Current: 300 µA (typical) for LM2904B-Q1 and LM2904BA-Q1
- **Operating Temperature Range**: 
  - -40°C to +125°C
- **Package Type**: 
  - Available in SOIC (8), TSSOP (8), and VSSOP (8)
- **Special Features**: 
  - AEC-Q100 qualified for automotive applications
  - Low input offset voltage (2 mV for LM2904BA-Q1, 3 mV for LM2904B-Q1)
  - Internal RF and EMI filter
  - Functional Safety-Capable
- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL)**: 
  - Level 1 per JEDEC J-STD-020E

#### Description:
The LM2904QDRQ1 is a dual operational amplifier designed for automotive applications. It consists of two independent, high-gain frequency-compensated operational amplifiers that can operate from a single supply over a wide voltage range. The device is characterized by low power consumption, low input offset voltage, and a common-mode input voltage range that includes ground, making it suitable for direct sensing applications.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Automotive Lighting**: Used in circuits for controlling vehicle lighting systems.
- **Body Electronics**: Integrated into systems for managing various electronic functions within the vehicle.
- **Automotive Head Unit**: Employed in audio systems for signal processing.
- **Telematics Control Unit**: Utilized in systems that manage vehicle communication and navigation.
- **Emergency Call (eCall)**: Supports safety features that enable automatic emergency calls.
- **Passive Safety Systems**: Integrated into brake systems and other safety-critical applications.
- **Electric Vehicle/Hybrid Electric Applications**: Used in inverter and motor control, on-board chargers, and battery management systems.

This operational amplifier is particularly valuable in cost-sensitive automotive applications due to its robust performance and reliability under harsh environmental conditions.
