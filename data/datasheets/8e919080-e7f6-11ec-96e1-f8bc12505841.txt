### Component Summary: CDCLVD1208RHDR

**Description:**
The CDCLVD1208 is a low additive jitter LVDS (Low Voltage Differential Signaling) buffer designed to distribute clock signals. It features a 2:8 differential buffer configuration, allowing one of two selectable clock inputs to be routed to eight pairs of differential LVDS outputs. This component is particularly suited for applications requiring high-speed clock distribution with minimal skew and jitter.

**Key Specifications:**
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Supply Voltage (VCC): 2.375 V to 2.625 V
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Static Supply Current (ICCSTAT): 17 mA to 28 mA (outputs unterminated, f=0Hz)
  - Supply Current at 100 MHz (ICC100): 62 mA to 84 mA
  - Supply Current at 800 MHz (ICC800): 87 mA to 111 mA
- **Power Consumption:**
  - Maximum power consumption (including termination resistors): 291.4 mW
- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - -40°C to 85°C
- **Package Type:**
  - 28-Pin VQFN (RHD), dimensions: 5 mm x 5 mm
- **Special Features:**
  - Low additive jitter: <300 fs RMS (10 kHz to 20 MHz)
  - Low output skew: 45 ps (maximum)
  - Universal inputs accepting LVDS, LVPECL, and LVCMOS
  - ESD protection: Exceeds 3 kV HBM, 1 kV CDM
- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - Level 2, according to JEDEC J-STD-020E

**Typical Applications:**
The CDCLVD1208 is commonly used in:
- Telecommunications and Networking
- Medical Imaging
- Test and Measurement Equipment
- Wireless Communications
- General-Purpose Clocking

### Summary:
The CDCLVD1208 is a versatile LVDS buffer ideal for high-speed clock distribution in various applications. Its ability to handle multiple input types and provide low jitter and skew makes it suitable for demanding environments such as telecommunications and medical imaging. The compact VQFN package and robust ESD protection further enhance its usability in modern electronic designs.
