Incorporated8Detailed Description\n8.1 Overview\nThe TPS2553-Q1 device isacurrent-limited, N-channel MOSFET, power-distribution switch foruse in\napplications where short circuits orheavy capacitive loads willbeencountered. Thedevice may provide upto1.5\nAofcontinuous load current. This device allows theuser toprogram thecurrent-limit threshold between 75mA\nand 1.7A(typical) viaanexternal resistor. Additional device shutdown features include overtemperature\nprotection and reverse-voltage protection. The device incorporates aninternal charge pump and gate drive\ncircuitry necessary todrive theN-channel MOSFET. The charge pump supplies power tothedriver circuit and\nprovides thenecessary voltage topullthegate oftheMOSFET above thesource. The charge pump operates\nfrom input voltages aslowas2.5Vandrequires little supply current. The driver controls thegate voltage ofthe\npower switch. The driver incorporates circuitry that controls theriseand falltimes oftheoutput voltage tolimit\nlarge current and voltage surges and provides built-in soft-start functionality. The TPS2553-Q1 device enters\nconstant-current mode when theload exceeds thecurrent-limit threshold.\n8.2 Functional Block Diagram\n8.3 Feature Description\n8.3.1 Overcurrent Conditions\nTheTPS2553-Q1 device responds toovercurrent conditions bylimiting theoutput current totheIOSlevels shown\ninFigure 22.When thedevice detects anovercurrent condition, thedevice maintains aconstant output current\nandreduces theoutput voltage accordingly. Two possible overload conditions canoccur.\nThe first condition iswhen ashort circuit orpartial short circuit ispresent when thedevice ispowered-up or\nenabled. The output voltage isheld near zero potential with respect toground and theTPS2553-Q1 device\nramps theoutput current toIOS.TheTPS2553-Q1 device willlimit thecurrent toIOSuntil theoverload condition is\nremoved orthedevice begins tothermal cycle. The device willremain offuntil power iscycled orthedevice\nenable istoggled.\nThe second condition iswhen ashort circuit, partial short circuit, ortransient overload occurs while thedevice is\nenabled andpowered on.The device responds totheovercurrent condition within time tIOS(see Figure 20).The\ncurrent-sense amplifier isoverdriven during this time and momentarily disables the internal current-limit\nMOSFET. The current-sense amplifier recovers andlimits theoutput current toIOS.Similar totheprevious case,\ntheTPS2553-Q1 device willlimit thecurrent toIOSuntil theoverload condition isremoved orthedevice begins to\nthermal cycle.\n12TPS2553-Q1\nSLVSBD0B –NOVEMBER 2012 –REVISED JUNE 2020 www.ti.com\nProduct Folder Links: TPS2553-Q1Submit Documentation Feedback Copyright ©2012 –2020, Texas Instruments IncorporatedFeature Description (continued)\nThe TPS2553-Q1 device thermal cycles ifanoverload condition ispresent long enough toactivate thermal\nlimiting inanyoftheprevious cases. Thedevice turns offwhen thejunction temperature exceeds 135°C(typical)\nwhile incurrent-limit. Thedevice remains offuntil thejunction temperature cools 10°C(typical) andthen restarts.\nTheTPS2553-Q1 device cycles onandoffuntil theoverload isremoved (see Figure 5andFigure 7).\n8.3.2 Reverse-Voltage Protection\nThe reverse-voltage protection feature turns offtheN-channel MOSFET whenever theoutput voltage exceeds\ntheinput voltage by135mV(typical) for4-ms (typical). Areverse current of(VOUT–VIN)/rDS(on) )willbepresent\nwhen thisoccurs. This prevents damage todevices ontheinput side oftheTPS2553-Q1 device bypreventing\nsignificant current from sinking into theinput capacitance. The TPS2553-Q1 device allows theN-channel\nMOSFET toturnononce theoutput voltage goes below theinput voltage forthesame 4-ms deglitch time.\n8.3.3 FAULT Response\nTheFAULT open-drain output isasserted (active low) during anovercurrent, overtemperature, orreverse-voltage\ncondition. The TPS2553-Q1 device asserts theFAULT signal until thefault condition isremoved andthedevice\nresumes normal operation. The TPS2553-Q1 device isdesigned toeliminate false FAULT reporting byusing an\ninternal delay deglitch circuit forovercurrent (7.5-ms typical) andreverse-voltage (4-ms typical) conditions without\ntheneed forexternal circuitry. This ensures that FAULT isnotaccidentally asserted due tonormal operation\nsuch asstarting into aheavy capacitive load. The deglitch circuitry delays theresponse toand release of\nsignaling fault conditions. Overtemperature conditions are not deglitched and assert the FAULT signal\nimmediately.\n8.3.4 Undervoltage Lockout (UVLO)\nTheundervoltage lockout (UVLO) circuit disables thepower switch until theinput voltage reaches theUVLO turn-\nonthreshold. Built-in hysteresis prevents unwanted onand offcycling due toinput voltage drop from large\ncurrent surges.\n8.3.5 Enable (EN)\nThe logic enable controls thepower switch, bias forthecharge pump, driver, and other circuits toreduce the\nsupply current. The supply current isreduced toless than 1-μAwhen alogic lowispresent onEN.Alogic high\ninput onENenables thedriver, control circuits, andpower switch. The enable input iscompatible with both TTL\nandCMOS logic levels.\n8.3.6 Thermal Sense\nThe TPS2553-Q1 device has aself-protection feature using two independent thermal sensing circuits that\nmonitor theoperating temperature ofthepower switch. Itdisables theoperation ifthetemperature exceeds\nrecommended operating conditions. The TPS2553-Q1 device operates inconstant-current mode during an\novercurrent condition, which increases thevoltage drop across thepower-switch. The power dissipation inthe\npackage isproportional tothevoltage drop across thepower switch, which increases thejunction temperature\nduring anovercurrent condition. ### Component Summary: TPS2553QDBVRQ1

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Input Voltage Range: 2.5V to 6.5V
  - Maximum Voltage on IN, OUT, EN, ILIM, FAULT: -0.3V to 7V
  - Reverse Input-Output Voltage Protection: -7V to 7V

- **Current Ratings:**
  - Maximum Load Current: 1.5A
  - Adjustable Current-Limit Range: 75mA to 1.7A (typical)
  - Continuous FAULT Sink Current: 25mA

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Supply Current (enabled): 120-140μA (depending on RILIM)
  - Supply Current (disabled): 0.1-1μA

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - -40°C to 125°C (Grade 1, AEC-Q100 qualified)

- **Package Type:**
  - SOT-23 (6 pins)

- **Special Features:**
  - Precision adjustable current limiting with ±6% accuracy at 1.7A
  - Fast overcurrent response time of 2μs
  - Built-in soft-start feature
  - ESD protection: 15kV (IEC61000-4-2)
  - Reverse voltage protection
  - Thermal shutdown protection

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - Level 2, 260°C, 1 year (according to JEDEC J-STD-020E)

#### Description:
The **TPS2553-Q1** is a precision adjustable current-limited power-distribution switch designed for applications that require protection against overcurrent conditions and heavy capacitive loads. It features an N-channel MOSFET that can handle up to 1.5A of continuous load current. The current limit can be programmed using an external resistor, allowing for flexibility in various applications. The device includes built-in protections such as reverse voltage protection, thermal shutdown, and fast overcurrent response, making it suitable for automotive and USB applications.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Automotive Applications:**
  - Power distribution and switching for automotive electronic control units (ECUs).
  - Precision current limiting for automotive supply rails.

- **USB Power Management:**
  - Current limiting for USB ports and hubs, ensuring compliance with USB specifications.
  - Protection against overcurrent conditions in USB-powered devices.

- **General Power Distribution:**
  - Applications requiring reliable power distribution with current limiting to protect downstream components from overloads.

The TPS2553-Q1 is ideal for designs that demand high reliability and precision in current management, particularly in automotive and consumer electronics where safety and performance are critical.
