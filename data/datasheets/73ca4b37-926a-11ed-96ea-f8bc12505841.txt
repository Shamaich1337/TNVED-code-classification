### Component Summary: TLV117112DCYT (Texas Instruments)

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Input Voltage (VIN): 2.0V to 5.5V (Absolute Maximum: 6.0V)
  - Output Voltage (VOUT): Fixed voltage options available (e.g., 3.3V for TLV117112DCYT)
  
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Maximum Output Current (IOUT): Internally limited, typically 1.1A
  - Quiescent Current (IQ): 100µA (max)

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Low quiescent current, significantly lower than standard 1117 devices (500x lower)

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Junction Temperature (TJ): -40°C to +125°C

- **Package Type:**
  - SOT-223 (DCY)

- **Special Features:**
  - Low dropout voltage: 455mV at 1A for VOUT = 3.3V
  - High Power Supply Rejection Ratio (PSRR): 65dB at 1kHz
  - Stable with 0-Ω ESR ceramic capacitors
  - Thermal shutdown and overcurrent protection
  - Minimum ensured current limit: 1.1A
  - Undervoltage lockout (UVLO)

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - Level 1 (according to JEDEC J-STD-020E)

#### Description:
The TLV1171 is a low-dropout (LDO) linear voltage regulator designed for applications requiring a fixed output voltage with low quiescent current. It is particularly suitable for battery-powered devices due to its low power consumption and high efficiency. The device provides a regulated output voltage while maintaining a low dropout voltage, making it ideal for applications where the input voltage is close to the output voltage.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Consumer Electronics:** Used in set-top boxes, TVs, monitors, and PC peripherals.
- **Communication Devices:** Suitable for modems and other communication products.
- **Power Management:** Acts as a post-regulator for switching power supplies, ensuring stable voltage levels.
- **Portable Devices:** Ideal for notebooks and other battery-operated devices due to its low quiescent current.

The TLV1171's combination of low dropout voltage, high PSRR, and low quiescent current makes it an excellent choice for a wide range of applications where efficient power management is critical.
