Decoupling and Layout  \nA clean and low noise power supply is very important in \namplifier circuit design, besides of input signal noise, \nthe power supply is one of important source of noise to \nthe amplifiers through +VS and -VS pins. Power supply \nbypassing is an effective method to clear up the noise \nat power supply, and the low impedance path to ground \nof decoupling capacitor will bypass the noise to GND. In application, 10μF ceramic capacitor paralleled with 0.1μF or 0.01μF ceramic capacitor is used in Figure 1. \nThe ceramic capacitors should be placed as close as \npossible to +V\nS and -VS power supply pins.  \n \nFigure 1. Amplifier Power Supply Bypassing  \n \nGrounding \nIn low  speed application, one node grounding technique \nis the simplest and most effective method to eliminate the noise generated by grounding. In high speed \napplication, the general method to eliminate noise is to \nuse a complete ground plane technique, and the whole \nground plane will help distribute heat and reduce EMI \nnoise pickup.  \n \nReduce Input -to-Output Coupling  \nTo reduce the input -to-output coupling, the input traces \nmust be placed as far away from the power supply or \noutput traces as  possible. The sensitive trace must not \nbe placed in parallel with the noisy trace in same layer . \nThey must be placed perpendicularly in different layers \nto reduce the crosstalk . These PCB l ayout techniques \nwill help to reduce unwanted positive feedback and \nnoise.
