The ESD \ntolerance has been improved on these devices to over \n±15kV for Human Body Model. These devices are pin-to-\npin compatible with MaxLinear’s SP232A and SP233A as \nwell as popular industry standards.This family of parts offer \na 120kbps data rate, 10V/us slew rate and an on-board \ncharge pump that operates from a single 5V supply using \n0.1µF ceramic capacitors.  \nThe SP202E, SP232E and SP233E devices have internal \ncharge pump voltage converters which allow them to \noperate from a single +5V supply. The charge pumps will \noperate with polarized or non-polarized capacitors ranging \nfrom 0.1 to 10µF and will generate the ±6V needed to \ngenerate the RS-232 output levels.\nThe SP233E design offers internal charge pump \ncapacitors. \nTheory Of Operation\nThe SP202E, SP232E and SP233E devices are made up \nof three basic circuit blocks: 1. Drivers, 2. Receivers, and \n3. charge pump. Each block is described below.\nDrivers\nThe drivers are inverting level transmitters that convert TTL \nor CMOS logic levels to EIA / TIA-232 levels with an \ninverted sense relative to the input logic levels. The typical \ndriver output voltage swing is ±6V. Even under worst case \nloading conditions of 3k Ω and 2500pF, the driver output is \nguaranteed to be ±5.0V minimum, thus satisfying the \nRS-232 specification. The driver outputs are protected \nagainst infinite short-circuits to ground without degradation \nin reliability. \nThe slew rate of the driver output is internally limited to \n30V/ µs in order to meet the EIA standards (EIA-232F). \nAdditionally, the driver outputs LOW to HIGH transition \nmeets the monotonic output requirements of the standard.\nReceivers\nThe receivers convert EIA / TIA-232 signal levels to \ninverted TTL or CMOS logic output levels. Since the input \nis usually from a transmission line, where long cable length and system interference can degrade the signal, the inputs \nhave a typical hysteresis margin of 500mV. This ensures \nthat the receiver is virtually immune to noisy transmission \nlines. The input thresholds are 0.8V minimum and 2.8V \nmaximum, again well within the ±3V RS-232 requirements. \nShould an input be left unconnected, an internal 5k Ω pull-\ndown resistor to ground will commit the output of the \nreceiver to a HIGH state.\nIn actual system applications, it is quite possible for signals \nto be applied to receiver inputs before power is applied to \nthe receiver circuitry. This occurs, for example, when a PC \nuser attempts to print, only to realize that the printer wasn't \nturned on. In this case an RS-232 signal from the PC will \nappear on the receiver input at the printer. When the printer \npower is turned on, the receiver will operate normally. All of \nthese devices are fully protected.\nCharge pump\nThe charge pump is an Exar patented design and uses a \nunique approach compared to older less efficient designs. \nThe charge pump requires 4 external capacitors and uses \na four phase voltage shifting technique. The internal power \nsupply consists of a dual charge pump that provides a \ndriver output voltage swing of ±6V. The internal oscillator \ncontrols the four phases of the voltage shifting. A \ndescription of each phase follows:\nPhase 1\nVSS charge store and double: The positive terminals of \ncapacitors C1 and C2 are charged from VCC with their \nnegative terminals initially connected to ground. This transfers the doubled and \ninverted (V-) voltage onto C4. Meanwhile, capacitor C1 is \ncharged from VCC to prepare it for its next phase.\nSP202E, SP232E, SP233E  High Performance RS-232 Line Drivers / Receivers  Description\n3/28/19  Rev 1.0.2 6Phase 3\nVDD charge store and double: Phase three is identical to \nthe first phase. ### Component Summary: SP202EEN-L/TR

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Supply Voltage (VCC): 5V (±10%)
  - V+ Output: Up to 11V
  - V- Output: Down to -11V
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Supply Current (No Load): 3-5 mA
  - Supply Current (Loaded): Up to 15 mA
  - Output Short Circuit Current: ±18 mA
- **Power Consumption:**
  - Power Dissipation: 375 mW (derated above 70°C)
- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Commercial: 0°C to 70°C
  - Industrial: -40°C to 85°C
- **Package Type:**
  - Available in NSOIC16 and WSOIC16 packages
- **Special Features:**
  - Improved ESD tolerance: ±15kV (Human Body Model)
  - Internal charge pump for generating RS-232 levels
  - Low power CMOS operation
  - Pin-to-pin compatible with SP232A and SP233A
- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL Level: Not specified in the provided data.

#### Description:
The SP202E is a high-performance RS-232 line driver and receiver designed to meet EIA/TIA-232 and V.28 serial communication protocols. It features a BiCMOS design that allows for low power consumption while maintaining high performance. The device includes an internal charge pump that operates from a single +5V power supply, generating the necessary voltage levels for RS-232 communication. The SP202E is part of a family of devices that includes the SP232E and SP233E, which offer similar functionalities with variations in external capacitor requirements.

#### Typical Applications:
The SP202E is commonly used in applications requiring serial communication, such as:
- **Data Communication:** Connecting computers to peripherals like printers and modems.
- **Embedded Systems:** Facilitating communication between microcontrollers and other devices.
- **Industrial Automation:** Used in systems that require robust communication over longer distances.
- **Telecommunications:** Implementing RS-232 interfaces in various telecom equipment.

This component is ideal for applications where reliable data transmission is critical, especially in environments prone to electrostatic discharge (ESD). Its compatibility with existing RS-232 standards makes it a versatile choice for engineers designing communication interfaces.
