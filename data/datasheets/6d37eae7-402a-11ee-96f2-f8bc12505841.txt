When the output load is higher\nthan the one supported by the device, the output current rises until the limit of typically 3.5 A is reached; at this\npoint the current is kept constant even when the load impedance is zero. The thermal protection acts when the\njunction temperature reaches 170 °C. The IC enters the shutdown status. As soon as the junction temperature\nfalls again below 150 °C the device starts working again. In order to calculate the maximum power the device can\ndissipate, keeping the junction temperature below T J-OP , the following formula is used:\nP D M A X = 125 − T A M B / R T H J − A (1)\n6.2 Output voltage setting for ADJ version\nIn the adjustable version, the output voltage can be set from 0.5 V up to the input voltage minus the voltage drop\nacross the pass transistor (dropout voltage), by connecting a resistor divider between the ADJ pin and the output,\nallowing remote voltage sensing. The resistor divider can be selected using the following equation:\nV O U T = V A D J 1 + R 1 / R 2 , w it ℎ V A D J = 0.5 V t yp . (2)\n6.3 Enable pin\nThe LD39200 features an enable function. When the EN voltage is higher than 1.2 V the device is ON, and if it is\nlower than 0.5 V the device is OFF. In shutdown mode, the total current consumption is 10 nA (typ). The EN pin\ndoes not have an internal pull-up, therefore it cannot be left floating if it is not used.\n6.4 Power Good pin (PG)\nSome applications require a flag showing that the output voltage is in the correct range. Power Good threshold\ndepends on the output voltage. When the output voltage is higher than 0.92 * V OUT(nom) , the PG pin goes to high\nimpedance. If the output voltage is below 0.80 * V OUT(nom)  the PG pin goes to low impedance. If the device works\nwell, the PG pin is at high impedance.\n6.5 Reverse current protection\nThe device avoids the reverse current to flow from the output to the input during any operating condition (EN = 0\nor EN = 1, V IN > V OUT + V DROP ). During fast turn-on/off this function prevents a big current from flowing to the\ninput. Moreover it is used to avoid the reverse current to flow from the output pin to the input one, when other\npower supplies, providing a voltage higher than the input voltage, are connected to the output pin. If a power\nsupply, providing a voltage lower than LDO output voltage, is connected to OUT pin, LDO works in current\nprotection, causing high power dissipation inside the device.\nWhen the device is disabled (EN = low) and V OUT > 0 V, a small current (few µA) is sunk from the OUT pin.LD39200\nApplication information \nDS10079  - Rev 3 page 9/29\n7 Typical performance characteristics\n(The following plots are referred to the typical application circuit and, unless otherwise noted, at T A = 25 °C)\nFigure 4. #### Description:
The **LD39200** is a high-performance low-dropout (LDO) linear voltage regulator designed to provide a stable output voltage with a maximum output current of 2 A. It operates within a wide input voltage range of 1.25 V to 6.0 V and features a low dropout voltage of 130 mV, making it suitable for applications where power efficiency is critical. The device is available in both adjustable and fixed output voltage versions, allowing flexibility in design.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Telecom Infrastructure:** The LD39200 is ideal for powering various telecom equipment due to its high PSRR and low dropout characteristics.
- **Medium Power Point of Load (POL) Regulation:** It can be used in point-of-load applications where space is limited and efficiency is paramount.
- **Consumer Electronics:** The regulator is suitable for consumer devices requiring stable voltage regulation with low power consumption.

This component is particularly useful in applications where a reliable and efficient power supply is necessary, such as in battery-operated devices, portable electronics, and sensitive analog circuits.
