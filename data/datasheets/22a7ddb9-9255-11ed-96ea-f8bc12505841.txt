### Component Summary: BFP520 H6327

**Description:**
The BFP520 is a low voltage silicon NPN RF bipolar transistor designed for high-frequency applications. It is part of Infineon's fifth generation RF bipolar transistor family and features a grounded emitter configuration (SIEGET™). The device is optimized for low noise and high gain, making it suitable for various RF applications.

**Key Specifications:**
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Collector-Emitter Voltage (VCEO): 2.5 V (max)
  - Collector-Emitter Breakdown Voltage (V(BR)CEO): 3.5 V (max)
  - Collector-Base Voltage (VCBO): 10 V (max)
  - Emitter-Base Voltage (VEBO): 1 V (max)

- **Current Ratings:**
  - Collector Current (IC): 50 mA (max)
  - Base Current (IB): 5 mA (max)

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Total Power Dissipation (Ptot): 125 mW (max)

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Junction Temperature (TJ): 150 °C (max)
  - Storage Temperature (TStg): -55 °C to 150 °C

- **Package Type:**
  - SOT343 (Surface Mount)

- **Special Features:**
  - Minimum Noise Figure (NFmin): 0.95 dB at 1.8 GHz, 2 V, 2 mA
  - High Gain (Gms): 24 dB at 1.8 GHz, 2 V, 20 mA
  - Output Third-Order Intercept Point (OIP3): 25 dBm at 1.8 GHz, 2 V, 20 mA
  - Qualified for industrial applications according to JEDEC47/20/22.

- **Moisture Sensitive Level:**
  - Moisture Sensitivity Level (MSL): Not specified in the provided text.

**Typical Applications:**
The BFP520 is commonly used in:
- Radio-frequency oscillators, such as LNB local oscillators.
- Broadband low noise amplifiers (LNAs) for applications including CATV, DVB-T, DAB/DMB, and FM/AM radio.
- LNAs for wireless communications, including cordless phones.

This transistor is particularly suited for applications requiring low noise and high gain at high frequencies, making it ideal for RF signal processing and amplification tasks in various communication systems.
