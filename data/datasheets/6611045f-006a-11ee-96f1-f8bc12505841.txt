### Component Summary: MM74HC86MX

**Description:**
The MM74HC86MX is a Quad 2-Input Exclusive OR (XOR) Gate, utilizing advanced silicon-gate CMOS technology. It is designed to achieve high-speed operation comparable to LS-TTL gates while maintaining low power consumption and high noise immunity typical of standard CMOS integrated circuits. The device features fully buffered outputs and is compatible with the 74LS logic family in terms of functionality and pinout.

**Key Specifications:**

- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Supply Voltage (VCC): 2 V to 6 V
  - Maximum Supply Voltage: 7.0 V
  - Input Voltage Range: -0.5 V to VCC + 0.5 V
  - Output Voltage Range: -0.5 V to VCC + 0.5 V

- **Current Ratings:**
  - DC Output Current (per pin): ±25 mA
  - Clamp Diode Current: ±20 mA
  - DC VCC or GND Current (per pin): ±50 mA

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Maximum Quiescent Supply Current: 40 µA (at 6 V)
  - Power Dissipation: 600 mW (SOIC package), 500 mW (S.O. package)

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Storage Temperature: -65 °C to +150 °C
  - Operating Temperature: -55 °C to +125 °C

- **Package Type:**
  - Available in SOIC-14 and TSSOP-14 packages.

- **Special Features:**
  - Typical Propagation Delay: 12 ns
  - Low Input Current: 1 µA Maximum
  - Output Drive Capability: 10 LS-TTL Loads
  - RoHS Compliant, Pb-Free, Halide Free
  - All inputs are protected from static discharge by internal diode clamps.

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL Level: 1 (according to JEDEC J-STD-020E)

**Typical Applications:**
The MM74HC86 is commonly used in digital logic circuits for applications such as:
- Data processing and signal manipulation
- Arithmetic operations in digital systems
- Logic functions in microcontroller interfacing
- Implementing complex logic functions in programmable logic devices
- General-purpose logic applications where XOR functionality is required.

This component is ideal for use in consumer electronics, automotive systems, and industrial control applications where reliable and efficient logic operations are necessary.
