### Component Summary: IRF9530NSTRLPBF

**Manufacturer:** Infineon Technologies  
**Product Code:** IRF9530NSTRLPBF

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Drain-to-Source Breakdown Voltage (V(BR)DSS): -100 V
  - Gate-to-Source Voltage (VGS): ±20 V

- **Current Ratings:**
  - Continuous Drain Current (ID) at T_C = 25°C: -14 A
  - Continuous Drain Current (ID) at T_C = 100°C: -10 A
  - Pulsed Drain Current (IDM): -56 A

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Maximum Power Dissipation (PD) at T_A = 25°C: 3.8 W
  - Maximum Power Dissipation (PD) at T_C = 25°C: 79 W
  - Linear Derating Factor: 0.53 W/°C

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Junction Temperature (TJ): -55°C to +175°C
  - Storage Temperature (TSTG): -55°C to +175°C

- **Package Type:**
  - D2Pak (TO-263AB) for surface mount applications
  - TO-262 for through-hole applications (Obsolete)

- **Special Features:**
  - P-Channel MOSFET
  - Fully Avalanche Rated
  - Fast Switching
  - Advanced Process Technology
  - Lead-Free

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - D2Pak: MSL1 (per JEDEC J-STD-020D)

#### Description:
The IRF9530NSTRLPBF is a fifth-generation HEXFET® power MOSFET designed for high-efficiency applications. It features a P-channel configuration, which allows it to switch and control high currents with low on-resistance, making it suitable for various power management tasks. The device is known for its rugged design and fast switching capabilities, which enhance its reliability in demanding environments.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Power Management:** Used in power supply circuits, DC-DC converters, and battery management systems.
- **Motor Control:** Suitable for driving motors in various applications, including robotics and industrial automation.
- **Signal Processing:** Can be utilized in signal switching applications where low on-resistance is critical.
- **High Current Applications:** Ideal for applications requiring high current handling with minimal power loss.

This component is particularly beneficial in designs where space is limited, and efficiency is paramount, making it a popular choice among engineers in the electronics industry.
