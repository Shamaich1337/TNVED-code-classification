Conversion factor  \nThe photo transfer curve (PTC) is used to measure the conversion factor of the sensor. The curve draws the \nmeasured photo -induced variance (subtract dark variance gray value) versus the mean photo -induced gray \nvalues (subtract dark offset). The slope of 0 to 70% range of saturation of the linear regression line is used to \nextract the conversion factor.  \n2. Full well capacity  \nThe full well capacity is calculated  by dividing the saturation by the conversion factor. #### Description:
The GMAX2505-BVM-HST-AU1 is a 5MP global shutter CMOS image sensor designed for high-speed imaging applications. It features a pixel size of 2.5 µm and operates in a 1/2” optical format. The sensor supports both 10-bit and 12-bit output modes, allowing for flexible data handling depending on the application requirements. The GMAX2505 is particularly noted for its high frame rate capabilities, achieving up to 290 frames per second in 10-bit mode.

#### Typical Applications:
The GMAX2505 is suitable for a variety of applications, including:
- **Machine Vision:** Ideal for industrial automation and quality control systems where high-speed image capture is critical.
- **High-Resolution Industrial Inspection:** Used in scenarios requiring detailed inspection of components and materials.
- **Intelligent Traffic Systems (ITS):** Effective for monitoring and analyzing traffic patterns and vehicle movements.
- **Robotics and Automation:** Provides real-time imaging for robotic vision systems, enhancing navigation and object recognition capabilities.

This sensor's advanced features, such as interleaved HDR and external triggering, make it versatile for both standard and specialized imaging tasks in various fields.
