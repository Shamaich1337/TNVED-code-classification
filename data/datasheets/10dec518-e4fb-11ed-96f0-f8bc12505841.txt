### Component Summary: LMV225SD/NOPB

**Key Characteristics and Specifications:**

- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Supply Voltage (VDD): 2.7V to 5.5V
  - Absolute Maximum Supply Voltage: 6.0V

- **Current Ratings:**
  - Supply Current (Active Mode): 
    - LMV225: 4.8 mA (typical) to 7 mA (maximum)
    - LMV226: 4.9 mA (typical) to 6.2 mA (maximum)
    - LMV228: 4.9 mA (typical) to 6.2 mA (maximum)
  - Shutdown Current: 0.44 μA (typical) to 4.5 μA (maximum)

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Active Mode: Varies based on the specific model, with typical values around 5 mA.
  - Shutdown Mode: Very low power consumption (μA range).

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - -40°C to +85°C

- **Package Type:**
  - Available in multiple packages: DSBGA (Thin and Ultra-Thin) and WSON.

- **Special Features or Notes:**
  - 30 dB linear RF power detection range.
  - Logic low shutdown functionality.
  - Integrated filter for low-ripple average power detection.
  - Buffered output for LMV226, suitable for GSM, EDGE, GPRS, and TDMA applications.

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL Level 1 per JEDEC J-STD-020E.

**Description:**
The LMV225SD/NOPB is an RF power detector designed for CDMA and WCDMA applications. It provides a linear output voltage that corresponds to the RF input power in dBm, with a detection range of -30 dBm to 0 dBm for the LMV225 variant. The device operates over a frequency range of 450 MHz to 2 GHz and is capable of accurate temperature and supply compensation.

**Typical Applications:**
- **CDMA RF Power Control:** Used in mobile communication systems to manage the power levels of RF signals.
- **WCDMA RF Power Control:** Similar applications in wideband CDMA systems.
- **Power Amplifier Modules:** Integration with power amplifiers for effective power management.
- **Signal Processing:** Utilized in systems requiring precise RF power measurements.

The LMV225 is particularly suited for applications that require high accuracy in power detection, such as in mobile communication devices, where maintaining optimal power levels is crucial for performance and battery life.
