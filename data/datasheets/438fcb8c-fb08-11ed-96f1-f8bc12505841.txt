### Component Summary: RXEF050-2 (Littelfuse)

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Rating**: 72V
- **Hold Current**: 0.50A
- **Trip Current**: 1.00A
- **Power Dissipation (PD)**: 0.77W
- **Maximum Time-to-Trip**: 4.0 seconds
- **Operating Temperature Range**: -40°C to 85°C
- **Package Type**: Radial-leaded
- **Moisture Sensitive Level**: Not specified in the provided data, but generally compliant with JEDEC J-STD-020E.
- **Special Features**: RoHS compliant, halogen-free, UL, CSA, TÜV, and CQC recognized.

#### Description:
The RXEF050-2 is a PolySwitch resettable device, specifically designed as a polymer positive temperature coefficient (PPTC) thermistor. Its primary function is to provide overcurrent protection in electronic circuits. When the current exceeds the hold current rating, the device transitions from a low-resistance state to a high-resistance state, effectively interrupting the circuit and protecting downstream components.

#### Typical Applications:
The RXEF050-2 is suitable for a variety of applications, including:
- **Consumer Electronics**: Used in devices like modems, CD-ROMs, game machines, and printers to prevent damage from overcurrent conditions.
- **Industrial Controls**: Protects transformers and other industrial equipment from excessive current.
- **Telecommunications**: Utilized in satellite video receivers and analog/digital line cards.
- **Medical Equipment**: Provides safety in medical devices by preventing overcurrent situations.
- **Lighting and Security Systems**: Ensures reliable operation by protecting against fault conditions.

### Additional Notes:
- The RXEF series is part of a broader range of Littelfuse's PolySwitch products, which offer various hold currents and voltage ratings, providing flexibility for engineers in designing protective circuits.
- The device is compatible with high-volume electronics assembly processes and assists in meeting regulatory requirements for electronic components.
- It is important to evaluate the device's suitability for specific applications, as it is not designed for repeated fault conditions or prolonged trip events.
