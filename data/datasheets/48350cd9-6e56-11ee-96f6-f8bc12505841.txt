### Component Summary: GSOT05C-E3-08 (Vishay)

#### Key Characteristics and Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Maximum Reverse Working Voltage (V_RWM): 5 V
  - Reverse Breakdown Voltage (V_BR): 6.5 to 8.7 V (at I_R = 1 mA)
  
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Peak Pulse Current (I_PPM): 30 A (according to IEC 61000-4-5)
  - Reverse Current (I_R): Up to 10 µA at V_R = 5.5 V

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Peak Pulse Power (P_PP): 480 W (for pin 1 to 3 or pin 2 to 3)
  
- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Junction Temperature (T_J): -55 to +150 °C
  - Storage Temperature (T_STG): -55 to +150 °C

- **Package Type:**
  - SOT-23

- **Special Features or Notes:**
  - Two-line ESD protection device
  - ESD immunity according to IEC 61000-4-2: ± 30 kV contact and air discharge
  - AEC-Q101 qualified
  - Moisture Sensitivity Level: MSL level 1 (according to J-STD-020)

#### Description:
The GSOT05C-E3-08 is a two-line ESD protection device designed to safeguard sensitive electronic components from electrostatic discharge (ESD) and voltage transients. It is housed in a compact SOT-23 package, making it suitable for space-constrained applications. The device operates in a bidirectional asymmetrical protection mode, allowing it to clamp both positive and negative voltage transients effectively.

#### Typical Applications:
- **ESD Protection:** Commonly used in consumer electronics, telecommunications, and automotive applications to protect data lines and signal paths from ESD events.
- **Signal Integrity:** Ensures the integrity of data signals in high-speed communication lines by clamping transient voltages that could disrupt signal quality.
- **Robustness in Harsh Environments:** Ideal for applications requiring high reliability and robustness against voltage spikes, such as industrial control systems and automotive electronics.

This summary provides a comprehensive overview of the GSOT05C-E3-08, highlighting its key specifications, functionality, and typical use cases in electronic designs.
