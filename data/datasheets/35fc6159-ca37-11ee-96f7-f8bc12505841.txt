The Opal Security Subsystem Class(SSC) 2.0 defines the details of data \nmanagement in storage devices and the classes authority for data access, and secures data from  theft and \ntampering by unauthorized persons who are able to gain access to the storage device or host syste m. \n \nTCG Opal 2.0 Main Features:  \n⚫ AES 256 -bit Hardware Self Encryption  \n⚫ Deploy Storage Device & Take Ownership:  \nThe Storage Device is integrated into its target system and ownership transferred  by setting or \nchanging the Storage Device’s owner credential.  \n⚫ Activate or Enroll Storage Device:  \nLBA ranges are configured and data encryption and access control credentials (re)generated \nand/or set on the Storage Device. Access control is configured for LBA range unlocking.  \n⚫ Lock & Unlock Storage Device:  \nUnlocking of one or more LBA ranges by the host and locking of those ranges under host control \nvia either an explicit lock or implicit lock triggered by a reset event. MBR shadowing provides a \nmechanism to boot into a secure pre -boot authentication environm ent to handle device unlocking.  \n⚫ Repurpose & End -of-Life:  \nErasure of data within one or more.  \n⚫ Physical Presence SID (PSID):  \nPSID is defined by TCG OPAL as a 32 -character string and the purpose is to revert SSD back to its \nmanufacturing setting when the drive is still OPAL -activated.
