### Component Summary: VI-JW1-IY from Vicor

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Input Voltage Range: 10V to 36V
  - Output Voltage Options: 3.3V, 5V, 5.2V, 5.5V, 5.8V, 6.5V, 10V, 12V, 13.8V, 15V, 18.5V, 24V, 28V, 36V, 40V, 48V, 52V, 72V, 85V, 95V
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Maximum Output Current: 20A
- **Power Consumption:**
  - Maximum Power Output: 100W
  - No Load Power Dissipation: 1.35W to 2W
- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Storage: -40°C to +105°C
  - Operating: -25°C to +100°C
- **Package Type:**
  - Half Brick, dimensions: 2.28” x 2.4” x 0.5” (57.9 x 61.0 x 12.7 mm)
- **Special Features:**
  - Isolated output
  - Up to 90% efficiency
  - Remote sense and current limit
  - Logic disable
  - Wide range output adjust
  - Zero-current-switching (ZCS) power architecture
  - Low noise FM control
  - RoHS compliant (VE versions)
- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL Level: 1 (JEDEC J-STD-020E)

#### Description:
The **VI-JW1-IY** is a high-density DC-DC converter from Vicor's MiniMod family, designed to provide isolated and regulated power in a compact half-brick package. It is capable of delivering up to 100W of power with high efficiency (up to 90%) and is suitable for various input and output voltage configurations. The converter utilizes Vicor's advanced zero-current-switching technology, which enhances efficiency and reduces electromagnetic interference.

#### Typical Applications:
The VI-JW1-IY is typically used in:
- **Power Management:** Ideal for applications requiring efficient power conversion and regulation.
- **Telecommunications:** Suitable for powering communication equipment where space and efficiency are critical.
- **Industrial Automation:** Used in control systems and automation equipment that require reliable power supplies.
- **Data Processing:** Employed in servers and data centers for efficient power distribution.
- **Embedded Systems:** Commonly found in embedded applications where compact size and high performance are essential.

This component is particularly valuable for designers looking to meet stringent power density and efficiency requirements in modern electronic systems.
