### Component Summary: HMC433ETR

**Manufacturer:** Hittite Microwave Corporation (now part of Analog Devices)

**Product Type:** GaAs HBT MMIC Divide-by-4 Static Divider

**Key Specifications:**
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Supply Voltage (Vcc): +3V ± 0.3V
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Supply Current (Icc): 53 mA (typical), up to 71 mA (max)
- **Power Consumption:**
  - Power consumption is approximately 159 mW (3V * 53 mA)
- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - -40°C to +85°C
- **Package Type:**
  - SOT26 (Surface Mount)
- **Special Features:**
  - Low additive SSB phase noise of -150 dBc/Hz at 100 kHz offset
  - Single-ended inputs and outputs to reduce component count and cost
  - Capable of operating from DC to 8 GHz input frequency
- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL1 (JEDEC J-STD-020E)

**Description:**
The HMC433ETR is a low noise, high-performance divide-by-4 static divider designed using InGaP GaAs HBT technology. It is packaged in an ultra-small SOT26 surface mount package, making it suitable for space-constrained applications. The device is capable of handling input frequencies from DC up to 8 GHz and operates with a single +3V DC supply. Its low phase noise characteristics make it ideal for maintaining system noise performance in various applications.

**Typical Applications:**
- **Prescaler for DC to C Band PLL Applications:** The HMC433ETR is commonly used in phase-locked loop (PLL) circuits where frequency division is required.
- **Wireless Communication:** It is suitable for use in UNII, point-to-point, and VSAT radios, as well as in WLAN standards such as 802.11a and HiperLAN.
- **Fiber Optic Systems:** The device can be utilized in fiber optic communication systems where precise frequency division is necessary.
- **Cellular Infrastructure:** It is applicable in 3G and other cellular communication systems, aiding in signal processing and management.

This component is particularly valuable in applications requiring low noise and high-frequency performance, making it a critical part of modern communication systems.
