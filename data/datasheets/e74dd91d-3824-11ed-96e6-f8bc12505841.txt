### Component Summary: TSV994IPT (STMicroelectronics)

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Supply Voltage (VCC): 2.5 V to 5.5 V
  - Absolute Maximum Supply Voltage: 6 V
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Output Current: 35 mA (max)
  - Supply Current: 820 µA (typical)
- **Power Consumption:**
  - Low power consumption: 820 µA (typical) at 5 V
- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - -40 °C to 125 °C
- **Package Type:**
  - TSSOP14
- **Special Features:**
  - Low input offset voltage: 1.5 mV max (A grade)
  - Rail-to-rail input and output
  - Wide bandwidth: 20 MHz gain-bandwidth product
  - Stable for gain ≥ 4 or ≤ -3
  - Low input bias current: 1 pA (typical)
  - ESD internal protection: ≥ 5 kV
- **Moisture Sensitive Level (JEDEC J-STD-020E):**
  - Level 1 for most packages, Level 3 for SO14

#### Description:
The **TSV994IPT** is a quad operational amplifier designed for low voltage operation with rail-to-rail input and output capabilities. It features a high gain-bandwidth product of 20 MHz, making it suitable for a variety of applications that require fast signal processing. The device is characterized by low power consumption and low input bias current, which enhances its performance in battery-powered and portable applications.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Battery-Powered Applications:** Ideal for devices that require efficient power management.
- **Portable Devices:** Suitable for mobile electronics due to its low power consumption.
- **Signal Conditioning:** Used in circuits that require amplification and filtering of signals.
- **Active Filtering:** Effective in audio and signal processing applications.
- **Medical Instrumentation:** Reliable for sensitive measurements in medical devices.
- **Automotive Applications:** Can be utilized in automotive electronics for various signal processing tasks.

This operational amplifier is particularly advantageous in applications where low power and high performance are critical, making it a versatile choice for engineers designing modern electronic systems.
