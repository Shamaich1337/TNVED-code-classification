### Component Summary: SMAJ28CA from STMicroelectronics

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Stand-off Voltage (V_RM): 28 V
  - Peak Pulse Voltage (IEC 61000-4-2): 30 kV (contact discharge), 30 kV (air discharge)

- **Current Ratings:**
  - Maximum Reverse Leakage Current (I_RM): 0.2 µA at 25 °C, 1 µA at 85 °C
  - Peak Pulse Power Dissipation (P_PP): 400 W (10/1000 µs), 2.3 kW (8/20 µs)

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Low leakage current ensures minimal power loss during operation.

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Junction Temperature (T_j): -55 °C to +150 °C
  - Storage Temperature (T_stg): -65 °C to +150 °C

- **Package Type:**
  - SMA (SMA footprint in accordance with IPC 7531 standard)

- **Special Features:**
  - Unidirectional and bidirectional types available.
  - Resin meets UL94, V0 standards.
  - Complies with J-STD-020 MSL level 1.
  - High surge capability for electrical overstress protection.
  - Planar technology for low leakage and high reliability.

- **Moisture Sensitive Level:**
  - JEDEC J-STD-020E: MSL Level 1

#### Description:
The **SMAJ28CA** is a Transient Voltage Suppressor (TVS) diode designed to protect sensitive electronic equipment from voltage spikes and electrostatic discharges. It is part of the SMAJ series, which is specifically engineered to handle electrical overstress and transient surges, making it suitable for high-end circuits that require low leakage current and high junction temperature stability.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Electrostatic Discharge (ESD) Protection:** Safeguarding sensitive components in consumer electronics, telecommunications, and industrial equipment.
- **Surge Protection:** Protecting against voltage transients in power management systems, automotive electronics, and data lines.
- **Signal Integrity Maintenance:** Ensuring reliable operation of high-speed data lines by preventing voltage spikes that could disrupt signal integrity.

This component is ideal for applications where reliability and performance under transient conditions are critical, making it a valuable choice for engineers designing robust electronic systems.
