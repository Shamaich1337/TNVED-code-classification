### Component Summary: ESD9X5VU-2/TR

**Manufacturer:** Will Semiconductor Ltd.

**Description:**
The ESD9X5VU is a uni-directional transient voltage suppressor (TVS) designed to provide high-level protection for sensitive electronic components against electrostatic discharge (ESD). It is particularly suitable for replacing multilayer varistors (MLVs) in consumer electronics such as mobile phones, notebooks, tablets, set-top boxes (STBs), and LCD TVs. The device features ultra-low capacitance steering diodes and a TVS diode, making it effective for high-speed data lines.

### Key Specifications:

- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Reverse Stand-off Voltage (VRWM): 5.0 V
  - Reverse Breakdown Voltage (VBR): 7.0 - 9.0 V

- **Current Ratings:**
  - Peak Pulse Current (IPP): 4 A (for 8/20μs pulse)
  - Reverse Leakage Current (IR): <1 nA (at VRWM = 5V)

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Peak Pulse Power (Ppk): 60 W (for 8/20μs pulse)

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Junction Temperature (TJ): 125 °C
  - Operating Temperature (TOP): -40 °C to 85 °C
  - Storage Temperature (TSTG): -55 °C to 150 °C

- **Package Type:**
  - FBP-02C

- **Special Features:**
  - ESD protection up to ±20 kV (contact and air discharge) according to IEC61000-4-2
  - Surge protection: 4 A (8/20μs) according to IEC61000-4-5
  - Ultra-low capacitance: CJ = 0.5 pF (typical)
  - Ultra-low leakage current: IR < 1 nA (typical)
  - Low clamping voltage: VCL = 18 V (typical at IPP = 16 A)

- **Moisture Sensitive Level:**
  - Not specified in the provided text, but typically assessed according to JEDEC J-STD-020E.

### Typical Applications:
The ESD9X5VU is commonly used in various high-speed data interfaces and consumer electronics, including:
- USB 2.0 and USB 3.0
- HDMI 1.3 and HDMI 1.4
- SATA and eSATA
- DVI
- IEEE 1394
- PCI Express
- Portable electronics and notebooks

The ESD9X5VU-2/TR is an effective solution for protecting sensitive electronic components from ESD and surge events, making it ideal for modern consumer electronics that require reliable performance in high-speed data transmission environments.
