This erroneous datacould cause data errors to an RS-422 system. To avoidthis, the MAX3030E –MAX3033E have hot-swap capa-\nble inputs. \nWhen a circuit board is plugged into a “hot” backplane,\nthere is an interval during which the processor is goingthrough its power-up sequence. During this time, theprocessor ’s output drivers are high impedance and are\nunable to drive the enable inputs of the MAX3030E –\nMAX3033E (EN, EN, EN_) to defined logic levels.\nLeakage currents from these high-impedance drivers,of as much as 10µA, could cause the enable inputs ofthe MAX3030E –MAX3033E to drift high or low.\nAdditionally, parasitic capacitance of the circuit boardcould cause capacitive coupling of the enable inputs toeither GND or V\nCC. These factors could cause the\nenable inputs of the MAX3030E –MAX3033E to drift to\nlevels that may enable the transmitter outputs. To avoidthis problem, the hot-swap input provides a method ofholding the enable inputs of the MAX3030E –MAX3033E\nin the disabled state as V\nCCramps up. This hot-swap\ninput is able to overcome the leakage currents and par-asitic capacitances that can pull the enable inputs tothe enabled state.\nHot-Swap Input Circuitry\nIn the MAX3030E –MAX3033E, the enable inputs feature\nhot-swap capability. At the input there are two NMOSdevices, M1 and M2 (Figure 10). When V\nCCis ramping\nup from zero, an internal 6µs timer turns on M2 and setsthe SR latch, which also turns on M1. Transistors M2, a2mA current sink, and M1, a 100µA current sink, pull ENto GND through a 5.6k Ωresistor. M2 is designed to pull\nthe EN input to the disabled state against an externalparasitic capacitance of up to 100pF that is trying toenable the EN input. After 6µs, the timer turns M2 off andM1 remains on, holding the EN input low against three-state output leakages that might enable EN. M1 remainson until an external source overcomes the required inputcurrent. At this time the SR latch resets and M1 turns off.\nWhen M1 turns off, EN reverts to a standard, high-impedance CMOS input. ### Component Summary: MAX3030EESE+T

**Key Characteristics and Specifications:**

- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Supply Voltage (VCC): +3V to +3.6V

- **Current Ratings:**
  - Supply Current (ICC): < 100 µA (static)

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Maximum Power Consumption: < 330 µW at VCC = 3.3V

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - -40 °C to +85 °C

- **Package Type:**
  - 16-Pin SO (Narrow)

- **Special Features:**
  - ±15 kV ESD protection on transmitter outputs
  - Hot-swap capability to prevent false transitions during power-up
  - Available in both 2 Mbps and 20 Mbps data rates
  - Slew-rate limiting for the 2 Mbps version to minimize EMI
  - Thermal shutdown feature

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL Level: Not specified in the provided text.

**Description of the Component:**
The MAX3030EESE+T is a quad RS-422 transmitter designed for high-speed digital data transmission over twisted-pair balanced lines. It complies with TIA/EIA-422-B and ITU-T V.11 standards, making it suitable for various communication applications. The device features enhanced ESD protection, allowing it to withstand electrostatic discharges of up to ±15 kV, ensuring reliability in harsh environments. The MAX3030EESE+T is particularly noted for its low power consumption, making it ideal for battery-powered and power-sensitive applications.

**Typical Applications:**
The MAX3030EESE+T is commonly used in:
- Telecom backplanes
- V.11/X.21 interfaces
- Industrial PLCs (Programmable Logic Controllers)
- Motor control systems

This component is particularly useful in applications where robust data transmission is required, and it can effectively drive parallel data due to its low propagation delay and skew characteristics. The hot-swap capability further enhances its usability in systems where components may be added or removed while powered.
