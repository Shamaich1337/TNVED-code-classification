### Component Summary: CD143A-SR3.3 (Bourns)

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Working Peak Voltage (VWM): 3.3 V
  - Breakdown Voltage Minimum @ 1 mA (VBR): 4.5 V
  - Operating Supply Voltage (Vdd - Gnd): 3.8 V
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Peak Pulse Current (IPP) (t_p = 8/20 µs): 12 A
- **Power Consumption:**
  - Peak Pulse Power (t_p = 8/20 µs): 200 W
- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Junction Temperature (TJ): -55 °C to +125 °C
  - Storage Temperature (TSTG): -55 °C to +150 °C
- **Package Type:**
  - SOT-143
- **Special Features:**
  - ESD protection per IEC 61000-4-2 (±30 kV)
  - Protects two lines
  - Low leakage current (5.0 µA max)
  - Low capacitance (4.5 pF typical at 1 MHz)
- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - Not specified in the provided text, but typically SOT-143 packages are MSL 1 or 2.

#### Description:
The CD143A-SR3.3 is a steering/TVS (Transient Voltage Suppressor) diode array designed to provide electrostatic discharge (ESD) protection for sensitive electronic components. It is particularly effective in safeguarding data lines against voltage spikes that can occur during handling or operation. The device is compact, measuring 2.80 mm x 1.20 mm, and is packaged in a SOT-143 format, making it suitable for surface mount applications.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Ethernet Interfaces:** Protects data lines in 10/100/1000 Base-T Ethernet applications.
- **Firewire and USB Ports:** Provides ESD protection for high-speed data transfer interfaces.
- **Portable Electronics:** Used in devices such as smartphones, tablets, and laptops to safeguard against ESD events.
- **Video/Graphic Cards:** Protects sensitive components in graphics processing units (GPUs) and video cards from voltage spikes.

This component is essential in modern electronics where data integrity and device longevity are critical, especially in environments prone to ESD events.
