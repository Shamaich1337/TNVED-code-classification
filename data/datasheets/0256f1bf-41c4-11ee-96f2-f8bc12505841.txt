An adverse effect that is common to many operational amplifiers is a change in the offset voltage \nas a result of  RF signal rectification. An operational amplifier that is more efficient at rejecting this change in \noffset as  a result of EMI has a higher EMIRR and is quantified by a decibel value. Measuring EMIRR can be \nperformed in  many ways, but this document provid es the EMIRR IN+, which specifically describes the EMIRR \nperformance  when the RF signal is applied to the noninverting input pin of the operational amplifier. In general, \nonly the  noninverting input is tested for EMIRR for the following three reasons:  \n• Operational amplifier input pins are known to be the most sensitive to EMI, and typically rectify RF signals  \nbetter than the supply or output pins.  \n• The noninverting and inverting operational amplifier inputs have symmetrical physical layouts and exhibit  \nnearly matching EMIRR performance.  \n• EMIRR is easier to measure on noninverting pins than on other pins because the noninverting input pin can  \nbe isolated on a printed -circuit -board (PCB). This isolation allows the RF signal to be applied directly to the  \nnoninverting input pin with no complex interactions from other components or connecting PCB traces.  \n  \n \n  \n \nRS621S, RS622S, RS62X \n \n  14 / 27 www. run-ic.com \n   \n \nDetailed Description （continued ） \nThe EMIRR IN+ of the RS62X is plotted versus frequency in Figu re 2. ### Component Summary: RS621XF

**Key Characteristics and Specifications:**

- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Supply Voltage (Vs): 2.5V to 5.5V (Single-supply)
  - Absolute Maximum Supply Voltage: 7V

- **Current Ratings:**
  - Quiescent Current: 600 µA to 800 µA (typical)
  - Output Short-Circuit Current: 50 mA
  - Input Current: ±10 mA (maximum)

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Supply Current in Shutdown Mode (for RS621S/RS622S): <1 µA

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - -40°C to +125°C

- **Package Type:**
  - SOT23-5 (2.90mm x 1.60mm)

- **Special Features or Notes:**
  - Rail-to-Rail Input and Output
  - High Gain Bandwidth: 7 MHz
  - Slew Rate: 3.7 V/µs
  - Input Offset Voltage: 0.7 mV (typical)
  - Enable/Shutdown functionality for RS621S/RS622S
  - Phase Reversal Protection
  - Moisture Sensitive Level: MSL 1 (JEDEC J-STD-020E)

**Description:**
The RS621XF is a high-performance, rail-to-rail CMOS operational amplifier designed for low voltage applications. It features a high gain bandwidth of 7 MHz and a slew rate of 3.7 V/µs, making it suitable for a variety of signal processing tasks. The device operates from a single supply voltage ranging from 2.5V to 5.5V and is capable of rail-to-rail input and output, which enhances its dynamic range in low-supply applications.

**Typical Applications:**
The RS621XF is commonly used in:
- Sensor interfaces
- Photodiode amplification
- Active filters
- Test equipment
- Driving analog-to-digital converters (A/D converters)

This operational amplifier is particularly well-suited for portable applications due to its low power consumption and wide operating temperature range, making it ideal for battery-powered devices and other applications where efficiency is critical.
