### Component Summary: TQL9044

**Manufacturer:** Qorvo, Inc. (formerly TriQuint)

**Product Code:** TQL9044

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Supply Voltage (VDD): +3.3 V to +5.25 V (recommended operating at +5 V)
  - Absolute Maximum Drain Voltage: +7 V

- **Current Ratings:**
  - LNA Mode (Bypass OFF): 70 mA
  - Bypass Mode (LNA OFF): 3 mA

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Typical power consumption in LNA mode: 350 mW (at +5 V, 70 mA)

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Storage Temperature: −65 to +150 °C
  - Operating Temperature: −40 to +85 °C

- **Package Type:**
  - 10-pin 3x3 mm DFN (Dual Flat No-lead) plastic package

- **Special Features:**
  - Integrated bypass mode functionality
  - Ultra-low noise figure of 0.6 dB at 1.9 GHz
  - High linearity with +34.5 dBm Output IP3 in LNA mode and +36 dBm Input IP3 in bypass mode
  - Internally matched, requiring only four external components for operation
  - Active bias for maintaining performance over temperature

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL Rating: Level 1 (260°C convection reflow)

#### Description:
The TQL9044 is a high-linearity, ultra-low noise gain block amplifier designed for use in wireless infrastructure applications. It operates within a frequency range of 1500 MHz to 2700 MHz and features an integrated bypass mode that allows the amplifier to be turned off or switched to a bypass state. This component is particularly noted for its low noise figure and high gain, making it suitable for enhancing signal quality in various communication systems.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Base Station Receivers:** Used to amplify weak signals received from antennas.
- **Tower Mount Amplifiers:** Enhances signal strength at the tower level for better transmission.
- **Repeaters:** Boosts signals in areas with poor reception.
- **FDD-LTE, TDD-LTE, WCDMA:** Supports various wireless communication standards.
- **General Purpose Wireless:** Versatile use in different wireless applications requiring signal amplification.

The TQL9044 is ideal for applications that demand high performance in terms of gain and noise figure, making it a critical component in modern wireless communication systems.
