### Component Summary: HMC8191LC4TR

**Key Specifications:**
- **Voltage Ratings:** Not applicable (passive mixer, no DC power required)
- **Current Ratings:** Maximum IF source/sink current: 3 mA
- **Power Consumption:** Continuous power dissipation: 657 mW (at 85°C)
- **Operating Temperature Range:** -40°C to +85°C
- **Package Type:** 24-terminal ceramic Leadless Chip Carrier (LCC), dimensions 4 mm x 4 mm
- **Special Features:**
  - Passive wideband I/Q mixer
  - RF and LO frequency range: 6 GHz to 26.5 GHz
  - Wide IF bandwidth: DC to 5 GHz
  - Excellent image rejection: 25 dBc (typical)
  - Low conversion loss: 9 dB (typical)
  - Low noise figure: 9 dB (typical)
  - High input IP3: 24 dBm (typical)
  - High isolation: LO to RF and LO to IF isolation of 40 dB (typical)
- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):** MSL3 (according to JEDEC J-STD-020E)

**Description:**
The HMC8191 is a passive, wideband I/Q monolithic microwave integrated circuit (MMIC) mixer designed for both downconversion and upconversion applications. It operates within a frequency range of 6 GHz to 26.5 GHz and supports an intermediate frequency (IF) bandwidth from DC to 5 GHz. The device is fabricated using a GaAs MESFET process, which contributes to its high performance and compact design.

**Typical Applications:**
The HMC8191 is suitable for a variety of applications, including:
- **Test and Measurement Instrumentation:** Used in equipment that requires precise signal processing.
- **Military, Aerospace, and Defense Applications:** Ideal for systems that demand high reliability and performance in challenging environments.
- **Microwave Point-to-Point Base Stations:** Utilized in communication systems that require efficient signal transmission over long distances.
- **Signal Processing:** Can be employed in various RF and microwave signal processing tasks due to its excellent performance characteristics.

This component is particularly advantageous in designs that require a wide frequency range and high dynamic range, allowing for simpler designs with fewer components and a smaller PCB footprint.
