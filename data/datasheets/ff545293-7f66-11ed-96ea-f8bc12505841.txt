### Component Summary: HMC368LP4ETR

**Description:**
The HMC368LP4ETR is a miniature GaAs PHEMT (Pseudomorphic High Electron Mobility Transistor) MMIC (Monolithic Microwave Integrated Circuit) designed as an amp-doubler-amp. It operates within the frequency range of 9 to 16 GHz and is packaged in a 4 x 4 mm leadless surface mount format. This component is primarily used for frequency multiplication in microwave applications.

**Key Specifications:**
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Supply Voltage (Vd1, Vd2): +5V ± 0.5V
  - Gate Bias Voltage (Vg1, Vg2): -2V to 0V
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Supply Current (Idd): 75 mA (typical)
- **Power Consumption:**
  - Maximum Power Dissipation: 812 mW (at T = 85 °C)
- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - Storage Temperature: -65 °C to +150 °C
  - Operating Temperature: -40 °C to +85 °C
- **Package Type:**
  - 16 mm² Leadless QFN SMT Package
- **Special Features:**
  - Output Power: +15 dBm (typical)
  - Low additive SSB Phase Noise: -140 dBc/Hz at 100 kHz offset
  - Fo and 3Fo Isolation: 18 dB (typical)
- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL1 (JEDEC J-STD-020E)

**Typical Applications:**
The HMC368LP4ETR is suitable for various applications, including:
- Microwave Radios
- VSAT (Very Small Aperture Terminal) systems
- Fiber Optic Infrastructure
- Military Communications and Radar systems

This component is particularly advantageous in LO (Local Oscillator) multiplier chains, allowing for a reduced parts count compared to traditional methods, thus enhancing system efficiency and performance.
