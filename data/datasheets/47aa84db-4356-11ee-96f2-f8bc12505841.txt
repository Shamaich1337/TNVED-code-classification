The jitter performance of this type of device is \ncharacterized by its additive jitter. Additive jitter is the jitter the device would add to a hypothetical jitter-free clock as  \nit passes through the device. The additive jitter of the ZL40216  is random and as such it is not correlated to the jitter \nof the input clock signal.\nThe square of the resultant random RMS jitter at the output  of the ZL40216 is equal to the sum of the squares of the \nvarious random RMS jitter sources including: input clock jitte r; additive jitter of the buff er; and additive jitter due to \npower supply noise. ### Component Summary: ZL40216LDG1

**Manufacturer:** Microchip Technology (formerly Microsemi Corporation)  
**Product Code:** ZL40216LDG1  
**Description:** The ZL40216 is a precision LVDS (Low Voltage Differential Signaling) fanout buffer designed to distribute clock signals with minimal jitter. It features six identical LVDS outputs and can accept various input signal types, making it versatile for high-frequency applications.

#### Key Specifications:
- **Voltage Ratings:**
  - Supply Voltage: 2.5 V ± 5% or 3.3 V ± 5%
  
- **Current Ratings:**
  - Current Consumption: 93 mA (when all outputs are active)

- **Power Consumption:**
  - Power supply options of 2.5 V or 3.3 V with a typical current consumption of 93 mA.

- **Operating Temperature Range:**
  - -40°C to +85°C

- **Package Type:**
  - 32 Pin QFN (Quad Flat No-lead), dimensions 5 x 5 mm

- **Special Features:**
  - Accepts differential or single-ended inputs (LVPECL, LVDS, CML, HCSL, LVCMOS)
  - Ultra-low additive jitter of 104 fs RMS
  - On-chip Low Drop Out (LDO) regulator for improved power supply noise rejection
  - Designed for high-frequency operation up to 750 MHz

- **Moisture Sensitive Level (MSL):**
  - MSL Level: Not specified in the provided text, but typically assessed according to JEDEC J-STD-020E standards.

#### Description:
The ZL40216 is a high-performance clock distribution device that serves as a fanout buffer for LVDS signals. It is engineered to minimize jitter while distributing clock signals across multiple outputs, making it ideal for applications requiring precise timing and synchronization.

#### Typical Applications:
- **Clock Distribution:** Used in general-purpose clock distribution systems to ensure low-jitter clock signals are available to various components.
- **Low Jitter Clock Trees:** Suitable for creating clock trees that require minimal jitter for high-speed data transmission.
- **Logic Translation:** Can be utilized for translating logic levels between different signaling standards.
- **Clock and Data Signal Restoration:** Effective in restoring clock and data signals in communication systems.
- **Wired Communications:** Applicable in Optical Transport Networks (OTN), SONET/SDH, Gigabit Ethernet (GE), 10 Gigabit Ethernet (10 GE), Fibre Channel (FC), and 10G FC.
- **Wireless Communications:** Used in systems requiring precise timing for signal processing.
- **Microprocessor Clock Distribution:** Ideal for distributing clock signals to high-performance microprocessors.
